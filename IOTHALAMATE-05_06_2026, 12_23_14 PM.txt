Administration
IV › 600 mg/mL, 30 mL vial (usually IV) (Rx)
For intravenous administration. Do NOT administer by rapid or bolus-like injections. Do NOT administer via a
central venous line. Inspect visually for particulate matter and discoloration prior to administration. Measure
patient dose by suitable radioactivity calibration system immediately prior to administration. For continuous IV
infusion, establish an adequate diuresis by an oral water load of 1,500 mL 2 hours prior to the beginning of the
clearance study. Insert a no. 14 or 16 French Foley catheter aseptically into the bladder. Start IV infusion lines of
lactated Ringer’s solution in each arm with two-way stopcocks (1 as injection site, 1 as blood withdrawal site).
Divide dose equally into two (a priming dose to be injected as is and a sustaining dose to be diluted in 30–60 mL of
isotonic sodium chloride). Inject priming dose into 1 arm, followed immediately by the sustaining solution through
the same site by IV infusion at 0.5 mL/minute. Discontinue the Lactated Ringer’s solution during infusion. Wait 40–
45 minutes to achieve constant plasma concentration. Begin collecting urine samples every 15 minutes, 3
consecutive collection samples are sufficient for most clinical studies. From the opposite arm, collect 5 mL blood
samples drawn 6 minutes prior to the midpoint of each collection period, placed in heparinized tubes, mixed, and
centrifuged. For single IV injection, administer Lugol’s solution, 3 drops orally, 3 times daily for 1–2 days prior to the
test. Begin oral water load 1 hour before the test. Start with 20 mL/kg and force any clear liquid until the test is
complete. Empty the bladder and label sample as Urine control. Inject 10–30 mL and wait 30 to 60 minutes. Collect
the entire urine and label Urine discard. Draw 4–5 mL of blood into a heparinized syringe and label Plasma #1. After
another 30–60 minutes, collect the entire urine and label Urine #1. Immediately draw another blood specimen and
label Plasma #2. After another 30–60 minutes, collect the urine and label Urine #2. Immediately draw the last blood
specimen and label Plasma #3.

Mechanism of Action
A sterile, nonpyrogenic aqueous injection containing approximately 1 mg sodium iothalamate per mL. The radioactive
concentration of the material is approximately 250–300 microCi/mL.
