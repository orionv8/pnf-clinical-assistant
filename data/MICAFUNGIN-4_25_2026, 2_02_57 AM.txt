Contraindications

Hypersensitivity to micafungin, other echincandins, or any component of the formulation.

Precautions

WARNING: Cases of hypersensitivity reactions including anaphylactoid reactions and anaphylaxis with shock have
been reported in patients who receive micafungin.

Use with caution to avoid anaphylactic reactions, hemolytic anemia, hepatic failure and renal impairment.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Fever, headache, hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia, diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, mucosal inflammation,
constipation.

Less Common: Hypotension, tachycardia, hypertension, peripheral edema, edema, bradycardia, atrial fibrillation,
insomnia, anxiety.

Drug Interactions

No known significant interactions

Mechanism of Action

Azoles are synthetic compounds that can be classified as either imidazoles or triazoles according to the number of
nitrogen atoms. Azoles exhibit activity by reducing the ergosterol synthesis through inhibition of fungal cytochrome
P450 enzymes.
