Dosage

ORAL › 200 mg capsule (Rx)

Endometriosis, by mouth, ADULT (female), mild disease, 200–400 mg daily; moderate-to-severe, 800 mg daily in 2
divided doses; continue therapy uninterrupted for 3–6 months up to 9 months. Fibrocystic breast disease, by
mouth, ADULT (female), 100–400 mg daily in 2 divided doses; pain and tenderness may be eliminated in 2–3 months;
elimination of nodularity may require therapy for 4–6 months. Hereditary angioedema, by mouth, ADULT, 200 mg 2
to 3 times daily; after favorable response, decrease the dosage to ≤100 mg at 1–3 months interval or longer if
needed; if an attack occurs, increase dose by up to 200 mg daily.

Administration

Indications

Treatment of endometriosis, fibrocystic breast diseases, and hereditary angioedema

Contraindications

Undiagnosed genital bleeding; pregnancy; breastfeeding; porphyria; markedly impaired hepatic, renal, or cardiac
function; androgen-dependent tumor; thrombosis or thromboembolic disease (active or history)

Precautions

WARNING: Thromboembolism, thrombotic, and thrombophlebitic events, including sagittal sinus thrombosis and
life-threatening or fatal strokes, have been reported.

Peliosis hepatis and benign hepatic adenoma have been reported with long-term use of the drug.

Has been associated with pseudomotor cerebri, a case of benign intracranial hypertension.

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of the following drugs:
C1 Inhibitors (thrombogenic effect)

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs:
Vitamin D Analogues [except Calcipotriene] (hypercalcemic effect)

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Danazol:
Corticosteroids (Dofetilide)

Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs:
Antidiabetic Agents

Avoid concomitant use with:
Decreases metabolism of the following drugs:
Carbamazepine

Enhances therapeutic effect of the following drugs:
Vitamin K Antagonists, e.g., Warfarin (anticoagulant effect)

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs:
Cyclosporine (hepatotoxic effect)

Increases serum concentration of the following drugs:
Cyclosporine, HMG-CoA Reductase Inhibitors [except Fluvastatin, Pravastatin, Rosuvastatin],

Increases serum concentration of the following drugs:
Pimozide, Simvastatin

Mechanism of Action

A synthetic derivative of ethisterone with weak androgenic and anabolic properties. It suppresses pituitary output of
FSH and LH resulting to the regression and atrophy of normal and ectopic endometrial tissues
