DEFEROXAMINE (DESFERRIOXAMINE)

Drug Classification

ATC CODE

V03AC01

ANATOMICAL

Various

THERAPEUTIC

All Other Therapeutic Products

PHARMACOLOGICAL

Iron Chelating Agents

Technical Specifications PNF Primary Care Reserved AMs

IM › IV INFUSION › SC › 500 mg powder (as mesylate), vial (Rx)

Dosage

IM / IV INFUSION / SC › 500 mg powder (as mesylate), vial (Rx)

Acute iron toxicity, by IM injection, ADULT, initially 1,000 mg, may be followed by 500 mg every 4 hours for 2 doses,
subsequent doses of 500 mg have been administered every 4–12 hours based on clinical response (maximum
recommended dose, 6,000 mg daily); ADOLESCENT and CHILD, 90 mg/kg per dose every 8 hours (maximum, 6,000
mg in 24 hours); by IV infusion, ADULT, initially 1,000 mg at 15 mg/kg per hour, subsequent doses may be given over
4–12 hours at a rate not to exceed 125 mg/hour; ADOLESCENT and CHILD, 15 mg/kg per hour (maximum, 6,000 mg in
24 hours). Chronic iron overload, by IM injection, ADULT, 500 to 1,000 mg daily (maximum, 1,000 mg daily); by IV
infusion, ADULT, 40–50 mg/kg daily (maximum, 60 mg/kg daily) over 8–12 hours for 5–7 days per week; ADOLESCENT
and CHILD ≥3 years, 20–40 mg/kg daily over 8–12 hours for 5–7 days per week, not to exceed 40 mg/kg daily until
growth has ceased; by SQ injection, ADULT, 1,000–2,000 mg daily or 20–40 mg/kg daily over 8–24 hours;
ADOLESCENT and CHILD ≥3 years, 20–40 mg/kg daily over 8–12 hours (maximum daily dose, 1,000–2,000 mg).

Administration

IM / IV INFUSION / SC › 500 mg powder (as mesylate), vial (Rx)

Administer by IV injection when severe toxicity is evidenced by cardiovascular collapse or systemic symptoms
(coma, shock, metabolic acidosis, or GI bleeding) or potentially severe intoxications (peak serum iron level >500
micrograms/dL). IM injection may be used for patients with acute iron toxicity that do not exhibit severe symptoms,
and in the treatment of chronic iron toxicity. NOTE: Limit vitamin C supplements in adults at 200 mg daily in divided
doses, in children ≥10 years at 100 mg daily, and in children <10 years at 50 mg daily.

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric:
Initiate at the lower end of the dosing range.
Renal Impairment:
Deferoxamine is readily dialyzable.
In CrCl 10–50 mL/minute and CRRT, administer 25 to 50%
of normal dose.

In CrCl <10 mL/minute, hemodialysis, and peritoneal
dialysis, avoid use.

Indications

Adjunct in the treatment of acute iron
intoxication; treatment of chronic iron overload
secondary to multiple transfusions

Contraindications

Severe renal disease; anuria

Precautions

Acute respiratory distress syndrome, ARDS;
Auditory effects (eg. tinnitus, high frequency hearing loss);
Ocular effects (eg. blurred vision, cataracts, corneal
opacities, decreased visual acuity, impaired peripheral,
color, and night vision, optic neuritis, retinal pigment
abnormalities, scotoma, visual loss or defect);
Growth retardation;
Infection (increased susceptibility to infection with Yersinia
enterocolitica and Yersinia pseudotuberculosis);
Mucormycosis;
Renal effects (e.g., increases in serum creatinine, acute
renal failure and renal tubular disorders; monitor for
changes in renal function);
Urine discoloration;
Elderly (eg. hearing loss and ocular disorders);
Pregnancy;
Lactation.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common and Less Common:
Flushing, hypotension, shock,
tachycardia, brain disease, dizziness, headache,
neuropathy, paresthesia, seizure, skin rash, urticaria,
growth suppression (children), hyperparathyroidism
(aggravated), hypocalcemia, abdominal distress,
abdominal pain, diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, dysuria,
urine discoloration (reddish), dysplasia (metaphyseal;
children <3 years; dose related), leukopenia,
thrombocytopenia, anaphylaxis, hepatic insufficiency,
muscle spasm, angioedema, hypersensitivity, infection
(Yersinia, mucormycosis), injection site reaction,
arthralgia, myalgia, blurred vision, cataract,
chromatopsia, corneal opacity, decreased peripheral
vision, decreased visual acuity, nocturnal amblyopia,
optic neuritis, retinal pigment changes, scotoma, vision
loss, visual field defect, hearing loss, tinnitus, acute renal
failure, renal tubular disease, acute respiratory distress
(dyspnea, cyanosis, and/or interstitial infiltrates),
asthma, fever

Drug Interactions

Avoid concomitant use with:
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects Deferoxamine:
Ascorbic Acid (left ventricular dysfunction), Multivitamins
/ Fluoride with ADE, Multivitamins / Minerals with ADEK,
Folate, Iron (left ventricular dysfunction), Multivitamins /
Minerals with AE, no Iron

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Prochlorperazine
(prolonged loss of consciousness)

TEST INTERATIONS. TIBC may be falsely elevated with high
serum iron concentrations or deferoxamine therapy.

Mechanism of Action

A chelating agent that complexes with ferric trivalent ions to
form ferrioxamine, which is removed by the kidneys. This
slows accumulation of hepatic iron and retards or
eliminated progression of hepatic fibrosis.
