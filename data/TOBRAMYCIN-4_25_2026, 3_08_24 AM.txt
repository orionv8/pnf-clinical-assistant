Dosage

OPHTHALM › [WATCH] 0.3% eye drops solution, 5 mL bottle (Rx)

Ocular infection, mild-to-moderate, into the eye, ADULT and CHILD ≥1 year, as eye drops solution, instill 1 drop into
the conjunctival sac twice daily (morning and evening); as eye ointment, apply ½ inch of ribbon into the affected
eye(s) 2–3 times daily. Ocular infection, mild-to-moderate, into the eye, ADULT and CHILD ≥1 year, as eye drops
solution, instill 1 drop into the conjunctival sac twice daily (morning and evening); as eye ointment, apply ½ inch of
ribbon into the affected eye(s) 2–3 times daily.

Administration

OPHTHALM › [WATCH] 0.3% eye drops solution, 5 mL bottle (Rx)

For ophthalmic use only. Do NOT inject into the eye. Do NOT use contact lenses during treatment. Avoid touching
tip of applicator to eye or other surfaces.

Dose Adjustment

Dose adjustment is unlikely due to low systemic absorption.

Indications

Treatment of external infections of the eye

Contraindications

Known hypersensitivity to tobramycin, other aminoglycosides, or any component of the formulation

Precautions

Hypersensitivity reactions (e.g., anaphylaxis);
Superinfection (e.g., fungal or bacterial superinfection with prolonged use);
Tendon inflammation or rupture;
Elderly (evaluate the patient’s or caregiver’s ability to safely and correctly administer the medication);
Children; Pregnancy and lactation.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Hypersensitivity reactions, lid itching, lid swelling, conjunctival erythema

Drug Interactions

No information found

Mechanism of Action

A topical ophthalmic antibiotic which binds to bacterial 30s and 50s ribosomal subunits and interferes with bacterial
protein synthesis.
