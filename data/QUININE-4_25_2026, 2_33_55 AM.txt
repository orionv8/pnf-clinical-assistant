Dosage

ORAL › 325 mg tablet (as sulfate) (300 mg base) (Rx)

Uncomplicated malaria due to P. falciparum or P. malariae (in combination with clindamycin, 10 mg/kg twice daily for
7 days; and primaquine tablet, 15 mg (0.25 mg base per kg) on day 1 and 3 tablets for adults >60 kg), by mouth, ADULT
(pregnant and lactating women), INFANT <6 months, 10 mg/kg every 8 hours for 7 days. 2nd line drug for severe
malaria, by IV drip, ADULT, 20 mg/kg in 500 mL D5W or 0.9 NaCl for 4 hours ass loading dose (maximum total dose,
2,000 mg), then 10 mg/kg in 0.9 NaCl or D5W for 4 hours every 8 hours as maintenance dose; shift to oral
artemether-lumefantrine plus clindamycin once patient can tolerate; CHILD 8–16 years (in combination with
clindamycin, 10 mg/kg twice daily for 7 days), 15 mg/kg for 4 hours in 10 mL/kg D5W or 0.9 NaCl as loading dose
(maximum infusion rate, 5 mg/kg per hour), then 10 mg/kg for 4 hours every 8 hours as maintenance dose; CHILD ≤7
years, 10 mg/kg for 4 hours as loading dose, then 10 mg/kg every 12 hours as maintenance dose. See National
Antibiotic Guidelines for other dosing information.

Administration

ORAL › 325 mg tablet (as sulfate) (300 mg base) (Rx)

Take with food to decrease incidence of gastric upset. Swallow dose whole to avoid bitter taste. Avoid use of
aluminum- or magnesium-containing antacids because of drug absorption problems

Dose Adjustment

Renal Impairment:
For patients with severe chronic impairment but not on dialysis, initiate dose at 648 mg, followed by 324 mg every 12
hours.

Hepatic Impairment:
For patients with mild-to-moderate impairment, no dosage adjustment needed. Monitor closely. For patients with
severe impairment, avoid use or reduce maintenance dose to 5–7 mg/kg of quinine salt every 8 hours.

Indications

1st line treatment of uncomplicated chloroquine-resistant P. falciparum malaria (in conjunction with other
antimalarial agents) in pregnant and lactating women and infants less than 6 months old; 2nd line treatment of
severe malaria.

Contraindications

Prolonged QT interval; myasthenia gravis; optic neuritis; G6PD deficiency; hypersensitivity to quinine, mefloquine,
quinidine, or any component of the formulation;

Precautions

Hypersensitivity reactions (discontinue following any signs of sensitivity); Hypoglycemia; Thrombocytopenia
(re exposure may result in increased severity of thrombocytopenia and faster onset); Altered cardiac conduction
(use with caution); Hepatic impairment (use with caution); renal impairment (use with caution; dose adjustment
recommended in severe chronic impairment)
Elderly (use with caution).
Pregnancy (crosses the human placenta in amounts not therapeutic to the infant; teratogenic effects, optic nerve
hypoplasia, and deafness have been reported in the infant following maternal use of very high doses); lactation

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with: Enhances therapeutic effect of the following drugs: Antihypertensive Herbs, Vitamin K
Antagonists [e.g., Warfarin (anticoagulant effect)]

Increases risk of hypoglycemic adverse effects of Quinine: Androgens [except Danazol], Antidiabetic Agents, Herbs
with Hypoglycemic Properties, MAO Inhibitors, Other hypoglycemia-associated Agents, Pegvisomant, Salicylates,
Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors

Increases risk of the following drugs: Herbs with Hypotensive Properties (excessive blood pressure lowering),
Significant methemoglobinemia: Nitric Oxide, Prilocaine, Sodium Nitrite

Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs: Codeine, Tramadol

Avoid concomitant use with: Decreases serum concentration of Quinine: Antacids [except Calcium Carbonate,
Sodium Bicarbonate], Carbamazepine, Dabrafenib, Fosphenytoin, Lopinavir, Mitotane, Phenobarbital, Phenytoin,
Rifampin, Ritonavir,

Decreases serum concentration of Tamoxifen [active metabolite/s]

Enhances therapeutic effect of Neuromuscular-blocking Agents

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Quinine: Artemether, Dapsone (hemolytic reactions), Ivabradine (QTc-
prolonging effect), Mifepristone (QTc-prolonging effect), QTc-prolonging Agents

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Dapsone (hemolytic reactions), Halofantrine,
Lumefantrine, Mefloquine (QTc-prolongation; convulsions)

Increases serum concentration of Quinine: Antihepatitis C viral Combination Products, Cimetidine, Conivaptan,
CYP3A4 Inhibitors, Fusidic Acid, Idelalisib, Macrolide Antibiotics e.g. Erythromycin [except Fidaxomicin], Mefloquine
[delay Mefloquine administration at least 12 hours after the last dose of Quinine], Stiripentol

Increases serum concentration of the following drugs: Afatinib [if concomitant use cannot be avoided, reduce
Afatinib dose by 10 mg if not tolerated], Amodiaquine, Bosutinib, Carbamazepine, Colchicine (enhances distribution
into certain tissues, e.g., brain), Dabigatran Etexilate [active metabolite/s], Doxorubicin (Conventional), Edoxaban,
Everolimus, Halofantrine, HMG-CoA reductase inhibitors [except Fluvastatin, Pitavastatin, Pravastatin, Red Yeast
Rice, Rosuvastatin], Metoprolol, Pazopanib, Phenobarbital, Ritonavir, Silodosin, Thioridazine, Topotecan, Vincristine
(Liposomal)

Mechanism of Action

A methanolquinoline that intercalates into the DNA structure of susceptible Plasmodium spp., disrupting the
parasite’s replication and transcription.
