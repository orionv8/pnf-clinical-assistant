Dosage

ORAL › 300 mg tablet (OTC)

Cough, by mouth, ADULT, as 600 mg tablet, 1 tablet 3–4 times daily; as 300 mg/5 mL, 2 tsp 3-4 times per day; CHILD
7–12 years, 1 tablet 3–4 times daily; as 300 mg/5 mL syrup, 10 mL (2 tsp) 3–4 times daily,; CHILD, 15 mg/kg per dose
(0.25 mL/kg per dose for 300 mg/5 mL) administered 3 times daily; CHILD >40 kg body weight, 2 tsp 3 times per day
of 300 mg/5 mL; CHILD 6– 12 years (20–40 kg), 1½ to 2 tsp of 300 mg/5 mL 3 times per day; CHILD 4–6 years (15.5–20
kg), 1 tsp of 300 mg/5 mL 3 times daily; CHILD 2–4 years (10–15.5 kg), ½ to 1 tsp of 300mg/5mL syrup 3 times per day.

Administration

ORAL › 300 mg tablet (OTC)

For syrup preparation, shake well before use. May be taken with or without food.

Dose Adjustment

All clinical trials were performed in patients with normal renal and liver function tests; no reports of adverse events
in patients with renal and liver dysfunction.

Indications

For the relief of mild to moderate cough due to
common colds and flu

Contraindications

Patients with severe allergy to Vitex negundo or any of its components

Precautions

Patients with known allergy to lagundi plants.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Mild adverse effects have been observed, such as itchiness, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.

Mechanism of Action

A natural medicinal preparation from the leaves of Vitex negundo Linn. (Fam. Verbenaceae), which is used for the
relief of cough.

Pregnancy & Remarks

PREGNANCY CATEGORY

In vitro and animal studies have shown Lagundi to be non-mutagenic and non clastogenic.
