Dosage

ORAL › 50 mg tablet (as hydrochloride) (Rx)

Muscle spasms, by mouth, ADULT, 50 mg every 8 hours.

Administration

ORAL › 50 mg tablet (as hydrochloride) (Rx)

Should be taken with food. Take after meals.

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric:
Administer half the dose.

Indications

Used in the improvement of myotonic symptoms in cervical syndrome periarthritis of the shoulder lumbago

Contraindications

Children; pregnancy; kidney dysfunction; neonates

Precautions

Hepatic Impairment;

Children (use is not recommended).

SKILLED TASKS. May impair ability to perform certain tasks, e.g., operating machinery or driving.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common and Less Common: Weakness, dizziness, insomnia, drowsiness, numbness or trembling in the extremities,
hepatic and renal dysfunction, hematological changes, skin rashes, itching, GI disturbances, urinary disorders

Rare: Shock

Drug Interactions

Avoid concomitant use with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Eperisone: Tolperisone HCl

Mechanism of Action

A centrally acting skeletal muscle relaxant used to improve myotonic symptoms.

Pregnancy & Remarks

PREGNANCY CATEGORY

Varied classification, A / B / C
