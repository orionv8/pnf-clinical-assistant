Dosage

ORAL › USP Grade (Liquid) (OTC)

Constipation: by rectum, ADULT, 2.250 g suppository, as needed; CHILD, 1.375 g suppository, as needed.

Administration

ORAL › USP Grade (Liquid) (OTC)

For rectal administration, moisten suppositories with water before insertion. Insert suppository high in the
rectum and retain for approximately 15 minutes. Suppository does not need to melt to produce response.

Dose Adjustment

N/A

Management of constipation

Contraindications

No information found

Precautions

May cause rectal discomfort or a burning sensation;
Hypovolemia, cardiac failure, or renal disease (e.g., circulatory overload, pulmonary edema, and heart failure)
Dehydration;
Diabetes mellitus

Adverse Drug Reactions

Local irritation (e.g., rectal discomfort, burning sensation), cramping pain, tenesmus.

Drug Interactions

No information found

Mechanism of Action

An osmotic dehydrating agent that possesses hygroscopic and lubricating properties. It increases osmotic pressure,
drawing water into the colon and stimulating evacuation.
