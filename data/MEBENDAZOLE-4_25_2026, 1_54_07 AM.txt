Dosage

ORAL › 100 mg tablet / capsule (Rx)

Ascaris lumbricoides (roundworm), Strongyloides stercoralis (roundworm), mixed infection, by mouth, ADULT,
ADOLESCENT, and CHILD ≥2 years, 500 mg as single dose of 100 mg twice daily for 3 days, repeat in 3 weeks if not
cured with initial treatment.

Administration

ORAL › 100 mg tablet / capsule (Rx)

May be administered with or without food. Tablets may be chewed, swallowed whole, or crushed and mixed with
food.

Indications

Management of common roundworm infections

Precautions

Bone marrow suppression; Hepatic impairment (undergoes extensive hepatic metabolism); Children (experience
with use in children <2 years of age is limited) Pregnancy (use with caution; consider postponing therapy
until the third trimester when possible).

Adverse Drug Reactions

Dizziness, drowsiness, headache, seizure, alopecia, angioedema, exanthema, itching, rash, Stevens-Johnson
syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis, urticaria, abdominal pain, diarrhea, vomiting, agranulocytosis, eosinophilia,
decreased hemoglobin, leukopenia, neutropenia, increased alkaline phosphatase, hepatitis, cylindruria,
glomerulonephritis, hematuria, hypersensitivity reactions (anaphylactic, anaphylactoid)

Drug Interactions

Avoid concomitant use with: Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects Mebendazole: Metronidazole (Stevens-
Johnson syndrome or toxic epidermal necrolysis)

Mechanism of Action

A benzimidazole derivative that inhibits the formation of helminth microtubules. It selectively and irreversibly blocks
glucose uptake and other nutrients in susceptible adult intestine-dwelling helminths.
