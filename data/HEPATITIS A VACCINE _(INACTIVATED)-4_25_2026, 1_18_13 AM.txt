Dosage

Administration

IM › Viral antigen not less than 720 EU in 0.5 mL Monodose vial (IM) (Junior) (Rx)

For IM administration into the deltoid muscle for older children and adults or into the anterolateral aspect of the
thigh in infants and young children. Do NOT administer to the gluteal region. Do NOT administer by IV, ID, or SC.
Shake well prior to use. Discard if suspension is discolored, does not appear homogenous after shaking, or if there
are cracks in the vial or syringe. Do NOT dilute.

Dose Adjustment

Hepatic Impairment: May require dose adjustment. Patients with chronic liver disease have lower antibody response.

Indications

For active immunization of persons 12 months and older against diseases caused by hepatitis A virus (HAV)

Contraindications

Immediate and/or severe allergic or hypersensitivity reaction to hepatitis A containing vaccines or any component of
the formulation, including neomycin

Precautions

Anaphylactoid or hypersensitivity reactions (immediate treatment, including epinephrine 1:1000 should be available
during vaccine use); Syncope (has been reported with use); Acute illness; Bleeding disorders, including
thrombocytopenia; Hepatic impairment; Immunodeficiency; Pregnancy (animal reproduction studies have not been
conducted); Lactation (not known if excreted into breastmilk).

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
Drowsiness, headache, irritability, chills, fatigue, insomnia, malaise, skin rash, menstrual disease, decreased
appetite, abdominal pain, anorexia, constipation, diarrhea, gastroenteritis, nausea, vomiting, injection site reactions
(erythema, soreness, warmth, pain, swelling, tenderness, bruising, induration), weakness, arm pain, back pain,
myalgia, stiffness, conjunctivitis, otitis media, asthma, cough, nasal congestion, nasopharyngitis, pharyngitis,
rhinitis, rhinorrhea, upper respiratory tract infection, fever, excessive crying

Less Common:
Anaphylaxis, angioedema, arthralgia, ataxia (cerebellar), bronchoconstriction, croup, bronchiolitis, dehydration,
dermatitis, dizziness, dysgeusia, dyspnea, encephalitis, erythema multiforme, eye irritation, flulike symptoms,
hepatitis, hyperhidrosis, otitis, Guillain-Barré syndrome, hypersensitivity reaction, hypertonia, hypoesthesia,
increased creatine kinase, increased serum transaminases (transient), injection site reactions (hematoma, nodule,
rash), insomnia, jaundice, lymphadenopathy, multiple sclerosis, myelitis, neuropathy, paresthesia, photophobia,
pneumonia, pruritus, respiratory congestion, seizure, serum sickness-like reaction, syncope, thrombocytopenia,
urticaria, vasculitis, vertigo, viral exanthem, wheezing

Drug Interactions

Avoid concomitant use with: Reduces therapeutic effect of HAV vaccine: Belimumab Fingolimod [administer at least
2 weeks apart; if vaccinated during fingolimod therapy, revaccinate 2–3 months after fingolimod discontinuation]
Immunosuppressants [Cytarabine, Liposomal] [administer at least 2 weeks apart; if vaccinated during
immunosuppressant therapy, revaccinate at least 3 months after immunosuppressant discontinuation]

Mechanism of Action

An inactivated viral vaccine that induces active immunity against hepatitis A virus infection.
