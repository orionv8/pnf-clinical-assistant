ANTITHYMOCITE IMMUNOGLOBULIN (ALG) (RABBIT)

Drug Classification

ATC CODE

L04AA04

ANATOMICAL

Antineoplastic and Immunomodulating Agents

THERAPEUTIC

Immunosuppressants

PHARMACOLOGICAL

Selective Immunosuppressants

Technical Specifications PNF Primary Care Reserved AMs

IV › 100 mg/5 mL vial 25 mg/5 mL vial (Rx)

Dosage

IV › 100 mg/5 mL vial 25 mg/5 mL vial (Rx)

WARNING: Should only be used by physicians experienced in immunosuppressive therapy for the treatment of
renal transplant patients. Medical surveillance is required during infusion. Acute renal transplant rejection, by IV
infusion, 1.5 mg/kg daily for 7–14 days. Aplastic anemia, 3.5 mg/kg/day for 5 days; may administer additional therapy
Double check with FDA if approved indication

Administration

IV › 100 mg/5 mL vial 25 mg/5 mL vial (Rx)

Administration: Infuse the first dose over at least 6 hours through a high-flow vein. Administer subsequent doses
over at least 4 hours through an in-line 0.22–micron filter. NOTE: Premedication with acetaminophen,
antihistamines, and/or corticosteroids may reduce infusion-related reactions.

Dose Adjustment

Obesity:
Use actual body weight (full weight) for calculation of weightbased dosing (mg/kg) for hematopoietic stemcell
transplant conditioning regimens.

Toxicity:
If WBC count is 2,000–3,000 cells/mm3 or platelet count is 50,000–75,000 cells/mm3, reduce dose by 50%.
If WBC count is <2,000 cells/mm3 or platelet count is <50,000 cells/mm3, consider discontinuing treatment.

Indications

Management of acute rejection of renal transplant; used in conjunction with concomitant immunosuppression;
severe aplastic anemia

Contraindications

Acute or chronic infection

Precautions

Cytokine release syndrome [CRS] (ranging from a mild, selflimiting “flu-like reaction” to severe, life-threatening
reactions with symptoms of hypotension, acute respiratory distress syndrome, pulmonary edema, myocardial
infarction, and tachycardia)

Infection; Malignancy (lymphoma, post-transplant lymphoproliferative disease [PTLD] or other malignancies)

Hematologic toxicity (reversible neutropenia and thrombocytopenia)

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Abdominal pain, chills, antirabbit antibody development, diarrhea, fever, cytomegalovirus infection,
dizziness, dyspnea, gastritis, GI moniliasis, headache, herpes simplex infection, hyperkalemia, hypertension, malaise,
nausea, leukopenia, oral moniliasis, pain, peripheral edema, sepsis, systemic infection, tachycardia,
thrombocytopenia, UTI, weakness

Less Common: Anaphylaxis, cytokine release syndrome, PTLD, neutropenia, serum sickness

Drug Interactions

Enhances therapeutic effect of Antithymocyte Immunoglobulin: Roflumilast (immunosuppressive effect)
Enhances therapeutic effect of Tofacitinib (immunosuppressive effect)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Antithymocyte Immunoglobulin: Pimecrolimus Tacrolimus, Topical
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Leflunomide (hematologic toxicity, i.e.,
pancytopenia, agranulocytosis, thrombocytopenia); Natalizumab (concurrent infection); Vaccines, Live (infection)
Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs: BCG, Intravesical; Vaccines (diminished immune response)

Mechanism of Action

Polyclonal antibody which appears to cause immunosuppression by acting on T-cell surface antigens and depleting
CD4 lymphocytes.
