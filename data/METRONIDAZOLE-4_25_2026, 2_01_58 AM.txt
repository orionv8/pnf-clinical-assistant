Dosage

ORAL › [Access] 250 mg tablet (as base) (Rx)

Amoebiasis, by mouth, ADULT, 35–50 mg/kg per day in 3 divided doses for 7–10 days or 800 mg 3 times daily for 5
days (intestinal infection); 5–10 days (extra-intestinal infection), (maximum, 2.4 g daily); CHILD 35 – 50 mg/ kg per day
in 3 divided doses for 7 – 10 days. Giardiasis, by mouth, ADULT, 15 mg/kg per day in 3 divided doses for 5–7 days or 2
g once daily for 3 days, 400 mg 3 times daily for 5 days, or 500 mg twice daily for 7–10 days; CHILD 1–3 years, 500 mg
once daily; CHILD 3–7 years, 600–800 mg once daily; CHILD 7–10 years, 1 g once daily; doses are given for 3 days.
Suspected antibiotic-associated colitis presenting as severe disease or with prolonged symptoms, by mouth,
ADULT and CHILD, 30 mg/kg per day in 4 divided doses for 10– 14 days. Severe diarrhea due to Entamoeba
histolytica, by mouth, ADULT, 500–750 mg 3 times a day for 7–10 days. Liver abscess with positive amoeba serology,
by mouth or IV, ADULT, 750 mg 3 times daily for 10 days. Trichomoniasis, first-line, by mouth, ADULT (women and
pregnant women), 2 g single dose; ADULT (women with HIV infection), 500 mg 2 times daily for 7 days.
Trichomoniasis, second-line, by mouth, ADULT, 500 mg 2 times daily for 7 days.

Administration

ORAL › [Access] 250 mg tablet (as base) (Rx)

For Oral: Tablets should be swallowed whole with water, during or after a meal; oral suspension should be taken
one hour before a meal or on an empty stomach. For IV: Infuse intravenously over 30 to 60 minutes; Avoid contact
of drug solution with equipment containing aluminum.

Indications

Management of acute invasive amebic dysentery; trichomonal vaginitis; amoebiasis; giardiasis

Contraindications

Chronic alcohol dependence

Mechanism of Action

A nitroimidazole, antiprotozoal drug, which has potent antibacterial activity against anaerobes, including
Bacteroides and Clostridium spp.
