Administration

I-BRONCHI / IT › 80 mg/mL sterile suspension (intratracheal / intrabronchial), 1.5 mL vial (Rx)

For intratracheal administration only. Slowly withdraw contents of the vial into a 3 or 5 mL plastic syringe through a
large-gauge needle (at least 20 gauge). Each vial is single-use only. Attach the pre-cut 9- cm 5-French end-hole
catheter to the syringe. Fill the catheter with the suspension. Discard excess suspension through the catheter so
that only the required dose remains in the syringe. Administer in 2 divided aliquots: 1st dose: 1.25 mL/kg birth
weight (BW) Succeeding doses: 0.635 mL/kg BW Should be administered by, or under the supervision of clinicians
experienced in intubation, ventilator management, and general care of premature infants. Prior to administration,
ensure proper placement and patency of the endotracheal tube. At the discretion of the clinician, the
endotracheal tube may be suctioned before administration. Allow the infant to stabilize before proceeding with
dosing. Administer either: Intratracheally by instillation in 2 divided aliquots through a 5-French end-hole catheter
after briefly disconnecting the endotracheal tube from the ventilator; or Intratracheally in a single aliquot through
the secondary lumen of a dual lumen endotracheal tube without interrupting mechanical ventilation.

Dose Adjustment

No information found

Indications

For the rescue treatment of respiratory distress
syndrome (RDS) in pre-term babies; prophylactic use in
premature infants at risk of RDS

Precautions

WARNING: Should only be administered by those trained and experienced in the care, resuscitation, and stabilization
of pre-term infants.

Acute changes in oxygenation and lung compliance (produces rapid improvements in lung oxygenation and
compliance that may require frequent adjustments to oxygen delivery and ventilator settings); Transient adverse
effects; (associated with transient episodes of bradycardia, hypotension, endotracheal tube blockade, and oxygen
desaturation).
Children (safety and efficacy in full term infants or older
pediatric patients with respiratory failure has not been
established).

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
Acquired pneumonia, acquired septicemia,
bronchopulmonary dysplasia, intracranial hemorrhage,
patent ductus arteriosus, pneumothorax, pulmonary
interstitial emphysema

Less Common:
Pulmonary hemorrhage

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs:
Paromomycin, Spectinomycin, Streptomycin

Avoid concomitant use with:
Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs:
Amikacin, Amphotericin B, Gentamicin, Kanamycin, Neomycin, Tobramycin, Vancomycin

Mechanism of Action

A sterile, non-pyrogenic pulmonary surfactant intended for intratracheal use only. It is an extract of natural porcine
lung surfactant consisting of 99% polar lipids (mainly phospholipids) and 1% hydrophobic low molecular
weight proteins (surfactant associated proteins SP-B and SP-C).
