Dosage

IV › 50 mg/mL, 20 mL vial (Rx)

NOTE: Used in conjunction with atropine. Establish a response to atropine before administering pralidoxime.
Organophosphate poisoning, by IV injection, ADULT and ADOLESCENT >16 years, 1,000–2,000 mg loading dose,
followed by maintenance repeat bolus of 1,000–2,000 mg after 1 hour and repeated every 10–12 hours thereafter, as
needed; or 30 mg/kg loading dose followed by a maintenance infusion of 8 mg/kg per hour; ADOLESCENT ≤16
years and CHILD, 20–50 mg/kg loading dose (maximum, 2,000 mg/dose), then administer maintenance infusion of
10–20 mg/kg per hour; or a repeat bolus of 20–50 mg/kg (maximum, 2000, mg/dose) may be administered after 1
hour and repeated every 10–12 hours thereafter, as needed. NOTE: For persistent symptoms, repeat the entire
series (severe symptoms) approximately 1 hour after administration of the last injection.

Administration

IV › 50 mg/mL, 20 mL vial (Rx)

Administer via IV infusion as 10–20 mg/mL solution over 15–30 minutes. In the presence of pulmonary edema
and/or fluid restriction, administer as a 50 mg/mL solution by slow IV injection over ≥5 minutes. Administer
continuous or intermittent IV infusion at a rate not exceeding 200 mg/minute. Discard any unused portions.
Consider IM or SQ administration when IV administration is not feasible. DILUTION. Dilute 1,000 mg with 20 mL SWFI
to make 50 mg/mL for fluid-restricted patients or where a more rapid administration is required. For all other
patients, further dilute with NS to a final concentration of 10–20 mg/mL.

Dose Adjustment

Renal Impairment:
Dose reduction is recommended.

Indications

Treatment of muscle weakness and/or respiratory depression secondary to poisoning due to organophosphate
anticholinesterase pesticides and chemicals (e.g., nerve agents)

Precautions

Myasthenia gravis;
Renal impairment;
Hepatic impairment;
Lactation.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Cardiac arrest, hypertension, tachycardia, dizziness, drowsiness, headache, paralysis, seizure, skin rash,
nausea, fasciculations, laryngospasm, muscle rigidity, weakness, accommodation disturbance, blurred vision,
diplopia, renal insufficiency, apnea, hyperventilation heparin; or 25–50 mg loading dose via slow IV injection, and the
remaining dose via continuous IV infusion over 8–16 hours, or the expected duration of heparin absorption.

Drug Interactions

No known significant interactions

Mechanism of Action

An antidote that reactivates cholinesterase that had been inactivated by phosphorylation due to exposure to
organophosphate pesticides and cholinesteras inhibiting nerve agents.
