Dosage

IM / IV › 1 g powder for injection (Rx)

Tuberculosis, by deep IM injection or IV infusion, ADULT, 1 g/day (max: 20 mg/kg/day) for 60-120 days, followed by 1 g
2-3 times/week or 15 mg/kg/day (max: 1 g/dose) for 2-4 months, followed by 15 mg/kg (max: 1 g/dose) 2-3
times/week; CHILD <15 years and ≤40kg: 15-30 mg/kg/day (max: 1 g/day) for 2-4 months, followed by 15-30 mg/kg
(max: 1 g/day) twice weekly; CHILD ≥15 years or >40kg: 15 mg/kg/day (max: 1 g/dose) for 2-4 months followed by 15
mg/kg (max: 1 g/dose) 2-3 times/week.

Administration

IM / IV › 1 g powder for injection (Rx)

Administer by deep IM injection into large muscle mass. Infuse IV over 60 minutes.

Indications

Second line treatment of multi-drug resistant tuberculosis in conjunction with at least one three other anti-
tuberculosis agent

Contraindications

Hypersensitivity to capreomycin or any component of the formulation.

Precautions

WARNING: Use caution in patients with pre-existing auditory impairment due to risk of additional eighth nerve
impairment and should be weighed against the benefits. Use caution in patients with renal impairment.

Other parenteral anti-tuberculosis agents (i.e. streptomycin) also have similar and sometimes irreversible toxic
effects, particularly on the eighth cranial nerve and renal function, simultaneous administration of these agents with
capreomycin is not recommended;
Safety has not been established in children and pregnant women.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Ototoxicity and nephrotoxicity

Less Common: Eosinophilia

Rare: Acute tubular necrosis, Bartter syndrome, hypersentivity, hypocalcemia, hypokalemia, hypomagesemia,
injection site reactions, leukocytosis, leukopenia, renal injury, thrombocytopenia, tinnitus, toxic nephritis, urinary
sediment abnormal, vertigo.

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of the following drugs:
Aminoglycosides e.g. Streptomycin; Neuromuscular-blocking agents e.g. Pancuronium; Polymyxin B

Enhances toxic effect of Capreomycin:
Non-tuberculosis drugs (i.e. aminoglycosides) (ototoxic or nephrotoxic effect)

Reduces therapeutic effect of BCG Vaccine

Mechanism of Action

Antimycobacterial drugs are used in the treatment of diseases caused by members of the Mycobacterium genus,
including tuberculosis (TB) and leprosy.
Combination chemotherapy is essential to kill heterogeneous populations of bacterial cells located in different
conditions within the host, and to prevent drug resistance.
