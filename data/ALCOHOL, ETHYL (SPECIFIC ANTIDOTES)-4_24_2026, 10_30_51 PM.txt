Dosage

ORAL › 95%, USP grade (Rx)

NOTE: IV administration is the preferred route. Continue therapy until methanol is no longer detected or at <20
mg/dL AND the patient is asymptomatic and metabolic acidosis has been corrected. Methanol poisoning, by IV
injection, ADULT and CHILD, initially 600–700 mg/kg (equivalent to 7.6 to 8.9 mL/kg using a 10% solution);
maintenance dose for nondrinkers, 66 mg/kg per hour maintenance dose (equivalent to 0.83 mL/kg per hour using
a 10% solution); maintenance dose for chronic drinkers, 154 mg/kg per hour maintenance dose (equivalent to 1.96
mL/kg per hour using a 10% solution). NOTE: Goal of therapy is to maintain serum alcohol levels >100 mg/dL.
Methanol poisoning, by mouth, ADULT and CHILD, initially 600–700 mg/kg (equivalent to 0.09 mL/kg per hour using
a 98% solution); maintenance dose for nondrinkers, 66 mg/kg per hour maintenance dose (equivalent to 0.78
mL/kg per hour using a 98% solution); maintenance dose for chronic drinkers, 154 mg/kg per hour maintenance
dose (equivalent to 0.20 mL/kg per hour using a 98% solution).

Administration

ORAL › 95%, USP grade (Rx)

For IV administration, administer via a central vein as a 10% solution in D5W. Administer the initial dose over 1 hour.
For oral administration, dilute solutions to ≤20% concentration with water or juice and administer every hour. May
also be administered via nasogastric tube. Alcoholic beverages may be substituted, but may contain different
percentages of ethanol: Vodka ~40% Wine ~12% Beer ~5% Out-of-hospital management is not recommended.
NOTE: Absolute ethyl alcohol [98% (196 proof) = 77.4 g ethanol/dL]

Dose Adjustment

Hemodialysis: Maintenance dose via IV administration:
For nondrinkers, 169 mg/kg per hour (equivalent to 0.22
mL/kg per hour using a 10% solution).
For chronic drinkers, 257 mg/kg per hour (equivalent to
3.26 mL/kg per hour using a 10% solution).

Maintenance dose via oral administration:
For nondrinkers, 169 mg/kg per hour (equivalent to 0.22
mL/kg per hour using a 98% solution).
For chronic drinkers, 257 mg/kg per hour (equivalent to
0.33 mL/kg per hour using a 98% solution).

Indications

Antidote for methyl alcohol (methanol) poisoning NOTE: Preferred antidote for methanol poisoning is fomepizole.
Use only if fomepizole is unavailable.

Precautions

Diabetes; Hepatic impairment or shock (use with caution); Children; Pregnancy (crosses the placenta, enters fetal
circulation, and has teratogenic effects); Lactation.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
Flushing, hypotension, agitation, CNS depression, coma, disorientation, drowsiness, encephalopathy, headache,
sedation, vertigo, hypoglycemia, gastric irritation, nausea, vomiting, urinary retention, nerve and tissue destruction,
phlebitis, polyuria, intoxication

Rare:
Seizure

Mechanism of Action

A clear, aqueous solution of ethyl alcohol that is used in the treatment of methanol toxicity due to its higher affinity
to alcohol dehydrogenase, thus inhibiting the formation of toxic metabolites of methanol
