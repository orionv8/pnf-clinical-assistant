Dosage

IV BOLUS / IV INFUSION › 20 mg vial (Rx)

Acute renal transplant rejection prophylaxis, by IV infusion, ADULT and CHILD=35 kg, with cyclosporine and
corticosteroids, 20 mg within 2 hours prior to transplant surgery, followed by a second 20 mg dose 4 days after
transplantation; withhold second dose if complications occur; CHILD<35 kg, with cyclosporine and
corticosteroids, 10 mg within 2 hours prior to transplant surgery, followed by a second 10 mg dose 4 days after
transplantation; the second dose should be withheld if complications occur (including severe hypersensitivity
reactions or graft loss).

Administration

IV BOLUS / IV INFUSION › 20 mg vial (Rx)

For IV administration only. Administer by IV bolus infusion or IV infusion over 20–30 minutes. Administer only after
assurance that patient will receive renal graft and immunosuppression. NOTE: Bolus dosing is associated with
nausea, vomiting, and local pain at the injection site.

Indications

Prophylaxis of acute organ rejection in renal transplantation

Contraindications

No information found

Precautions

WARNING: Should only be used by physicians experienced in immunosuppressive therapy and management of
transplant patients. Adequate laboratory and supportive medical resources must be readily available.

Re-exposure to basiliximab with a subsequent course of therapy should only be done with extreme caution.

Anaphylactoid/hypersensitivity reactions Diabetes (new-onset diabetes) Human antimurine antibodies [HAMA]
(lymphoproliferative disorders (may increase incidence)); opportunistic infections (may increase incidence))

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Basiliximab: Trastuzumab (neutropenic effect)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Basiliximab: Denosumab (serious infections)
Reduces diagnostic effect of Coccidioides immitis Skin Test

Avoid concomitant use with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Basiliximab: Roflumilast (immunosuppressive effect)
Enhances therapeutic effect of Tofacitinib (immunosuppressive effect)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Basiliximab: Pimecrolimus, Tacrolimus (Topical),
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Belimumab, Leflunomide (hematologic toxicity, such
as pancytopenia, agranulocytosis, and/or thrombocytopenia), Natalizumab (concurrent infection), Live Vaccines
(infection)
Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs: BCG (Intravesical), Vaccines

Mechanism of Action

Chimeric (murine/human) immunosuppressant monoclonal antibody, which blocks the alpha-chain of the
interleukin2 (IL-2) receptor complex. This receptor is expressed on activated T-lymphocytes and is a critical pathway
for activating cell-mediated allograft rejection.
