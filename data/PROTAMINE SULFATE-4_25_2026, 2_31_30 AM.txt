Dosage

IV › 10 mg/mL, 5 mL ampule (Rx)

NOTE: Individualize doses based on administered heparin, its route of administration, time elapsed since heparin
administration, and blood coagulation studies. Each mg of protamine, calculated on the dried basis, neutralizes
not less than 100 USP heparin units. Dosing should be guided by blood coagulation studies. Since heparin
disappears rapidly from circulation, protamine dose should also decrease with time elapsed following heparin
administration. Heparin overdose (heparin administered via IV injection), by IV injection, ADULT, administer at ≤5
mg/minute (maximum, 50 mg/dose); a few minutes after heparin administration, 1 mg for every 100 U of heparin;
30–60 minutes after heparin administration, 0.5–0.75 mg for every 100 U of heparin; ≥2 hours after heparin
administration, 0.25 to 0.375 mg for every 100 U of heparin. Heparin overdose (heparin administered via IV
infusion), by IV injection, ADULT, 25–50 mg after stopping heparin administration. Heparin overdose (heparin
administered via SQ injection), by IV injection, ADULT, 1–1.5 mg for every 100 U ofheparin; or 25–50 mg loading dose
via slow IV injection, and the remaining dose via continuous IV infusion over 8–16 hours, or the expected duration
of heparin absorption. Enoxaparin overdose, by IV infusion, ADULT, if ≤8 hours since administration, 1 mg for every
1 mg of enoxaparin; if >8 hours since administration, 0.5 mg for every 1 mg of enoxaparin. Dalteparin overdose, by IV
infusion, ADULT, 1 mg for every 100 anti-Xa U of dalteparin; if aPTT remains prolonged 2–4 hours after first
infusion, administer a second infusion.

Administration

IV › 10 mg/mL, 5 mL ampule (Rx)

For IV use only. Administer undiluted via very slow IV injection over a 10- minute period in doses not exceeding 50
mg. If further dilution is desired, dilute using D5W or NS. Do NOT store diluted solutions. Inspect visually for
particulate matter and discoloration prior to administration.

Indications

Treatment of heparin overdose

Contraindications

Known drug intolerance

Precautions

WARNING:
Can cause severe hypotension, cardiovascular collapse, noncardiogenic pulmonary edema, catastrophic pulmonary
vasoconstriction, and pulmonary hypertension.
Vasopressors and resuscitation equipment should be immediately available in case of a severe reaction to
protamine.

Should NOT be given when bleeding occurs without prior heparin use.

Hyperheparinemia or bleeding has been reported 30 minutes - 18 hours after cardiac surgery. Keep patient under
close observation after cardiac surgery.

Do NOT administer more than 50 mg over a short period unless a larger dose is clearly needed.
Hypersensitivity;

Drug Interactions

Avoid concomitant use with:
Incompatible with the following products:
Antibiotics, e.g., Cephalosporins, Penicillins

Mechanism of Action

Simple proteins of low molecular weight that are rich in
arginine and strongly basic. Forms a stable salt with
heparin (strongly acidic) to inhibit the anticoagulant
activity of both drugs.
