Dosage

IM / IV › 20 mg/mL, 10 mL vial (as chloride) (Rx)

NOTE: Dose to effect. Doses will vary due to interpatient variability. Initial dose of succinylcholine must be
increased when nondepolarizing agent pretreatment is used. Intubation, by IV injection, ADULT, 0.6 mg/kg (range,
0.3– 1.1 mg/kg); OLDER CHILD and ADOLESCENT, initially 1 mg/kg per dose (maintenance, 0.3–0.6 mg/kg every 5– 10
minutes as needed); YOUNGER CHILD, initially 2 mg/kg per dose (maintenance, 0.3–0.6 mg/kg every 5– 10 minutes
as needed). Rapid sequence intubation, by IV injection, ADULT, 1–1.5 mg/kg; OLDER CHILD and ADOLESCENT,
initially 1 mg/kg per dose (maintenance, 0.3–0.6 mg/kg every 5– 10 minutes as needed); YOUNGER CHILD, initially 2
mg/kg per dose (maintenance, 0.3–0.6 mg/kg every 5– 10 minutes as needed). Muscle relaxant in general
anesthesia, by IV injection, ADULT, 0.3–1.1 mg/kg as single dose, supplementary doses of 50–100% the initial dose
may be given at 5-to 10-minute intervals; CHILD 1-12 years, 1 mg/kg; CHILD <1 year, 2 mg/kg; for prolonged
procedures, ADULT, 0.1 – 0.2% at 2.54 mg/minute, adjusted as necessary.

Administration

IM / IV › 20 mg/mL, 10 mL vial (as chloride) (Rx)

May be given by rapid IV injection without further dilution.

Dose Adjustment

Obesity:
Use ideal body weight for obese patients.

Indications

To facilitate both rapid sequence and routine endotracheal intubation; to relax skeletal muscles during surgery

Contraindications

Personal or familial history of malignant hyperthermia; myopathies associated with elevated serum creatine
phosphokinase (CPK) values; acute phase of injury following major burns, multiple trauma, extensive denervation of
skeletal muscle, or upper motor neuron injury

Precautions

WARNING: Acute rhabdomyolysis with hyperkalemia, ventricular arrhythmias and cardiac arrest have been reported
(rarely) in children with undiagnosed skeletal muscle myopathy. Prolonged resuscitation may be required. Use in
children should be reserved for emergency intubation or where immediate airway control is necessary.

Patients with reduced plasma cholinesterase activity due to genetic abnormalities of plasma cholinesterase or when
associated with other conditions, e.g., pregnancy, severe liver disease, renal disease (consider dose reduction);

Bradycardia;

Intraocular pressure, IOP (use caution with narrow-angle glaucoma or penetrating eye injuries);

Malignant hyperthermia (risk may be increased with concomitant administration of volatile anesthetics);

Vagal tone;

Burn injury;

Conditions which may antagonize neuromuscular blockade (e.g., alkalosis, hypercalcemia, demyelinating lesions,
peripheral neuropathies, denervation, infection, muscle trauma, and diabetes mellitus);

Conditions which may potentiate neuromuscular blockade (e.g., electrolyte abnormalities, severe hyponatremia,
severe hypocalcemia, severe hypokalemia, hypermagnesemia, neuromuscular diseases, acidosis, acute intermittent
porphyria, Lambert-Eaton syndrome, myasthenia gravis, renal failure, and hepatic failure);

Hyperkalemia;

Plasma pseudocholinesterase disorders;

Elderly;

Pregnancy (use with caution);

Lactation (use with caution).

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common and Less Common: Arrhythmias, bradycardia, cardiac arrest, hypertension or hypotension, tachycardia,
rash, hyperkalemia, excessive salivation, jaw rigidity, muscle fasciculation, postoperative muscle pain,
rhabdomyolysis (with possible myoglobinuric acute renal failure), increased intraocular pressure, acute renal failure
(secondary to rhabdomyolysis), apnea, respiratory depression (prolonged), anaphylaxis, malignant hyperthermia,
acute quadriplegic myopathy syndrome (prolonged use), myositis ossificans (prolonged use)

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Suxamethonium: Aminoglycosides (respiratory depressant effect), Bambuterol,
Capreomycin, Clindamycin Topical, Cyclosporine Systemic, Lincosamide, Lithium, Loop Diuretics, Magnesium Salts,
Minocycline, Procainamide, Quinidine, Tetracycline Derivatives, Vancomycin
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Analgesics Opioid (bradycardic effect), Cardiac
Glycosides e.g., Digoxin (arrhythmogenic effect)
Reduces therapeutic effect of Suxamethonium: Loop Diuretics e.g., Furosemide

Avoid concomitant use with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Suxamethonium: Colistimethate, Phenelzine (neuromuscular-blocking effect),
Polymyxin B, Quinine
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Suxamethonium: Acetylcholinesterase Inhibitors e.g., Neostigmine
(prolonged neuromuscular blockade)
Increases serum concentration of Suxamethonium: Acetylcholinesterase Inhibitors e.g., Neostigmine,
Cyclophosphamide, Echothiophate Iodide

Mechanism of Action

A short-acting muscle relaxant that acts like acetylcholine. It produces depolarization of the motor end-plate at the
myoneural junction which causes sustained flaccid skeletal muscle paralysis.
