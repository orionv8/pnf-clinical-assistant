Dosage

OPHTHALM › 3% eye ointment, 4.5 g tube (Rx)

Herpes simplex keratitis, into the eye, ADULT, apply a small amount (~1 cm ribbon) into the affected eye(s) 5 times
daily at approx. 4-hour intervals while awake, continue treatment for at least 3 days after resolution of keratitis.

Administration

OPHTHALM › 3% eye ointment, 4.5 g tube (Rx)

For ophthalmic application only. Apply to the inside of the lower eyelid, then close the eye(s) for 30 seconds
following application. Do NOT wear contact lenses during treatment. Avoid touching the applicator to the eye or
other surfaces.

Dose Adjustment

Dose adjustment is unlikely due to low systemic absorption

Indications

Treatment of herpes simplex keratitis

Contraindications

Hypersensitivity to acyclovir, valacyclovir,
or any component of the formulation

Precautions

Ocular effects (e.g., transient stinging immediately following
application; blurred vision).

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
Foreign body sensation of the eye, epithelial keratopathy, eye irritation, conjunctivitis, stinging of the eye

Less Common:
Blepharitis, eyelid edema, hypersensitivity reaction

Drug Interactions

No known significant interactions

Mechanism of Action

An acyclic purine nucleoside analogue which inhibits viral DNA polymerase, thus preventing viral DNA synthesis.
