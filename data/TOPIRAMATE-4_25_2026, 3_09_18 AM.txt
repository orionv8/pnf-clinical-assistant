Administration

ORAL › 25 mg Tablet (Rx)

May be taken with or without food. Administer in divided doses. Do NOT crush, break, or chew tablets due to bitter
taste. Do NOT abruptly discontinue therapy because of the possibility of increasing seizure frequency. Taper
dosage gradually to prevent rebound effects and to minimize the potential of increased seizure frequency, unless
safety concerns require a more rapid withdrawal.

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric:
An initial dose of 25 mg daily may be recommended, followed by incremental increases of 25 mg at weekly intervals
until an effective dose is reached.

Renal Impairment:
In CrCl <70 mL/minute, administer 50% of the dose and titrate more slowly.
In hemodialysis, supplemental dose may be needed.

Indications

Initial monotherapy or adjunct therapy inpatients ≥2 years with partial onset or primary generalized tonic-clonic
seizures.

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Decreases excretion of the following drugs: Memantine, Quinidine
Enhances therapeutic effect of Topiramate: Brimonidine (Topical), Doxylamine, Dronabinol, Hydroxyzine, Magnesium
Sulfate, Minocycline, Nabilone (CNS depressant effect)
Enhances therapeutic effect of the following drugs: Amitriptyline, Mirtazapine (CNS depressant effect) Pramipexole,
Ropinirole, Rotigotine (sedative effect)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Topiramate: Anticholinergics Loop Diuretics (hypokalemic effect) Other
CNS Depressants Rufinamide (sleepiness and dizziness)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: SSRIs (psychomotor impairment) Primidone
(osteomalacia and rickets) Valproate Products

Avoid concomitant use with:
Decreases serum concentration of Topiramate: Carbamazepine, Mefloquine
Decreases serum concentration of the following drugs: Aripiprazole [double dose when used concomitantly; reduce
dose to 10–15 mg daily when topiramate is discontinued], Contraceptives [Estrogens and Progestins], Ulipristal
Enhances therapeutic effect (CNS depressant effect) of Topiramate: Droperidol, Ethyl Alcohol, Methotrimeprazine
[reduce midazolam dose by 50% when used concomitantly] Tapentadol [start at one-third to one-half of the normal
dose]
Enhances therapeutic effect (CNS depressant effect) of the following drugs: Buprenorphine, Hydrocodone [consider
starting with a 20–30% lower dose when used concomitantly], Methotrimeprazine [reduce midazolam dose by 50%
when used concomitantly], Orphenadrine, Paraldehyde, Perampanel, Sodium Oxybate, Thalidomide, Zolpidem
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Topiramate: Other Carbonic Anhydrase Inhibitors (acid-base disorders;
kidney stones; metabolic acidosis) Salicylates Thiazide Diuretics (hypokalemic effect)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Azelastine, Nasal (CNS depressant effect) Metformin
Salicylates (salicylate toxicity)
Increases serum concentration of Topiramate: Ethyl Alcohol, Thiazide Diuretics
Reduces therapeutic effect of Topiramate: Mefloquine
Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs: Contraceptives, Estrogens and Progestins (contraceptive failure
is possible) Methenamine

FOOD INTERACTION. Ketogenic diet may increase the possibility of acidosis and/or kidney stones.

Mechanism of Action

Topiramate is a sulfamate-substituted monosaccharide that potentiates GABA action and inhibits glutamate release
through interfering with both calcium and sodium channels.
