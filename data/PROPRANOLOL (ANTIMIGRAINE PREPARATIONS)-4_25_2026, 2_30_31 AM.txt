Dosage

ORAL › 10 mg tablet (as hydrochloride) (Rx)

Migraine headache prophylaxis, by mouth, ADULT, initially 80 mg daily divided every 6–8 hours, increase by 20–40
mg/dose every 3–4 weeks to a maximum of 160–240 mg daily given in divided doses every 6–8 hours; if satisfactory
response is not achieved within 6 weeks of starting therapy, withdraw drug gradually over several weeks; CHILD
>12 years, initially, 40 mg twice or thrice daily, increase to weekly intervals up to 160 mg daily (maximum, 240 mg
daily); CHILD =12 years, 10–20 mg twice or thrice daily.

Administration

ORAL › 10 mg tablet (as hydrochloride) (Rx)

To be taken on an empty stomach. See Propranolol under Cardiac Therapy – Antiarrhythmics (Class II
Antiarrhythmics) in Cardiovascular System for other information.

Indications

Prophylaxis for migraine headache.

Contraindications

BP <50/30 mmHg; uncompensated CHF (unless the failure is due to tachyarrhythmias being treated with propranolol);
cardiogenic shock; severe sinus bradycardia; sick sinus syndrome or heart block greater than first degree (except in
the presence of a functioning artificial pacemaker) ; heart rate <80 bpm; bronchial asthma; bronchospasm.

Precautions

WARNING: Do NOT withdraw abruptly, particularly in patients with CAD. Gradually taper dose to avoid acute
tachycardia, hypertension, and/or ischemia.

Mechanism of Action

Propranolol is a non-selective beta-adrenergic blocker that competitively blocks response to beta1and
beta2adrenergic stimulation. These result in decrease in heart rate, myocardial contractility, blood pressure, and
myocardial oxygen demand.
