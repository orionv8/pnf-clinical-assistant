Dosage

ORAL › 500 micrograms tablet (Rx)

ORAL Peripheral neuropath: by mouth, ADULT, 500 micrograms 3 times daily; INTRAVENOUS AND
INTRAMUSCULAR Peripheral neuropath: by IV or IM injection, ADULT, 500 micrograms 3 times weekly.
Megaloblastic anemia: by IV or IM injection, ADULT, initially 500 micrograms 3 times weekly; 500 micrograms every
1–3 months as maintenance dose after approximately 2 months of treatment).

Administration

ORAL › 500 micrograms tablet (Rx)

For IM or IV use. Administer immediately after removing from package to limit direct exposure to light. For IM route,
avoid multiple injections at the same site. Do NOT inject near highly innervated regions. NOTE: Do NOT use for
more than 1 month without clear signs of clinical improvement. Avoid long term use in patients regularly exposed
to mercury or mercury compounds. See Mecobalamin under Vitamins in Chapter 1: Alimentary Tract and Metabolism
for other information.

Indications

Symptomatic treatment of peripheral neuropathy associated with vitamin B12 deficiency (e.g., numbness, pain,
paralysis), including diabetic neuropathy and polyneuropathy; management of megaloblastic anemia associated with
vitamin B12 deficiency.

Mechanism of Action

A form of Vitamin B12, a water-soluble vitamin It acts as coenzymes in nucleic acid synthesis.
