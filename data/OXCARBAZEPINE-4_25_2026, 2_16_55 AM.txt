Dose Adjustment

Renal Impairment:
In CrCl <30 mL/minute, initiate at ½ the usual starting dose and increase slowly to achieve the desired clinical
response.

Indications

Management of partial-onset seizure in adults and children and partial seizure with secondary generalization.

Contraindications

No information found

Precautions

Hyponatremia; anaphylactic reactions and angioedema (eg., laryngeal edema can be fatal); serious dermatological
reactions (some fatal); bone metabolism.

Suicidal behavior and ideation; neuropsychiatric adverse events; DRESS or multi-organ hypersensitivity (some fatal);
hematologic events; glaucoma; hyponatremia.

HLA-B*1502 allele carrier (e.g., SJS or toxic epidermal necrolysis (SJS/TEN) during treatment).

Elderly (increased incidence of adverse effects).

Pregnancy (no adequate and well-controlled studies); lactation (excreted in breastmilk).

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Dizziness, somnolence, diplopia, fatigue, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, abnormal vision, ataxia, tremor,
dyspepsia, abnormal gait, nystagmus, maculopapular rash, convulsions, rash, status epilepticus, fatigue, edema,
weight gain, asthenia, diarrhea, hypotension, hyponatremia, sprains, strains, muscle weakness, gastritis,
constipation, acne, headache, nervousness, agitation, dysmetria, abnormal coordination, speech disorder, rhinitis,
dry mouth, toothache, lymphadenopathy, rectal hemorrhage, infection, thirst, emotional lability, hypoesthesia,
pharyngitis, purpura, earache, ear infection, vaginitis, micturition frequency.

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of CNS Depressants

Avoid concomitant use with:
Decreases serum concentration of Hormonal contraceptives
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: MAO Inhibitors (serotonin syndrome)
Reduces therapeutic effect of Hormonal contraceptives

Mechanism of Action

Oxcarbazepine is a prodrug related to carbamazepine. It works by binding to the open channel conformation of
voltage-sensitive sodium channels at a site within the channel itself at the a subunit.
