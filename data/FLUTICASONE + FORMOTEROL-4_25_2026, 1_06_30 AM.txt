Indications

Management of adult asthma

Contraindications

acute asthma attack

Precautions

Patient with active or quiescent pulmonary TB, untreated systemic infections, ocular herpes simplex, or with fungal,
viral or airway infections, thyrotoxicosis, pheochromocytoma, diabetes mellitus, hypokalemia, cardiovascular
disorders; hepatic impairment; Pregnancy and lactation.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Hypokalemia, paradoxical bronchospasm, increased wheezing, shortness of breath, QTc interval prolongation,
insomnia, headache, tremor, dizziness, abnormal dreams, agitation, asthenia, palpitations, ventricular extra-systoles,
angina pectoris, tachycardia, hypertension, peripheral edema, oral candidiasis or fungal infections, hoarseness,
stomatitis, dry mouth, diarrhea, dyspepsia, nausea, asthma exacerbation, dysphonia, throat irritation, sinusitis,
hyperglycemia, increased blood lactate levels, decrease BMD, cataract, glaucoma, rash, pruritus, contusion, skin
atrophy, anaphylaxis reactions, arrhythmia (potentially fatal), cardiac arrest (potentially fatal)

Drug Interactions

Avoid concomitant use with:
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Fluticasone + Formoterol:
Alcohol (impaired cardiac tolerance)
Antihistamines e.g. Diphenhydramine (prolonged QTc interval)
Antipsyschotic Agents (prolonged QTc-interval)
Digitalis Glycoside e.g. Digoxin (ventricular arrhythmias)
Disopyramide (prolonged QTc-interval)
Halogenated Hydrocarbon Anesthetics (ventricular arrhythmias)
L-dopa (impaired cardiac tolerance)
L-thyroxine (impaired cardiac tolerance)
MAO Inhibitors e.g. Phenelzine (prolonged QTc-interval)
Oxytocin (impaired cardiac tolerance)
Procainamide (prolonged QTc-interval)
Quinidine (prolonged QTc-interval)
Tricyclic Antidepressants (prolonged QTc-interval)

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs:
Diuretics (hypokalemia)
Steroids (hypokalemia)
Xanthine Derivatives e.g. Theophylline (hypokalemia)

Increases serum concentration of Fluticasone:
CYP3A4 Inhibitors e.g., Ketoconazole, HIV Protease
Inhibitors, Clarithromycin, Ketoconazole

Reduces therapeutic effect of Fluticasone + Formoterol:
Beta Blockers e.g. Propranolol

Mechanism of Action

A combination product containing a corticosteroid with glucocorticoid activity and a long-acting beta2-adrenergic
agonist.
