Dosage

ORAL › 25 mg tablet (as fumarate) (Rx)

Acute bipolar mania, by mouth, ADULT, monotherapy and adjunct to lithium/valproate: initially 50 mg twice daily.
Increase by 50 mg twice daily each day up to 200 mg twice daily. Further dose increases up to a maximum of 400
mg twice daily should be done in increments of no greater than 100 mg twice daily, CHILD=10 years, monotherapy:
initially 25 mg twice daily. Increase to 50 mg twice daily on day 2, then increase by 50 mg twice daily each day up to
200 mg twice daily on day 5. Further dose adjustments may be done in increments of =100 mg/day up to a maximum
of 600 mg/day in 2-3 divided doses. Acute schizophrenia, by mouth, ADULT, initially 25 mg twice daily. Increase in
increments of 25-50 mg/day twice or thrice daily each day until an average of 300-400 mg/day in 2-3 divided doses
on day 4. Further dose adjustments should be done at intervals of no less than 2 days in increments of 25-50 mg
twice daily up to a maximum dose of 400 mg twice daily, CHILD=13 years, initially 25 mg twice daily. Increase to 50
mg twice daily on day 2 then further increase by 100 mg/day divided into 2 doses until 400 mg/day divided into 2
doses is reached on day 5. Further dose increases may be done in increments of =100 mg/day up to a maximum of
800 mg/day in 2-3 divided doses. Bipolar depression, by mouth, ADULT, initially 50 mg once daily at bedtime.
Increase to 100 mg once daily at bedtime on day 2. Increase by 100 mg/day as needed up to a maximum of 300 mg
once daily at bedtime on day 4. Bipolar maintenance, by mouth, ADULT, continue therapy at the lowest effective
dose and adjust dose depending on clinical response and tolerability. Schizophrenia maintenance, by mouth, 150–
750 mg/day (in divided doses) Depression, adjunct to any antidepressant, by mouth, 25 mg once daily NOTE:
Antipsychotics should not be discontinued abruptly because of the possibility of withdrawal and rebound
psychosis; discontinuation or cross-titration should be done gradually to minimize the potential of withdrawal,
rebound psychosis and extrapyramidal side effects, cholinergic rebound, rebound insomnia, and unwanted
pregnancy, unless safety concerns require a more rapid withdrawal.

Administration

ORAL › 25 mg tablet (as fumarate) (Rx)

May be taken with or without food.

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric:
Lower doses are generally used, but higher doses may be used if tolerated; initially 50 mg once daily and may
increase in increments of 50 mg/day to an effective dose based on efficacy and tolerability

Hepatic Impairment:
Initially 25 mg once daily or in 2 divided doses. Slowly increase by 25-50 mg once daily to effective dose.

Indications

Management of acute schizophrenia, schizophrenia maintenance, acute bipolar mania, bipolar maintenance, bipolar
depression, bipolar maintenance, depression (as an adjunct only)

Contraindications

No information found

Precautions

WARNING: NOT approved for treatment of dementia-related psychosis (increased risk of death)

Cerebrovascular disease; elderly patients at risk of aspiration pneumonia; treatment of depression in patients under
25 years (increased risk of suicide)

STORAGE AND STABILITY:
Store between 20-25°C.
Excursions permitted to 15-30°C.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Asthenia, dysarthria, dyspnea, increased appetite, irritability, peripheral edema, sleep disorders, dizziness,
increased risk for diabetes and dyslipidemia

Less Common: Hyponatremia, hypothyroidism, restless legs syndrome, rhinitis.

Rare: Angioedema, inappropriate secretion of antidiuretic hormone, rhabdomyolysis, SJS.

NOTE: Patients taking this drug may experience weight gain and/or
sedation.

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Reduces the therapeutic effects of the following drugs: Amphetamines, Guanethidine
Reduced drug clearance with: Lorazepam (by 20%)
Enhances the therapeutic effects of the following drugs: CNS depressants, Antihypertensives [except
guanethidine]
Increases risk adverse/toxic effect of Quetiapine: Metoclopramide
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Iohexol, Iomeprol, Iopamidol (seizures) Mequitazine
(arrhythmogenic effect) Serotonin modulators (neuroleptic malignant syndrome) SSRIs (psychomotor impairment)
Lorazepam – quetiapine may reduce the clearance of lorazepam by 20% Serotonin modulators – may enhance the risk
of neuroleptic malignant syndrome and serotonin syndrome with antipsychotics

Avoid concomitant use with:
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of other Antipsychotics (increased hospitalization rates and length of stay;
increased mortality)

Mechanism of Action

Quetiapine is dibenzothiazepine atypical antipsychotic structurally related to clozapine. It is a dopamine D1, dopamine
D2, 5-HT2 alpha1-adrenoceptor, and histamine-1 receptor antagonist.
