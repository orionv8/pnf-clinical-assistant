Indications

Management of transfusional iron overload if current chelation therapy in patients with thalassemia is
not insufficient.

Contraindications

Neutropenia; agranulocytosis; pregnan cy; lactation

Precautions

WARNING: Can cause agranulocytosis that can lead
to serious infections and death.

Agranulocytosis or neutropenia (eg. fatal agranulocytosis);
Hepatic impairment;
Elderly and children;
Pregnancy (can cause fetal harm);
Lactation.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
Neutropenia, agranulocytosis, nausea,
abdominal pain or discomfort, vomiting, diarrhea,
dyspepsia, decreased neutrophil, increased weight,
altered appetite, arthralgia, back pain, pain in extremity,
arthropathy, headache, chromaturia, decreased plasma
zinc concentration, urine discoloration (reddish-brown)

Less Common:
Thrombocytosis, pancytopenia, atrial
fibrillation, cardiac failure, hypospadias, diplopia,
papilledema, retinal toxicity, enterocolitis, rectal
hemorrhage, gastric ulcer, pancreatitis, parotid gland
enlargement, chills, pyrexia, edema peripheral, multi organ failure, jaundice, hepatomegaly, anaphylactic
shock, hypersensitivity, cryptococcal cutaneous
infection, enteroviral encephalitis, pharyngitis,
pneumonia, sepsis, furuncle, infectious hepatitis, rash
pustular, subcutaneous abscess, metabolic acidosis,
dehydration, myositis, chondropathy, trismus, cerebellar
syndrome, cerebral hemorrhage, convulsion, gait
disturbance, increased intracranial pressure, impaired
psychomotor skills, pyramidal tract syndrome,
somnolence, bruxism, depression, OCD, glycosuria,
hemoglobinuria, acute respiratory distress syndrome,
epistaxis, hemoptysis, pulmonary embolism,
hyperhidrosis, periorbital edema, photosensitivity
reaction, pruritus, urticaria, rash, Henoch–Schönlein
purpura, hypotension, hypertension

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Incompatible with the following products:
Products containing Polyvalent Cations (may form
chelates)

Avoid concomitant use with:
Increases serum concentration of Deferiprone:
UGT1A6 Inhibitors, e.g., Phenylbutazone

Mechanism of Action

An iron- chelating agent which binds to ferric iron and
removes it from the blood stream.
