Dosage

IV › 1 g powder, vial (Rx)

Testicular cancer, by IV infusion, ADULT, combination chemotherapy with mesna: 1,200 mg/m2 daily for 5 days over
at least 30 minutes, repeat every 3 weeks or after hematologic recovery; VIP regimen (etoposide, mesna, cisplatin)
or VeIP regimen (vinblastine, mesna, cisplatin): 1,200 mg/m2 daily for 5 days every 3 weeks for 4 cycles. Lymphoma,
sarcoma, solid tumors, by IV infusion, ADULT, Regimen 1: 8–12 g/m2 divided over 3–5 days, repeat course every 2–4
weeks; Regimen 2: 6 g/m2 divided over 5 days, repeat course every 3 weeks; Regimen 3: 5–6 g/m2 (maximum, 10 g),
give as a single 24-hour infusion, repeat course every 3 to 4 weeks; CHILD, 1,200 to 1,800 mg/m2/day for 3 to 5 days
every 21 to 28 days.

Administration

IV › 1 g powder, vial (Rx)

Administer IV infusion over at least 30 minutes (infusion times may vary by protocol; refer to specific protocol for
infusion duration) with adequate hydration of at least 2 L of oral or IV fluid daily. NOTE: Administer with mesna and
hydration of at least 2 L of oral or IV fluid per day to prevent bladder toxicity. Associated with a moderate emetic
potential. Antiemetics are recommended to prevent nausea and vomiting. See General Information on Nitrogen
Mustard Analogues under Antineoplastic Agents for other information.

Dose Adjustment

Renal Impairment:
Consider dose reduction. Ifosfamide and metabolites are excreted renally and may accumulate in patients with renal
dysfunction.
For patients with CrCl <10 mL/min, Ifosfamide is not recommended.
Ifosfamide and metabolites are dialyzable.

Hepatic Impairment:
Extensively hepatically metabolized to both active and inactive metabolites. Use with caution.
For patients with bilirubin >3 mg/dL, administer 25% of dose.

Obesity:
Utilize patient’s actual body weight (full weight) for calculation of body surface area-based or weight-based dosing,
particularly when the intent of therapy is curative. If a dose reduction is utilized due to toxicity, consider resumption
of full weight-based dosing with subsequent cycles, once cause of toxicity is resolved.

Indications

Treatment of germ cell testicular cancer (thirdline), lymphoma, sarcoma, solid tumors

Contraindications

Urinary outflow obstruction; severe renal and hepatic impairment

Precautions

HIGH-ALERT MEDICATION
The Institute for Safe Medication Practices (ISMP) includes this drug in the list of drug classes with a heightened risk
of causing significant patient harm when used in error.

WARNING: Nephrotoxicity can be severe and may result in renal failure. Myelosuppression is increased with single
high doses and decreased renal function. Severe myelosuppression may occur when administered in combination
with other chemotherapy agents or radiation therapy. Antimicrobial prophylaxis may be necessary in some
neutropenic patients. CNS toxicities can be severe and may result in encephalopathy and death. Hemorrhagic cystitis
can be severe and can be reduced by the prophylactic use of mesna.

WARNING: Hazardous agent; use appropriate precautions for handling and disposal.

Drug Interactions

NOTE: Ifosfamide is a substrate of CYP2B6 (major), CYP2C19 (minor), CYP2C8 (minor), CYP2C9 (minor), CYP3A4 (minor).

Monitor closely with:
Decreases metabolism of Ifosfamide: CYP2B6 Inhibitors
Enhances therapeutic effect of Ifosfamide: Trastuzumab (neutropenic effect)
Enhances therapeutic effect of Vitamin K Antagonists, e.g., Warfarin (anticoagulant effect)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Ifosfamide: Allopurinol (bone marrow suppression), Busulfan
(hemorrhagic cystitis), Cisplatin, Denosumab (serious infections)
Reduces diagnostic effect of Coccidioides immitis Skin Test

Avoid concomitant use with:
Decreases serum concentration of Ifosfamide: Dabrafenib
Enhances therapeutic effect of Ifosfamide: Roflumilast (immunosuppressive effect)
Enhances therapeutic effect of Tofacitinib (immunosuppressive effect)
Increases metabolism of Ifosfamide: CYP2B6 Inducers
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Ifosfamide: Dipyrone (agranulocytosis, pancytopenia), Pimecrolimus,
Tacrolimus (Topical)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following
drugs: Clozapine (agranulocytosis), Leflunomide (hematologic toxicity such as pancytopenia, agranulocytosis, and/or
thrombocytopenia), Natalizumab (concurrent infection), Vaccines (Live)
Reduces therapeutic effect of Ifosfamide: Echinacea
Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs: BCG, Vaccines (Inactivated) [complete all ageappropriate
vaccinations at least 2 weeks prior to starting Ifosfamide; if vaccinated during Ifosfamide therapy, revaccinate at
least 3 months after discontinuation], Vaccines (Live) [do not administer vaccines for at least 3 months after
Ifosfamide therapy]

Mechanism of Action

A nitrogen mustard analogue structurally related to cyclophosphamide and is inhibitory to protein synthesis and DNA
synthesis by causing crosslinking of DNA strands and by binding with nucleic acids and other intracellular structures.
