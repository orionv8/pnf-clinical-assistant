Dosage

ORAL › 10 mg tablet (Rx)

Acute mania or mixed mania, by mouth, ADULT, initially 10–15 mg once daily, slowly titrate dose based on efficacy
and tolerability (maximum, 30 mg once daily), CHILD =10 years, initially 2 mg once daily for 2 days, increase to 5 mg
once daily for 2 days, further increase to a target dose of 10 mg once daily, slowly titrate dose in 5 mg increments
based on efficacy and tolerability (maximum, 30 mg once daily). Autism-related irritability, by mouth, CHILD =6
years, initially 2 mg once daily for 7 days, then increase to 5 mg once daily, slowly titrate dose in 5 mg increments at
intervals of =1 week based on efficacy and tolerability (maximum, 15 mg once daily). Major depressive disorder,
adjunct, by mouth, ADULT, initially 2–5 mg once daily, may increase by 5 mg daily at intervals of =1 week based on
efficacy and tolerability (maximum, 15 mg once daily). Schizophrenia, by mouth, ADULT, initially 10–15 mg once daily,
slowly titrate dose based on efficacy and tolerability (maximum, 30 mg once daily); CHILD =13 years, initially 2 mg
once daily, slowly titrate dose based on efficacy and tolerability (maximum, 30 mg once daily). Tourette syndrome,
by mouth, CHILD =6 years or <50 kg, initially 2 mg once daily for 2 days, then increase to a target dose of 5 mg once
daily, slowly titrate dose at intervals of no less than 1 week based on efficacy and tolerability (maximum, 10 mg once
daily); assess the need for ongoing treatment periodically; CHILD =50 kg, initially 2 mg once daily for 2 days, then
increase to 5 mg once daily for 5 days with a target dose of 10 mg once daily on day 8, slowly titrate dose in 5 mg
increments at intervals of no less than 1 week based on efficacy and tolerability (maximum, 20 mg once daily).

Administration

ORAL › 10 mg tablet (Rx)

May be taken with or without food Orally disintegrating tablets must be removed from the foil blister by peeling
back. Do NOT push the tablet through the foil. Place tablet in mouth immediately upon removal. Tablet dissolves
rapidly in saliva and may be swallowed without liquid. If needed, can be taken with liquid. Do NOT split the tablet.
NOTE: Do NOT discontinue abruptly because of the possibility of withdrawal and rebound psychosis. Discontinue
gradually to avoid physical withdrawal symptoms, including anorexia, anxiety, diaphoresis, diarrhea, dizziness,
dyskinesia, headache, myalgia, nausea, paresthesia, restlessness, tremulousness, and vomiting.

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric:
May tolerate lower doses better. Antipsychotics are considered potentially inappropriate in the elderly. Avoid in
patients 65 years and older with dementia due to an increased risk of mortality, cerebrovascular accidents (stroke), a
greater rate of cognitive decline with use, and potential to cause or exacerbate or hyponatremia unless all
nonpharmacologic options have failed or are not possible or the patient is threatening substantial harm to self or to
others.

Indications

Management of Schizophrenia; acute mania or mixed mania; adjunct treatment of major depressive disorder; bipolar
disorder; autism-related irritability; Tourette syndrome.

Precautions

WARNING: Elderly patients with dementia-related psychosis are at an increased risk of death. Aripiprazole is NOT
approved for the treatment of dementia-related psychosis.

Closely monitor for clinical worsening and for the emergence of suicidal thoughts and behaviors.

Cardiovascular disease; Parkinson disease (may aggravate motor disturbances); seizures.

Elderly (may be at increased risk of seizures; increased risk of mortality and cerebrovascular accidents; potential to
cause or exacerbate SIADH or hyponatremia); children (increased risk of suicidal thoughts and behavior; safety and
efficacy have not been established).

Pregnancy (risk of abnormal muscle movements and withdrawal symptoms in newborns with use during the third
trimester); lactation (excreted in breastmilk; monitor infant for possible adverse effects).

SKILLED TASKS. May impair ability to perform tasks, which require mental alertness, like driving or operating
machinery.

TEST INTERACTION. May give false positive urine immunoassay screening tests for amphetamines.

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Aripiprazole: Antidepressants (serotonergic effect) Antihypertensives CNS
Depressants (central effects)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Aripiprazole: Lithium (neurotoxic effect) Methylphenidate,
Metoclopramide (sleepiness; dizziness)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Acetylcholinesterase Inhibitors (extrapyramidal
symptoms) Antidepressants (serotonin syndrome; neuroleptic malignant syndrome) Iohexol, Iomeprol, Iopamidol
(seizures) Mequitazine (arrhythmogenic effect) Serotonin Modulators (serotonin syndrome; neuroleptic malignant
syndrome) SSRIs (psychomotor impairment) Methylphenidate
Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs: Amphetamines, CNS Stimulants (stimulant effect) Antidiabetics,
Dopamine Agonists Guanethidine (antihypertensive effect)

Avoid concomitant use with:
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Aripiprazole: Other Antipsychotics (increased side effect burden, high
dose prescribing, increased hospitalization rates and length of stay, higher treatment costs, and increased mortality)

Mechanism of Action

Aripiprazole is a quinolinone derivative and an atypical antipsychotic agent, which acts as a partial agonist at
dopamine D2 and serotonin 5-HT21A receptors and as an antagonist at 5-HT22A receptors.
