Dosage

IV › 250 mg/mL, 2 mL ampule (Rx)

Idiopathic methemoglobinemia, by mouth, ADULT, 300-600 mg daily in divided doses. Urinary acidification, by
mouth, ADULT, 4-12 mg daily in 3-4 divided doses; CHILD, 500 mg every 6-8 hours.

Administration

IV › 250 mg/mL, 2 mL ampule (Rx)

Avoid rapid IV injection as this may cause temporary faintness or dizziness. See Ascorbic Acid (Vitamin C) under
Vitamins in Chapter 1: Alimentary Tract and Metabolism for other information.

Indications

Treatment of methemoglobinemia; urinary acidification

Contraindications

Anemia, breastfeeding, diabetes mellitus, G6PD deficiency, hemochromatosis, nephrolithiasis, pregnancy,
sideroblastic anemia, sodium restriction, thalassemia

Mechanism of Action

A water-soluble vitamin that acts as a free radical, an antioxidant scavenger, and plays a major role in oxidation-
reduction reactions; Ascorbic acid is a cofactor for enzymes involved in the biosynthesis of collagen, carnitine, and
neurotransmitters.
