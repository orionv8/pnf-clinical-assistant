Administration

UNASSIGNED › 652 mg/mL (equivalent to 320 mg iodine) solution for injection, 50 mL vial (Rx)

For adults, may be a vesicant; ensure proper needle or catheter placement prior to and during infusion; avoid
infiltration For children, intravenous or intra-arterial; administer without further dilution; warm to temperature or
body temperature prior to administration NOTE: Not for intrathecal use; inadvertent administration may cause
death,convulsions/seizures, cerebral hemorrhage, coma, paralysis, arachnoiditis, acute renal failure, cardiac
arrest, rhabdomyolysis, hyperthermia, and brain edema; patients should be adequately hydrated priorto and
following iodixanol administration

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric:
No dose adjustments provided in themanufacturer’s labeling;
use caution in severe impairment and in setting of combined renal and hepatic disease

Pediatric:
The concentration and volume of iodixanol to be used should be individualized depending upon age, weight, size of
vessel, and rate of blood flow within the vessel.

Indications

For use in adult for cardioangiography, conventional cerebral angiography, peripheral angiography (conventional),
abdominal angiography, urography, venography, CT enhancement & studies of the gastrointestinal (GI) tract & for use
in children for cardioangiography, urography, CT enhancement & studies of the GI tract; lumbar, thoracic & cervical
myelography; arthrography & hysterosalpingography

Contraindications

Intrathecal administration;
hypersensitivity to iodixanol or any component of the formulation

Precautions

Patients with history of previous reaction to contrast dye, iodine-based contrast media or patients with known
clinical hypersensitivity (bronchial asthma, hay fever, food allergies)

Patients with severe cutaneous adverse reactions (including Stevens-Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal
necrolysis, acute generalized exanthematous pustulosis, drug reaction with eosinophilia and systemic symptoms

Patients with hepatic impairment administered with an oral cholecystographic agent followed by an intravascular
iodinated radiocontrast agent

Patients with cardiovascular disease and heart failure receiving concurrent diuretic therapy

Patients with hyperthyroidism or with an autonomously functioning thyroid nodule; use caution in severe
thyrotoxicosis

Patients with multiple myeloma or other paraproteinacious disease; pheochromocytoma; chronic pulmonary
emphysema

Patients with homozygous sickle cell disease Serious, rarely fatal, thromboembolic events causing myocardial
infarction and stroke in angiographic procedures with both ionic and nonionic contrast media

Avoid extravasation; may result in tissue necrosis and/or compartment syndrome, particularly in patients with
severe arterial or venous disease

Avoid fluid restrictions in patients with acute renal failure, preexisting renal impairment, multiple myeloma,
advanced vascular disease, and diabetes

Avoid angiography in patients with homocystinuria

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
discomfort at injection site, pain at injection site, warm sensation at injection site

Less Common:
angina pectoris, chest pain; headache, migraine, vertigo, altered sense of smell, paresthesia; pruritis, erythema, skin
rash; dysgeusia

Drug Interactions

Avoid concomitant use with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of the following drugs:
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Iodixanol:
Aldesleukin (allergic or hypersensitivity reactions to iodinated contrast agents)

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs:
MetFORMIN (renal dysfunction)

Mechanism of Action

Iodixanol is an x-ray contrast medium that contains about 49.1% of Iodine. It is a nonionic dimeric iodinated
radiographic contrast medium, and it is isoosmolar with blood.
