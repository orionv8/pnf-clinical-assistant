Dosage

RESPIR › Inhalation: Nasal Aqueous Solution: 0.05%/dose (as propionate) x 120 doses (OTC)

Asthma prophylaxis, by aerosol inhalation, ADULT, 100– 200 micrograms 1–2 times daily in mild asthma; up to 500–
1,000 micrograms twice daily in severe cases; CHILD 4–16 years, 50–100 micrograms twice daily, increased to 200
micrograms twice daily, if necessary. Severe chronic asthma, by inhalation of nebulized solution, ADULT, 500–2,000
micrograms twice daily CHILD 4–16 years, 1,000 micrograms twice daily.

Administration

RESPIR › Inhalation: Nasal Aqueous Solution: 0.05%/dose (as propionate) x 120 doses (OTC)

Administer twice daily every day by the orally inhaled route only. After inhalation, rinse the mouth with water and
spit out the water without swallowing.

Dose Adjustment

No information found

Indications

Prophylaxis of asthma

Contraindications

Prophylaxis of asthma

Precautions

Patient with active or latent TB; Fungal, bacterial or systemic viral infection; Cataracts and/or glaucoma; Untreated
localized infection of the nasal mucosa; Untreated cutaneous infection, rosacea, acne vulgaris, perioral dermatitis,
perianal or genital pruritus, pruritus without inflammation; Hepatic impairment; Avoid abrupt withdrawal when
switching from systemic to oral or orally inhaled corticosteroid; Children; Pregnancy and lactation.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common and Less Common: Eosinophilic conditions, adrenal insufficiency, growth retardation, Cushing's syndrome,
decreased bone density, cataract, glaucoma, paradoxical bronchospasm (potentially fatal)

Rare: Psychological effects

Drug Interactions

Increases serum concentration of Fluticasone: CYP3A4 Inhibitors e.g. Ritonavir

Mechanism of Action

A glucocorticoid with a fluorocarbothioate ester linkage at the 17-carbon position. It has potent vasoconstrictive and
anti-inflammatory activity, but weak hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) inhibitory effect when applied topically.
