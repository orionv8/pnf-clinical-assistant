Technical Specifications PNF Primary Care Reserved AMs

IV INFUSION › 500 mL (Rx)

Composition: Concentration: 5–8%, 5–10% Protein: 35–50 g; rich in branched-chain amino acids Calories: 50–200
Electrolytes variable

Dosage

IV INFUSION › 500 mL (Rx)

NOTE: Individualize dose based on patient’s needs. Adjust dose based on a patient’s age, symptoms, and body
weight. Hepatic encephalopathy, by drip IV infusion, ADULT, 500 to 1,000 mL per dose at a rate of 500 mL over 180–
300 minutes (about 25–40 drops/minute).

Administration

IV INFUSION › 500 mL (Rx)

Administer by slow IV infusion. 500–1,000 mL is reconstitue with a dextrose solution and administered over 24
hours via a central vein. See General Information on Amino Acid Solutions listed above for other information.

Dose Adjustment

Pregnancy:
Adjust dose to achieve a reduction to less than 5,000 IU of vitamin A daily in pregnant women of gestational month of
3 months or earlier or women who have an intention to become pregnant.

Indications

Management of hepatic encephalopathy in patients with acute and chronic liver diseases

Contraindications

Severe renal disorder; abnormal amino acid metabolism other than hepatic disorders

Precautions

Severe acidosis; Congestive heart failure; Elderly and children; Pregnancy (may cause cranial neural crest and other
malformations in newborns).

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
Acidosis, nausea, vomiting, chest discomfort, palpitation, chills, fever, headache, vascular pain

Rare:
Skin eruptions

Mechanism of Action

Management of hepatic failure in patients with acute and chronic liver diseases. Helpful in patients under
hypercatabolic state with negative nitrogen balance due to increased skeletal muscle breakdown.

Pregnancy & Remarks

PREGNANCY CATEGORY

Not classified
