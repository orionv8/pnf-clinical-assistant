Dosage

ORAL › 100 mg tablet (Rx)

NOTE: Use the lowest effective dose for the shortest duration of time, consistent with individual patient goals, to
reduce risk adverse events. Avoid long-term administration. Transition to oral therapy to oral therapy after initial
improvement. Pain, by IM injection, ADULT, 50–100 mg daily in 1 or 2 divided doses; may administer up to 100 mg
twice daily for severe pain (maximum, 200 mg in 24 hours for up to 3 days).

To be administered by deep IM injection into the gluteal muscle every 4 hours.

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric:
Start at lower end of dosing range. Use with caution.

Renal and Hepatic Impairment:
Use with caution in mild-to-moderate impairment. Use is contraindicated in severe impairment.

Indications

Management of painful conditions, including rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, acute gout,
and inflammation or pain following surgery.

Contraindications

Perioperative pain in coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery; active peptic ulcer or cerebrovascular bleeding;
history of NSAID-induced GI bleeding or perforation; concomitant use with high-dose methotrexate (=15 mg/week) or
ciprofloxacin; severe edema; severe hepatic or renal impairment; use in third trimester of pregnancy; use in
children.

Precautions

WARNING: Cardiovascular risks. NSAIDs are associated with an increased risk of adverse cardiovascular thrombotic
events, including fatal MI and stroke. Use is contraindicated for the treatment of perioperative pain in CABG surgery.
Gastrointestinal events. NSAIDs may increase risk of GI irritation, inflammation, ulceration, bleeding, and perforation.

History of GI disease, e.g. bleeding or ulcer; Hypertension; Renal or hepatic impairment; Elderly (at greater risk of
serious GI events, hyperkalemia, and renal toxicity);

Pregnancy (crosses the placenta; cardiovascular anomalies and cleft palate following use in the first trimester;
myocardial degenerative changes, prenatal constriction of the ductus arteriosus, fetal tricuspid regurgitation,
failure of the ductus arteriosus to close postnatally following use in the third trimester);

Lactation (excreted in breastmilk).

SKILLED TASKS. May impair ability to perform tasks, which require mental alertness, like driving or operating
machinery.

TEST INTERACTION. May interfere with urine tests that rely on acid precipitation as an endpoint or on color reactions
for carbonyl groups, including detection of albumin, bile salts, 17ketosteroids or 17hydroxycorticosteroids in urine.

Mechanism of Action

Reversibly inhibits COX-1 and COX-2 resulting in decreased formation of prostaglandin precursors and induces a
decrease of prostaglandin synthesis in CNS and PNS. It also inhibits lipoxygenase, resulting in a decreased
leukotriene production and decreased polymorphonuclear neutrophil activation.
