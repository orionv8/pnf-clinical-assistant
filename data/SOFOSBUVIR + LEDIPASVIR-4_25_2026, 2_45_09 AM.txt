Contraindications

Hypersensitivity to any component of the formulation.

Precautions

Hepatitis B virus reactivation has been reported in HBsAg positive patients and in patients with serologic evidence
of resolved HBV infection.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Headache, fatigue, and weakness.

Less Common: Irritability, insomnia, dizziness, depression, nausea, diarrhea, hyperbilirubinemia, myalgia, increased
creatine phosphokinase, cough, and dyspnea.

Rare: Angioedema, reactivation of HBV, and skin rash.

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Increased toxicity due to Sofosbuvir/Ledipasvir:
Amiodarone, Warfarin

Avoid concomitant use with:
Increases the level/ effect of Colchicine

Decreases levels of Sofosbuvir/Ledipasvir:
Carbamazepine, Oxcarbamazepine, Phenobarbital, Phenytoin, Rifabutin, And Rifampin

Mechanism of Action

Ledipasvir inhibits the HCV NS5A protein necessary for viral replication; sofosbuvir inhibits NS5B RNA-dependent
RNA polymerase essential for viral replication, and acts as a chain terminator.
