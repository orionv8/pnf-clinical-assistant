Precautions

May cause lactic acidosis, hepatomegaly, renal toxicity, hepatitis B acute exacerbation;
Use is not recommended in patients with hepatic and renal impairment, HIV-1 and HBV co-infection.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Headache and decreased bone mineral density.
Less Common: Fatigue, skin rash, glycosuria, abdominal pain, nausea, diarrhea, dyspepsia, flatulence, vomiting, back
pain, arthralgia, cough

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Increases the level or effect of Tenofovir:
Daclatasvir, Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir

Mechanism of Action

The active moiety of Tenofovir alafenamide inhibits
replication of HBV by inhibiting HBV polymerase.
