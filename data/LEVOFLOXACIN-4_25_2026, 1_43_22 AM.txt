Dosage

ORAL › [Access] 250 mg tablet (Rx)

Note: Sequential therapy (IV to oral) may be instituted based on prescriber's discretion. Sepsis, intra-abdominal
source, by IV infusion, ADULT, 750 mg every 24 hours with metronidazole. Febrile neutropenia, low risk, by mouth,
ADULT, 750 mg with amoxicillin-clavulanic acid until patient is afebrile and absolute neutrophil count >500cells/μL.
Antibacterial prophylaxis, by mouth, by IV infusion, ADULT, 500-750 mg. Osteomyelitis, hematogenous, vertebral,
MRSA likely, by IV infusion, ADULT, 750 mg every 24 hours with vancomycin; MRSA unlikely, by IV infusion, ADULT,
750 mg every 24 hours with oxacillin. Osteomyelitis, foot bone, by mouth, ADULT, 750 mg every 24 hours. Bacterial
purulent pericarditis, S. aureus, Group A Streptococcus, Enterobacteriaceae, S. pneumoniae, by IV infusion,
ADULT, 750 mg every 24 hours with vancomycin. Acute epiglottitis, by IV infusion, ADULT, 750 mg every 24 hours
with clindamycin. Acute sinusitis, clinical failure after 3 days, by mouth, ADULT, 500 mg for 5 days; by IV infusion,
ADULT, 750 mg for 7-10 days. Acute otitis media (AOM), with anaphylaxis, by mouth, ADULT, 750 mg every 24 hours
for 5 days. Acute otitis media, clinical failure after 3 days, severe penicillin allergy, by mouth, ADULT, 750 mg twice
daily for 5 days. Acute mastoiditis, by IV infusion, ADULT, 750 mg daily. Acute bacterial exacerbation of chronic
bronchitis (ABECB), severe, by mouth, ADULT, 500 mg every 24 hours for 5-10 days. Acute bacterial exacerbation
(bronchiectasis), by mouth, ADULT, 500 mg every 24 hours for 7-10 days. Community-acquired pneumonia,
moderate risk, by mouth, ADULT, 750 mg with ampicillin-sulbactam/ cefuroxime sodium/ ceftriaxone for 7-28 days;
High risk, no risk for P. aeruginosa, by IV infusion, ADULT, 750 mg with ceftriaxone or ertapenem; High risk, risk for
P. aeruginosa, by IV infusion, ADULT, 750 mg with gentamicin/amikacin and azithromycin and piperacillin-
tazobactam/ cefepime/ meropenem. Hospital-acquired pneumonia, high risk of mortality and with risk factor for
MDR, by IV infusion, ADULT, 750 mg with vancomycin/linezolid and piperacillin-tazobactam/ cefepime/ meropenem
for 7 days or longer. Ventilator-associated pneumonia, with risk factors for MRD VAP and with structural lung
disease, by IV infusion, ADULT, 750 mg with vancomycin/linezolid and piperacillin-tazobactam/ cefepime/
meropenem/ aztreonam for 7 days or longer. Pathogen-specific treatment, P. aeruginosa, by IV infusion, ADULT,
750 mg with amikacin OR gentamicin; Klebsiella pneumoniae, by IV infusion, ADULT, 750 mg every 24 hours.

Administration

ORAL › [Access] 250 mg tablet (Rx)

For oral: Tablets may be administered without regard to meals. Oral solution should be administered at least 1 hour
before or 2 hours after meals. Maintain adequate hydration of patient to prevent crystalluria. Administer at least 2
hours before or 2 hours after antacids containing magnesium or aluminum, sucralfate, metal cations (e.g. iron),
multivitamin preparations with zinc, or didanosine chewable/buffered tablets or the pediatric powder for solution.
For IV: Infuse 250 to 500 mg IV solution over 60 minutes; infuse 750 mg IV solution over 90 minutes. Too rapid of
infusion can lead to hypotension. Avoid administration through an intravenous line with a solution containing
multivalent cations (eg, magnesium, calcium). Maintain adequate hydration of patient to prevent crystalluria

Mechanism of Action

Quinolones are broad-spectrum antibacterial agents. They act by blocking bacterial DNA synthesis by inhibiting
bacterial topoisomerase II and topoisomerase IV.
Inhibition of topoisomerase II prevents the relaxation of positively supercoiled DNA while inhibition of
topoisomerase IV interferes with separation of replicated chromosomal DNA.
