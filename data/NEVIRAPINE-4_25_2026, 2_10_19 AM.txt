Dosage

Administration

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric: Refer to adult dosing.

Renal Impairment: Patients requiring dialysis: Administer additional 200 mg dose after each dialysis treatment.

Hepatic Impairment: Moderate to severe: Not recommended for use.

Indications

In combination therapy with other antiretroviral agents for the treatment of HIV-1.

Contraindications

Moderate-to-severe hepatic impairment;
use in occupational or non-occupational post-exposure prophylaxis regimen

Precautions

Patients taking nevirapine should be closely monitored for adverse skin reactions and hepatotoxicity during the first
18 weeks of treatment, extra vigilance is advised during the first 6 weeks of treatment. Use with extreme caution in
patients with moderate hepatic impairment, contraindicated in those with severe hepatic impairment, Treatment
should be permanently stopped in patients who suffer a severe rash, rash accompanied by constitutional symptoms,
hypersensitivity reactions or clinical hepatitis.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Rash
Less common: Hypersensitivity reactions
Rare: Hepatitis, hepatic failure, SJS, and toxic epidermal necrolysis.

Drug Interactions

Avoid concomitant use with:
Decreases serum concentration of the following drugs:
Aripiprazole, Caspofungin, Contraceptives, Dolutegravir, Fosamprenavir, Indinavir, Itraconazole, Ketoconazole,
Lopinavir, Voriconazole

Decreases serum concentration of Nevirapine:
Carbamazepine, Dabrafenib, Mitotane, Rifampin

Increases serum concentration of the following drugs:
Rilpivirine, Tizanidine

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Efavirenz

Increases metabolism of Nevirapine:
CYP2B6 Inducers (Strong), CYP3A4 Inducers (Strong)

Increases metabolism of Methadone
