Dosage

ORAL › 250 mg capsule (Rx)

2nd line treatment for uncomplicated malaria due to P. falciparum or P. malariae (in combination with quinine
sulfate, 10 mg/kg every 8 hours for 7 days; and primaquine tablet, 15 mg (0.25 mg base per kg) on day 1 and 3 tablets
for adults >60 kg), by mouth, ADULT, 3 mg/kg once daily for 7 days. Severe malaria due to P. falciparum or P.
malariae, second_x0002_line treatment (in combination with artemether_x0002_lumefantrine), by mouth, ADULT,
3 mg/kg once daily for 7 days.

Administration

ORAL › 250 mg capsule (Rx)

Should be given on an empty stomach (1 hour prior to, or 2 hours after meals) to increase total absorption.
Administer at least 1–2 hours prior to, or 4 hours after antacid because aluminum and magnesium cations may
chelate with tetracycline and reduce its total absorption. Administer around-the-clock to promote less variation in
peak and trough serum levels. See Tetracycline under Antibacterials for Systemic Use – Tetracyclines in Chapter 7:
Antiinfectives for Systemic Use for other information.

Dose Adjustment

Renal Impairment: If CrCl is 50–80 mL/minute, administer every 8–12 hours. If CrCl is 10–50 mL/minute, administer
every 12–24 hours. If CrCl <10 mL/minute, administer every 24 hours.

Hepatic Impairment: Use with caution. Maximum dose is 1 g daily.

Indications

For chloroquine-resistant falciparum malaria (in combination with other antimalarials)

Mechanism of Action

A tetracycline antibiotic that inhibits bacterial synthesis by binding with the 30s ribosomal subunit(s) of susceptible
microorganisms.
