DEXTROMETHORPHAN

Drug Classification

ATC CODE

R05DA09

ANATOMICAL

Respiratory System

THERAPEUTIC

Cough and Cold Preparations

PHARMACOLOGICAL

Cough Suppressants, Excluding Combinations with Expectorants

Technical Specifications PNF Primary Care Reserved AMs

ORAL › 10 mg tablet (as hydrobromide) (OTC)

Dosage

ORAL › 10 mg tablet (as hydrobromide) (OTC)

Cough suppressant, by mouth, ADULT and CHILD >12 years, 10–20 mg every 4 hours or 30 mg every 6–8 hours
(maximum 120 mg daily); CHILD 6–12 years, 5–10 mg every 4 hours or 15 mg every 6–8 hours (maximum 60 mg every
24 hours). NOTE: NOT for use in children <4 years of age.

Administration

ORAL › 10 mg tablet (as hydrobromide) (OTC)

May be taken with or without food.

Dose Adjustment

Hepatic Impairment:
Use with caution. Metabolized in the liver.

Indications

Symptomatic relief of coughs caused by the common cold or inhaled irritants

Contraindications

Known hypersensitivity to dextromethorphan or any component of the formulation; concurrent administration with
or within 2 weeks of discontinuing an MAO inhibitor

Precautions

Serotonin syndrome (symptoms of agitation, confusion, hallucinations, hyperreflexia, myoclonus, shivering, and
tachycardia may occur with concomitant pro serotonergic drugs); Elderly (no specific information found on use in
elderly); children; debilitated patients

Adverse Drug Reactions

Confusion, excitement, irritability, nervousness, serotonin syndrome

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with: Enhances therapeutic effect of Dextromethorphan: Antiemetics, 5-HT3 Antagonists
(serotonergic effect)

Enhances therapeutic effect of Antipsychotic Agents (dopamine blockade; serotonergic effect)

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of
Dextromethorphan:
Antiemetics, 5-HT3 Antagonists (serotonin syndrome)

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs:
Antipsychotic Agents (neuroleptic malignant syndrome; serotonin syndrome)
Memantine, Metoclopramide (neuroleptic malignant syndrome; serotonin syndrome), Parecoxib, Peginterferon Alfa-
2b

Avoid concomitant use with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Dextromethorphan:
MAO Inhibitors e.g. Phenelzine (serotonergic effect), Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors [except Fluvoxamine]
(serotonergic effect)

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of
Dextromethorphan:
Dapoxetine, MAO Inhibitors e.g. Phenelzine (serotonin syndrome), Other Serotonin Modulators [except Tedizolid]
(serotonin syndrome)

Increases serum concentration of Dextromethorphan:
Abiraterone Acetate, CYP2D6 Inhibitors, Panobinostat, Quinidine, Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors [except
Fluvoxamine]

TEST INTERACTIONS: False-positive phencyclidine, opioids
and heroin urine drug screen.

Mechanism of Action

An N-methyl-D-aspartate receptor antagonist that decreases the sensitivity of cough receptors and interrupts
cough impulse transmission by depressing the medullary cough center through sigma receptor stimulation
