Dosage

ORAL › 25 mg tablet (Rx)

Toxoplasmosis treatment (in combination with a sulfonamide and leucovorin calcium), by mouth, ADULT, 50–75 mg
daily for 1–3 weeks depending on patient's tolerance and response, then dose may be reduced by 50% and
continue for 4–5 weeks, with 1–4 g sulfadiazine daily; CHILD, initially 1 mg/kg daily divided into 2 equal daily doses
for 2 to 4 days, may decrease dose to 0.5 mg/kg daily divided into 2 doses for 4 weeks, with usual pediatric dose of
sulfadiazine.

Administration

ORAL › 25 mg tablet (Rx)

Administer with meals to minimize GI distress.

Indications

A diaminopyrimidine that inhibits parasitic dihydrofolate reductase, resulting in inhibition of vital tetrahydrofolic acid
synthesis. It is active against pre-erythrocytic forms and is also a slow-acting schizontocide.

Contraindications

Megaloblastic anemia secondary to folate deficiency; pregnancy; lactation

Precautions

Hematologic effects (most commonly with high doses; monitor CBC and platelets twice weekly in high-dose therapy);
Folate deficiency (use with caution); G6PD deficiency (use with caution); renal impairment; hepatic impairment;
Seizure disorders (use with caution).
Pregnancy (adverse events have been observed in animal reproduction studies; supplementation of folate is
strongly recommended); lactation (excreted in breast milk; may result in significant systemic concentrations in
breastfed infants).

Adverse Drug Reactions

Arrhythmias (large doses), rash, anorexia, atrophic glossitis, vomiting, leukopenia, megaloblastic anemia,
pancytopenia, pulmonary eosinophilia, thrombocytopenia, hematuria, anaphylaxis
Potentially Fatal: Cutaneous reactions (e.g., erythema multiforme, Stevens-Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal
necrolysis (with sulfadoxine at large doses)

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Bone marrow suppression: Proguanil, Sulfonamides
e.g. Co-trimoxazole, Zidovudine, Cytostatic Agents Co-trimoxazole (pancytopenia; megaloblastic anemia),
Lorazepam (hepatotoxicity)

Reduces therapeutic effect of Pyrimethamine: Methylfolate

Avoid concomitant use with:
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Pyrimethamine: Artemether

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Dapsone (hemolytic reactions), Lumefantrine

Mechanism of Action

A diaminopyrimidine that inhibits parasitic dihydrofolate reductase, resulting in inhibition of vital tetrahydrofolic acid
synthesis. It is active against pre-erythrocytic forms and is also a slow-acting schizontocide.
