Dosage

100 mg + 224 mg capsule (Rx)

Colorectal cancer, metastatic, by mouth, ADULT, in combination with oral leucovorin calcium 300 mg/m2 tegafur +
672 mg/m2 uracil daily in 3 divided doses, or every 8 hours on days 1 through 28 every 35 days, begin subsequent
cycles after a 7-day break. Head and neck, gastric, colorectal, hepatocellular, hepatobiliary, pancreatic, lung,
breast, and bladder cancer, by mouth, ADULT, 300–600 mg tegafur component daily in 2–3 divided doses. Uterine or
cervical cancers, by mouth, ADULT, 600 mg tegafur component daily in 2–3 divided doses. Dose based on BSA,
number of tegafur + uracil (UFT) capsules required: <1.17 BSA, 3 UFT capsules daily 1.17-1.49 BSA, 4 UFT capsules
daily 1.5-1.83 BSA, 5 UFT capsules daily >1.83 BSA, 6 UFT capsules daily

Administration

100 mg + 224 mg capsule (Rx)

To be taken at least 1 hour before or 1 hour after meals. NOTE: Do not make up doses that are withheld during the
28 days of consecutive treatment. If tegafur and uracil treatment is withheld, leucovorin calcium should also be
withheld. Do NOT reduce leucovorin calcium dose if tegafur and uracil dose is reduced.

Dose Adjustment

Obesity:
Use actual body weight (full weight) for calculation of BSAbased or weight-based dosing, especially if the intent of
therapy is curative.
If dose reduction is done due to toxicity, consider resumption of full weight-based dosing with subsequent cycles,
especially if cause of toxicity is resolved.

Dihydropyrimidine dehydrodeganase (DPD) mutations:
Genetic DPD deficiency of has been associated with prolonged clearance and increased and potentially fatal toxicity,
e.g., diarrhea, neutropenia, neurotoxicity.

Indications

First-line treatment for metastatic colorectal cancer, in combination with leucovorin calcium; treatment of head and
neck, gastric, colorectal, hepatocellular, hepatobiliary, lung, pancreatic, breast, bladder, and uterine or cervical
cancers

Contraindications

Women who are or may become pregnant; breastfeeding; infants, children, or adolescents; severe hepatic
impairment; evidence of bone marrow suppression from prior radiation therapy or prior chemotherapy; known
deficiency of hepatic CYP2A6; known or suspected dihydropyrimidine dehydrogenase (DPD) deficiency; concurrent
or recent treatment with DPD inhibitors; concurrent use, or within 7 days of tegafur + gimeracil + oteracil

Precautions

WARNING: Hazardous agent. Use appropriate precautions for handling and disposal.

Bone marrow suppression (e.g. anemia, leukopenia, neutropenia, thrombocytopenia; may require dosage
modification; monitor blood counts as necessary)

Cardiovascular effects (e.g. myocardial infarction, ischemia, angina; use with caution in patients with history of
significant cardiac disease)

Gastrointestinal toxicity (e.g. milddiarrhea, nausea, vomiting, anorexia, stomatitis, and abdominal pain; withhold
treatment for grade 2 or higher diarrhea)

Hepatotoxicity (e.g. fatal fulminant hepatitis, hepatic cirrhosis; long-term administration may lead to cirrhosis
without remarkable increase in transaminases; discontinue if prolonged prothrombin time or decreased albumin
occurs).

Fertility effects (both men and women of childbearing potential should use effective contraception during and for 3
months following cessation of treatment; may cause irreversible infertility).

Pregnancy (may cause fetal harm)

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Tegafur + Uracil: Trastuzumab (neutropenic effect)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Tegafur + Uracil: Denosumab (serious infections), Leucovorin
CalciumLevo-leucovorin, Methotrexate
Reduces diagnostic effect of Coccidioides immitis Skin Test

Avoid concomitant use with:
Decreases serum concentration of Tegafur + Uracil: Moderate and Strong [active metabolites of tegafur] (inhibits
conversion of tegafur to 5-fluorouracil)
Enhances therapeutic effect of Tegafur + Uracil: Roflumilast (immunosuppressive effect)
Enhances therapeutic effect of Tofacitinib (immunosuppressive effect)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Tegafur + Uracil: Dipyrone (agranulocytosis, pancytopenia),
Pimecrolimus, Tacrolimus (Topical)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Clozapine (agranulocytosis), Leflunomide
(hematologic toxicity such as pancytopenia, agranulocytosis, and/or thrombocytopenia), Natalizumab (concurrent
infection)
Increases serum concentration of Tegafur + Uracil: Gimeracil [active metabolites of tegafur, specifically fluorouracil]
Increases serum concentration of the following drugs: Diclofenac (Systemic), Fosphenytoin, Phenytoin, Vitamin K
Antagonists, e.g., Warfarin
Reduces therapeutic effect of Tegafur + Uracil: Allopurinol, Echinacea
Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs: BCG (Intravesical), Vaccines (live virus vaccines may progress to
infection)

Mechanism of Action

Tegafur is a prodrug of fluorouracil. Uracil is an inhibitor of the enzyme dihydropyrimidine dehydrogenase, which is
responsible for the degradation of fluorouracil.
