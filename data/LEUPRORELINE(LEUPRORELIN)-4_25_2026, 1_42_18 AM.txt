Dosage

IM / SC › 1.88 mg powder (as acetate), single-dose vial with syringe (Rx)

Prostate cancer, advanced, by IM or SC depot injection, ADULT, administer 3.75 mg once a month or 11.25 mg every 3
months. Endometriosis; uterine leiomyomata (fibroids), by IM or SC depot injection, ADULT, administer 3.75 mg
once a month or 11.25 mg every 3 months, for up to 6 months.

Administration

IM / SC › 1.88 mg powder (as acetate), single-dose vial with syringe (Rx)

For IM administration, administer as a single injection to the gluteal area, anterior thigh, or deltoid. Rotate
injection site periodically. For SC administration, rotate injection site. Choose site with adequate SC tissue but
without excessive pigment, nodules, lesions, or hair (e.g., upper or mid-abdomen, upper buttocks). Avoid areas
with brawny or fibrous tissues or areas that may be compressed or rubbed (e.g., belt or waistband). Administer
within 30 minutes of preparation.

Dose Adjustment

Renal and Hepatic Impairment:
Use with caution. No studies have been conducted.

Indications

Endometriosis; prostate cancer; uterine leiomyomata (fibroids)

Contraindications

Women who are or may become pregnant, breast-feeding, undiagnosed abnormal vaginal bleeding

Precautions

WARNING: Hazardous agent. Use appropriate precautions for handling and disposal.

Cardiovascular diseases (may increase risk for cardiovascular disease; sudden cardiac death and stroke have been
reported among men; may prolong the QT / QTc interval)

Decreased bone density (use with caution in patients with additional risk factors for bone loss);

Endometriosis (may exacerbate endometriosis or uterine leiomyomata)

Hyperglycemia (diabetes and/or worsening of glycemic control have been reported in men).

Pituitary apoplexy (may present as sudden headache, vomiting, visual or mental status changes, and infrequently
cardiovascular collapse; immediate medical attention required);

Prostate cancer (may increase risk for cardiovascular disease, diabetes, insulin resistance, obesity, alterations in
lipids, and fractures).

Psychiatric illness (use with caution; alteration in mood, memory impairment, and depression have been associated
with use)

Seizures

Spinal cord compression (monitor closely for weakness and paresthesia in first few weeks of therapy)

Tumor flare

Urinary tract obstruction (monitor closely for urinary tract obstruction and hematuria in first few weeks of therapy).

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs: Antidiabetic Agents, Choline C11

Avoid concomitant use with:
Enhances QTc-prolonging effect of Leuproreline: Hydroxychloroquine, Promazine, QTc-prolonging Agents \Highest
and Moderate Risk)

Mechanism of Action

An analogue of gonadorelin with similar properties, is used for the suppression ofgonadal sex hormone production in
the treatment of malignant neoplasms of the prostate.
