Dosage

Administration

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric:
Should be administered with caution.

Renal Impairment:
Not recommended for patients with moderate or severe renal impairment, may require dose interval adjustment of
lamivudine and tenofovir that cannot be achieved with combination tablet.

Hepatic Impairment:
Monitor closely for adverse reactions, especially nervous system symptoms related to Efavirenz.

Indications

First-line treatment for HIV infection.

Contraindications

Patients with clinically significant hypersensitivity to tenofovir, lamivudine, efavirenz or any of the excipients
contained in the formulation.

Precautions

WARNING: Chronic hepatitis B and lactic acidosis or hepatomegaly may occur.
Should not be administered concomitantly with other cytidine analogues such as Emtricitabine.
Refer to individual drug monographs of Tenofovir,
Lamivudine and Efavirenz.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Refer to individual drug monographs of Lamivudine, Tenofovir, and Efavirenz.

Drug Interactions

Refer to individual monographs of Lamivudine, Tenofovir, and Efavirenz.

Mechanism of Action

A combination of three antiviral drugs used to treat HIV infection in adults and adolescents.
