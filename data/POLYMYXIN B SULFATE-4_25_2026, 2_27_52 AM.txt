Dosage

IM / IT / IV › [RESERVE] 500 000 units powder for solution for Injection (intrathecal/IM/TV) in 5 mL vial (Rx)

Infection, intravenous, ADULT AND CHILDREN, 15 000- 25 000 u/kg/day every 12 hr, INFANT, up to 40 000 u/kg/day;
intramuscular, ADULT AND CHILDREN, 25 000-30 000 u/kg/day divided at 4-6 hr intervals, INFANT, up to 40 000
u/kg/day; intrathecal, ADULT AND CHILDREN >2yr, 50 000 u once daily for 3-4 days then 50 000 u once every other
day for at least 2 weeks after cultures of CSF are negative and sugar content returned to normal, CHILDREN <2yr,
20 000 u once daily for 3-4 days or 25 000 u once every other day, continue 25 000 u once every other day for at
least 2 weeks after cultures of CSF are negative and sugar content returned to normal

Administration

IM / IT / IV › [RESERVE] 500 000 units powder for solution for Injection (intrathecal/IM/TV) in 5 mL vial (Rx)

Administration: Used parenterally for the treatment of infections due to susceptible Gram-negative bacteria,
especially Pseudomonas aeruginosa; given intrathecally in meningeal infection, and by subconjunctival injection
for eye infections NOTE: Intramuscular/intrathecal/intravenous administration only to hospitalized patients IM use
is not routinely recommended in infants and children due to severe pain at injection site

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric: For kidney impairment, reduce from 15 000 u/kg downward every 12 hr; total daily dose not more than 25
000-30 000 u/kg/day

Indications

Treatment of infections of the blood stream caused by susceptible strains of Pseudomonas aeruginosa; likewise
indicated in serious infections caused by susceptible strains of the following organisms: Escherichia coli, Aerogenes
(specifically bacteremia), and Klebsiella pneumoniae (specifically bacteremia).

Off-label, adult: selective gastrointestinal tract decontamination

Contraindications

Known hypersensitivity to polymyxin B or any component of the formulation

Precautions

May cause severe pain at IM injection site or thrombophlebitis at IV infusion site
Patients with preexisting renal impairment, advanced age and dehydration
Avoid parenteral use whenever possible
Not absorbed well from a normal gastrointestinal tract if present in breast milk

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
facial flushing; neurotoxicity (includes ataxia,blurred vision, drowsiness, irritability, numbness of extremities, oral
paresthesia), dizziness, drug fever, meningitis (intrathecal administration); urticaria; hypocalcemia, hypochloremia,
hypokalemia, hyponatremia; nephrotoxicity; anaphylactoid reaction; pain at injection site; neuromuscular blockade,
weakness

Drug Interactions

Avoid concurrent or sequential use:
Anesthesia or muscle relaxants (respiratory depression from neuromuscular blockade)
Decreases therapeutic effects of the following drugs:
BCG (intravesical), BCG vaccine
Cholera vaccine
Lactobacillus and Estriol
Sodium picosulfate

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of polymyxin B sulfate:
Capreomycin (neuromuscular-blocking effect)
Cefazedone (neph rotoxic effect)
Methoxyflurane (nephrotoxic effect)
Prolonged use (fungal or bacterial superinfection)

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs:
Bacitracin (neprotoxicity)
Colistimethate (neuromuscular-blocking effect)
Mecamylamine (neuromuscular-blocking effect)
Neuromuscular-blocking agents
Other nephrotoxic drugs (nephrotoxicity)

Mechanism of Action

Polymyxin B and other polymyxin antibacterials act primarily by binding to membrane phospholipids and disrupting
the bacterial cytoplasmic membrane. It has a bactericidal action on most Gram-negative bacilli except Proteus spp.
