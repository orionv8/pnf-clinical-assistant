Dosage

ORAL › 2.5 mg tablet (as mesilate) (Rx)

Hyperprolactinemia, by mouth, ADULT, initially 0.5 to 2.5 mg once daily at bedtime to increase tolerance; slowly
increase the daily dose by 1.25 to 2.5 mg every 1–2 weeks up to an average of 20–30 mg once daily (maximum, 100
mg once daily).

Administration

ORAL › 2.5 mg tablet (as mesilate) (Rx)

Administer with food to decrease GI distress.

Dose Adjustment

Hepatic Impairment:
May require dose adjustment. Undergoes extensive hepatic metabolism. Use with caution.

Indications

Management of hyperprolactinemia.

Contraindications

Uncontrolled hypertension; eclampsia; preeclampsia; pregnancy-induced hypertension; postpartum patients with
coronary artery disease or other severe cardiovascular conditions.

Precautions

Cardiac valvular fibrosis; cardiovascular effects (e.g., symptomatic hypotension; seizures, hypertension, MI, and
stroke).

Visual or auditory hallucinations; impulse control disorders; sedation (e.g., sudden sleep onset and somnolence);
dementia; psychosis.

Melanoma; pleural or retroperitoneal fibrosis (prolonged and high-dose daily use); peptic ulcer disease (severe GI
bleeding; may be fatal).

Acromegaly (digital vasospasm); diabetic ketoacidosis; macroadenomas; pituitary irradiation; prolactinsecreting
adenomas.

Elderly (concomitant diseases or drug therapy may complicate therapy).

Pregnancy (a pregnancy test every 4 weeks is recommended as long as no menses occur); lactation.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Hypotension, Raynaud’s phenomenon, syncope, vasospasm (digital), dizziness, fatigue, headache,
drowsiness, hypoglycemia, nausea, lightheadedness, constipation, anorexia, GI hemorrhage, abdominal cramps,
dyspepsia, diarrhea, vomiting, xerostomia, increased susceptibility to infection weakness, amblyopia, rhinitis, flu-like
symptoms, nasal congestion, sinusitis.

Less Common: Abdominal distress, acquired valvular heart disease, alopecia, anxiety, ataxia, blepharospasm, blurred
vision, bradycardia, cardiac arrhythmia, cerebrovascular accident (postpartum), cold extremities, cold intolerance,
confusion, constrictive pericarditis, delusions, depression, dyskinesia, dysphagia, dyspnea, epileptiform seizures,
ergot alkaloids toxicity, erythromelalgia, GI ulcer, hallucination, heavy headedness, MI (postpartum), hypersensitivity
reaction, hypertension (postpartum), increased cerebrospinal fluid pressure, increased libido, insomnia, lassitude,
lethargy, muscle cramps, narcolepsy, nervousness, nightmares, onoff phenomenon, pallor, paranoia, paresthesia,
pericardial effusion, peripheral edema, pleural effusion, psychomotor agitation, psychosis, pulmonary fibrosis,
retroperitoneal fibrosis, pleurisy, seizure (postpartum), skin mottling, skin rash, status epilepticus (postpartum),
sleep disorder, tachycardia, tingling of the ears, tinnitus, transient blindness, urinary frequency, urinary
incontinence, vasodepressor syncope, urinary retention, ventricular tachycardia, vertigo, visual disturbance.

Mechanism of Action

Bromocriptine is a semisynthetic ergot alkaloid and dopamine agonist with high affinity for the D2 receptor and weak
a-agonist activity. The dopamine receptor agonist activity also causes inhibition of prolactinsecreting cells in the
anterior pituitary for the treatment of hyperprolactinemia and for reducing tumor sizes in prolactin-and growth
hormone-secreting pituitary adenomas.
