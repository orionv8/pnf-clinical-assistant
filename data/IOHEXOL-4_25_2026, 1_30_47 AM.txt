Administration

IT / I-VASC › 300 mg iodine/mL, 50 mL vial (Rx)

Always administer the lowest dose necessary to obtain adequate visualization. Apply sterile technique in the
withdrawal from container and administration of the product. Visually inspect for particulate matter and
discoloration prior to administration. Slightly warm to body temperature immediately prior to injection. Patients
should be well hydrated prior to and following administration of any contrast medium. For intrathecal
administration, always keep the head elevated above the highest level of the spine. Do not lower head of table
more than 15o in moving contrast medium cranially. Inject slowly (over 1–2 minutes) to avoid excessive mixing. To
maintain as a bolus, move medium to distal area very slowly. Use fluoroscopic monitoring. Avoid intracranial entry
of bolus. Avoid early and high cephalad dispersion of the medium. Avoid abrupt or active patient movement to
minimize excessive mixing of medium with CSF. Instruct patient to remain passive. Move patient slowly and only as
necessary. NOTE: In patients with excessive lordosis, consider lateral position for injection and movement of the
medium cephalad. Maintaining the patient post-myelography in an upright position (via wheelchair or ambulation)
may help minimize adverse effects.

Dose Adjustment

Pheochromocytoma:
Administer absolute minimum amount required.

Indications

Angiocardiography (ventriculography, selective coronary arteriography), aortography, including studies of the aortic
root, aortic arch, ascending aorta, abdominal aorta and its branches; contrast enhancement for computed
tomographic head and body imaging; intravenous digital subtraction angiography of the head, neck, abdominal, renal
and peripheral vessels; peripheral arteriography; excretory urography

Contraindications

Systemic or local infection

Precautions

WARNING: Procedures involving radiopaque diagnostic agents should only be done by qualified personnel with in-
depth knowledge of the procedure to be done. Emergency treatment for severe reactions and appropriate facilities
needs to be readily available should there be any complication in the procedure. Severe delayed reactions may occur
after administration; well- trained personnel and emergency facilities should be readily available within 30- 60
minutes after administration.

WARNING: Reports of serious and rarely fatal thromboembolic events leading to myocardial infarction and stroke
have been documented. Exercise extra caution in intravascular administration especially in angiographic procedures
(caution in guidewire and catheter manipulation, use of manifold systems and/or three-way stopcocks, frequent
catheter flushing with heparinized saline solutions and minimizing the length of the procedure should be
considered). Plastic syringes have decreased the possibility of in- vitro clotting.

Potentially hazardous in patients with multiple myeloma or other paraproteinemia, particularly therapeutically
resistant anuria.

May promote sickling in individuals who are homozygous for sickle cell disease

Development of severe cutaneous adverse reactions (SCAR) may develop after 1 hour to several weeks following IV
administration, including Stevens-Johnson syndrome and toxic epidermal necrolysis (SJS/TEN), acute generalized
exanthematous pustulosis (AGEP) and drug reaction with eosinophilia and systemic symptoms (DRESS).

Severe liver and kidney functional disturbances, severe thyrotoxicosis, or myelomatosis (use with extreme care);
Pheochromocytoma (measures for the treatment of hypertensive crisis should be readily available);
Thyroid storm;

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
Arrhythmias, angina, nausea, vomiting, hypotension, photomas, headache, pain, dizziness (transient), backache

Less Common:
Asystole, cardiac failure, bradycardia, tachycardia, vasovagal reaction, dyspnea, rhinitis, coughing, laryngitis,
urticaria, warm sensation

Rare:
Diarrhea, dyspepsia, cramp, dry mouth, purpura, abscess, pruritus, fever, hives, stomachache, visual hallucination,
neurological changes

Drug Interactions

NOTE: Do NOT admix with other pharmaceuticals. Many radiopaque contrast agents are incompatible in vitro with
many other drugs.

Avoid concomitant use with:
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects Iohexol, specifically seizures

[NOTE: Discontinue the following drugs at least 48 hours prior to myelography]:
CNS Stimulants, MAO Inhibitors e.g., Phenothiazine Derivatives
Tricyclic Antidepressants e.g. Amitriptyline
Major tranquilizers, Anti- psychotics

Mechanism of Action

NOTE: For IV and other parenteral routes as appropriate. A nonionic, water-soluble radiographic contrast medium. It
will opacify blood vessels in the path of flow of the contrast medium permitting radiographic visualization of the
internal structures until significant hemodilution occurs.
