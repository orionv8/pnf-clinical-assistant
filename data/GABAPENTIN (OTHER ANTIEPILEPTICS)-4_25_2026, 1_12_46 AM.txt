Dosage

ORAL › 100 mg capsule (Rx)

Seizures, partial onset, by mouth, ADULT or CHILD =12 years, initially 300 mg on the 1st day, 300 mg twice a day on
the 2nd day, and 300 mg 3 times a day on the 3rd day; or 300 mg 3 times daily on the 1st day, increase dose based on
response and tolerability; usual dose range is 900–1,800 mg daily administered in 3 divided doses; CHILD 5–11
years, initially 10–15 mg/kg daily in 3 divided doses, titrate to effective dose over approximately 3 days, increase
dosage based on response and tolerability; usual dose range is 25–35 mg/kg daily in 3 divided doses with maximum
interval of 12 hours (maximum, 50 mg/kg daily); CHILD 3–4 years, initially 10–15 mg/kg daily in 3 divided doses, titrate
to effective dose over approximately 3 days, increase dosage based on response and tolerability; usual dose range
is 40 mg/kg daily in 3 divided doses. NOTE: If gabapentin is discontinued or if another anticonvulsant is added to
therapy, it should be done slowly over a minimum of 1 week.

Administration

ORAL › 100 mg capsule (Rx)

May be taken with or without food. Administer first dose on first day at bedtime to avoid somnolence and
dizziness. When given 3 times daily, the maximum time between doses should not exceed 12 hours. Do NOT
discontinue abruptly because of the possibility of increasing seizure frequency. Withdraw gradually to minimize
the potential of increased seizure frequency, unless safety concerns require a more rapid withdrawal. See
Gabapentin under Analgesics – Other Analgesics and Antipyretics: Drugs for Neuropathic Pain in : Nervous System
for other information.

Indications

Adjunct therapy in the treatment of partial seizures with and without secondary generalization in adults and pediatric
patients 3 years and older.

Mechanism of Action

Gabapentin is a leucine analog of GABA. Its therapeutic properties are due to its selective binding to a2d subunits of
voltage-sensitive calcium channels, causing closure of N and P/Q presynaptic calcium channels that diminishes
excessive neuronal activity and neurotransmitter release.
