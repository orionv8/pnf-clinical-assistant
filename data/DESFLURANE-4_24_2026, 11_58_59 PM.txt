Dosage

RESPIR › Inhalation: 240 mL bottle (Rx)

Anesthesia, induction, by inhalation, ADULT, initially 3%; increase by 0.5–1% increments every 2–3 breaths; end
tidal concentrations 4–11% v/v [NOTE: Inspired concentrations >12% have been safely administered during
induction and may require a reduction of nitrous oxide or air]. Anesthesia, maintenance, by inhalation, ADULT, 2.5
to 8.5% v/v with or without concomitant nitrous oxide; CHILD, 5.2–10% with or without nitrous oxide. NOTE: Not
indicated as induction agent or for maintenance of anesthesia in non-intubated children <6 years. Dosing based on
patient’s age and Minimum Alveolar Concentration (MAC): Age [MAC with 100% Oxygen] [MAC with 60% N2O/ 40%
O2] 9 months [10%] [7.5%] 3 years [8.9%] [6.4%] 25 years [7.3%] [4%] 45 years [6%] [2.8%] 70 years [5.2%] [1.7%]

Administration

RESPIR › Inhalation: 240 mL bottle (Rx)

Inhalation via desflurane-specific calibrated heated vaporizer NOTE: Monitor BP, heart rate and rhythm,
temperature, oxygen saturation, end-tidal CO2 and end-tidal desflurane concentration prior to and throughout
procedure. Monitor signs and symptoms of airway narrowing for pediatric patients. Assess for malignant
hyperthermia, hypercapnia, muscle rigidity, tachycardia, cyanosis, arrhythmias, hypotension or hypertension.

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric: Geriatric dose requires approximately 70% the adult dose in 100 % oxygen and 40% the adult dose of
nitrous oxide

Note: Minimum Alveolar Concentration (MAC) decreases with increasing age. Adjust dose accordingly.

Indications

Induction and maintenance of anesthesia.

Contraindications

Known or suspected genetic susceptibility to malignant hyperthermia; induction of anesthesia in pediatric patients;
history of moderate-tosevere hepatic impairment following anesthesia with halogenated agents and not otherwise
explained; history of hepatitis or in whom hepatic dysfunction, jaundice, unexplained fever, leukocytosis, or
eosinophilia has occurred due to a halogenated inhalational anesthetic.

Precautions

Patient with or at risk of raise intracranial pressure; Patients at risk of QT prolongation; Neuromuscular diseases e.g.
Duchenne muscular dystrophy; Chronic renal or hepatic impairment.

Children (safety has not been investigated in non-intubated patients < 2 years)

Pregnancy and lactation.

SKILLED TASKS. May impair ability to perform tasks, which require mental alertness, like driving or operating
machinery.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Decreased oxygen saturation, bradycardia, hypertension (including malignant hypertension), nodal
arrhythmia, shivering, tachycardia, headache, nausea, vomiting, sialorrhea, laryngospasm, conjunctivitis, cough,
breath-holding, apnea, increased bronchial secretions, pharyngitis.

Less Common: Abdominal pain, acute pancreatitis, agitation, asthma, atrial fibrillation, bigeminy, blood coagulation
disorder, bronchospasm, cardiac arrhythmia, cholestasis, dizziness, dyspnea, erythema, hemoptysis, hemorrhage,
hepatic failure, hepatic necrosis, fever, hepatitis (including cytolytic hepatitis), hepatotoxicity, hyperkalemia,
hypokalemia, hypokinesia (ventricular), hypoxia, hypotension, shock, ischemic heart disease, leukocytosis, malaise,
malignant hyperthermia, metabolic acidosis, myalgia, pruritus, respiratory distress, respiratory failure,
rhabdomyolysis, scleral icterus, seizure, myocardial infarction, torsade de pointes, urticaria, vasodilatation,
ventricular dysfunction, weakness.

Drug Interactions

Avoid concomitant use with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of the following drugs: Blood pressure-lowering agents; Hypotensionassociated
agents (hypotensive effect); Dopamine; Epinephrine (nasal; oral inhalation and systemic); Norepinephrine
(arrhythmogenic effect); Metyrosine; Ropinirole, Rotigotine, Pramipexole (sedative effect); Neuromuscular-blocking
Agents, Nondepolarizing, e.g., Atracurium (neuromuscular-blocking effect) QTc-prolonging Agents (QTc-prolonging
effect)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Desflurane: Ephedrine nasal and systemic (arrhythmogenic effect);
Methylphenidate (hypertensive effect); Other CNS Depressants; Rufinamide (sleepiness and dizziness)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Amifostine (hypotensive effect); Fenoterol,
Formoterol, Isoproterenol, Metaraminol (arrhythmogenic effect); SSRIs (psychomotor impairment)

Mechanism of Action

Desflurane is a highly fluorinated, volatile methyl ethyl esther used for maintenance of general anesthesia. It
enhances inhibitory postsynaptic activity and inhibits excitatory synaptic activity, resulting in reversible loss of
consciousness and of pain sensations, suppression of voluntary motor activity, reduction of autonomic reflexes, and
sedation of respiratory and the cardiovascular system.
