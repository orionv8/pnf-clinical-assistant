Contraindications

Patients with demonstrable collections of air in the pleural, pericardial, or peritoneal space; intestinal obstruction;
occlusion of the middle ear; arterial air embolism; decompression sickness; chronic obstructive airway disease;
emphysema; recent pneumoencephalography

Precautions

Addictive (may be associated with abuse and/or addiction);

Body space volume expansion; Bone marrow suppression (prolonged use may produce bone marrow suppression;
increased risk in vitamin B12 deficiency and other nutritional deficiencies); Neurologic effects (increased risk in
vitamin B12 deficiency and other nutritional deficiencies);

Vitreoretinal surgery (may increase intraocular pressure resulting in retinal artery occlusion, ischemia, or optic nerve
damage and vision loss).

Adverse Drug Reactions

Nausea, vomiting, neurologic effects (prolonged use)

Rare: Hypotension, CNS stimulation, confusion, dizziness, headache, nausea, vomiting, apnea

Drug Interactions

Avoid concomitant use with:
Enhances sedative effect of the following drugs: Pramipexole; Ropinirole; Rotigotine
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Ketamine: CNS depressants; Rufinamide (sleepiness and dizziness)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of SSRIs (psychomotor impairment)

Mechanism of Action

Nitrous Oxide is an odorless gas that serves as an anesthetic and analgesic. At high concentrations, it causes a
narcotic effect and may replace oxygen, causing asphyxiation.
