NOTE: Recommended starting dose combination is determined according to asthma severity. Consider higher

daily). COPD, by aerosol inhalation, ADULT, 2 inhalations (160mcg/4.5mcg) twice daily (maximum, 4 inhalations daily).

Administration

RESPIR › DPI: 80 micrograms budesonide + 4.5 micrograms formoterol (as fumarate dihydrate) x 60 doses with appropriate
accompanying dispenser (Rx)

Prior to first use, “load” the inhaler by turning the grip on the inhaler as far as it will move in 1 direction, then turning
in the opposite direction as far as it will go. The inhaler is “loaded” with a dose, indicated by a “click”. Loading
procedure should be done twice. It does not need to be repeated with subsequent uses even when not used
regularly. Place mouthpiece gently between teeth, closing lips around inhaler. Inhale deeply and hold breath for 5–
10 seconds. The amount of drug delivered is small, and the individual will not sense the medication as it is inhaled.
Remove mouthpiece prior to exhalation. Do NOT breathe out through the mouthpiece. After use of the inhaler,
rinse mouth or oropharynx with water and spit out rinse solution. Withdrawal and discontinuation of a
corticosteroid should be done slowly and carefully. Use with caution when transferring from systemic to inhaled
corticosteroids.

Indications

Treatment of asthma in patients ≥12 years of age where combination therapy is indicated; maintenance treatment of
airflow obstruction associated with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), including chronic bronchitis and
emphysema

Contraindications

As primary treatment of status asthmaticus, acute episodes of asthma or COPD requiring intensive measures, and
potentially worsening or rapidly deteriorating asthma or COPD.

Precautions

WARNING: Long-acting beta2-adrenergic agonists (LABAs) increase the risk of asthma-related death and
asthma_x0002_related hospitalization in pediatric and adolescent patients. Use only in patients not adequately
controlled on a long-term asthma control medication or if disease severity clearly warrants treatment with both an
inhaled corticosteroid and a LABA. Once asthma control is achieved and maintained, assess the patient at regular
intervals and step-down therapy if possible without loss of asthma control, and maintain the patient on a
long_x0002_term asthma control medication.

Adrenal suppression; diabetes; Bone density; osteoporosis;
Bronchospasm; asthma (not for acute bronchospasm);
COPD (not use for acute episodes of COPD); Hypersensitivity reactions; immunosuppression (restrict use in active
TB); lower respiratory infections (monitor patients closely); oral candidiasis; Serious effects or fatalities; Vasculitis;
cardiovascular disease; Renal impairment; hepatic impairment (monitor closely due to hepatic metabolism of both
agents); Hypokalemia; Ocular disease (consider routine eye exams in chronic users); Seizure disorders; Thyroid
disease (changes in thyroid status may necessitate dose adjustments;metabolic clearance of corticosteroids
increases in hyperthyroid patients and decreases in hypothyroid ones); Children (titrate to lowest effective dose to
minimize the systemic effects; growth should be routinely monitored); Pregnancy and lactation (refer to individual
agents).

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Headache, dizziness, abdominal distress, oral candidiasis, vomiting, influenza, back pain, upper and lower
respiratory tract infection, pharyngolaryngeal pain, sinusitis, bronchitis, nasal congestion

Less Common: Agitation, anaphylaxis, angina pectoris, angioedema, anxiety, atrial arrhythmia, behavioral changes,
bronchospasm, bruise, cataract, cough, decreased linear skeletal growth rate (children), depression, dermatitis,
extrasystoles, glaucoma, hypercorticoidism signs and symptoms, hyperglycemia, hypersensitivity reaction,
hypertension, hypokalemia, hypotension, immunosuppression, increased intraocular pressure, insomnia, muscle
cramps, nausea, nervousness, palpitations, pruritus, restlessness, skin rash, tachycardia, throat irritation, tremor,
urticaria, ventricular arrhythmia, voice disorder

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Budesonide + Formoterol: Atomoxetine (tachycardia; hypertensive effect),
Cannabinoid [except Cannabidiol] (tachycardia), Tedizolid (tachycardia; hypertensive effect)

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Budesonide +
Formoterol:
Amphotericin B (hypokalemic effec), Caffeine
(hypokalemic effect), Inhalational Anesthetics
(arrhythmogenic effect), MAO Inhibitors, Other
Sympathomimetics, Tricyclic Antidepressants

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following
drugs:
Atosiban (pulmonary edema and/or dyspnea), Ceritinib (hyperglycemic effect), Deferasirox (GI ulceration, irritation, or
bleeding), Doxofylline, Loop Diuretics (hypokalemic effect), Moderate Risk QTc-prolonging Agents (QTc-prolonging
effect), Theophylline Derivatives (hypokalemic effect), Thiazide Diuretics (hypokalemic effect)

Reduces therapeutic effect of Budesonide + Formoterol:
Beta1 Blockers (bronchodilator effect), Betahistine

Avoid concomitant use with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Budesonide + Formoterol:Linezolid (hypertensive effect) [reduce initial doses
Budesonide + Formoterol]

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Budesonide + Formoterol:
Mifepristone (QTc-prolonging effect), Other Long-acting Beta2 Agonists

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs:
Highest Risk QTc-prolonging Agents (QTc-prolonging effect), Loxapine

Increases serum concentration of Budesonide: Telaprevir

Reduces therapeutic effect of Budesonide + Formoterol:
Beta Blockers, Nonselective (bronchodilator effect)

Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs:
Aldesleukin (antineoplastic effect), Hyaluronidase, Iobenguane I 123

Mechanism of Action

A combination product containing a corticosteroid and a long-acting beta2-adrenergic agonist.
