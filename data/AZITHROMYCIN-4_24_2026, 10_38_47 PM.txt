Dosage

ORAL › {Watch} 250 mg capsule (as base / as dihydrate) (Rx)

Uncomplicated typhoid fever, by mouth, CHILD, 20 mg/kg for 5-7 days; ADULT, 500 mg-1g for 5-7 days. Complicated
typhoid fever, by mouth, CHILD, 20 mg/kg for 5-7 days, ADULT, 500 mg 1-2 tablets for 5-7 days Leptospirosis, mild,
by mouth, CHILD, 1 g loading dose then 10 mg/kg for 2 days; Moderate to severe, by mouth, CHILD, 500 mg for 5
days. Prophylaxis, by mouth, CHILD, 10 mg/kg; If children are exposed for more than 7 days, the dose should be
repeated after 1 week. Acute bacterial arthritis, monoarticular, by mouth, ADULT, 1g for 7 days minimum. Dental
prophylaxis, allergic to penicillin or ampicillin, by mouth, CHILD, 15 mg/kg; ADULT, 500 mg. Conjunctivitis,
Chlamydia trachomatis, by mouth, NEONATE 3-10 days, 20 mg/kg every 24 hours for 3 days. Gonococcal
conjunctivitis, for presumptive Chlamydia co-infection, by mouth, ADULT, 1 g per day PLUS with topical levofloxacin
OR tobramycin OR erythromycin ointment every 4 days for 2-3 weeks or until resolution of symptoms. Pharyngitis
or tonsilitis, penicillin allergy, by mouth, CHILD, 12 mg/kg for 5 days; ADULT, 500 mg then 250 mg for 4 days or 500
mg for 3 days Pertussis, by mouth, INFANT <1 month, 10 mg/kg every 24 hours for 5 days; INFANT >2 months, 10
mg/kg on day 1 then 5 mg/kg every 24 hours for 4 days; ADULT, 500 mg on day 1 then 250 mg every 24 hours on day
2 to 5. Acute bacterial exacerbation of chronic bronchitis (ABECB), severe, by mouth, ADULT, 500 mg every 24
hours for 3 days. Community-acquired pneumonia, by mouth, CHILD, 10 mg/kg for 3 days or 10 mg/kg on day 1 then 5
mg/kg on day 2 to 5; Low-risk, without co-morbid illness, by mouth, ADULT, 500 mg for 3-5 days; Low-risk, with
stable co-morbid illness, by mouth, ADULT, 500 mg with co-amoxiclav OR cefuroxime for 3-5 days; Moderate-risk,
by mouth, ADULT, 500 mg with ampicillin-sulbactam or cefuroxime. High-risk, risk for P. aeruginosa, by IV infusion,
ADULT, 500 mg with piperacillin-tazobactam/ cefepime/meropenem and gentamicin/amikacin.

Administration

ORAL › {Watch} 250 mg capsule (as base / as dihydrate) (Rx)

Capsules should be taken at least 1 hour before, or 2 hours after, food; oral suspension can be taken with food to
reduce GI discomfort and increase tolerability. NOTE: Oral suspensions (100 mg/5 mL and 200 mg/5 mL) may be
stored for 10 days post-reconstitution and taken without regard to food.

Dose Adjustment

Renal Impairment:
For severe impairment – refer to specialist

Hepatic Impairment:
(except for Contraindications stated above):
For severe impairment – reduce dose

Indications

Suspected uncomplicated genital chlamydial infections (e.g., urethritis, cervicitis, trachoma);

low-risk community-acquired pneumonia (CAP-LR) in adults, when Legionella, Chlamydia or Mycoplasma is
suspected, or when there is hypersensitivity to the beta-lactams;

non-severe CAP in child with hypersensitivity to amoxicillin.

Contraindications

With history of cholestatic jaundice or hepatic impairment associated with prior azithromycin use.

Precautions

Cardiac conditions: congenital long QT syndrome and family history of QT prolongation; torsades de pointes,
ventricular arrhythmia and other arrhythmias, congestive heart failure, recent MI;

Proarrhythmic conditions (e.g., hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia);

Renal impairment
Exacerbations of myasthenia gravis

Elderly patients (may be more susceptible to drug-associated QT prolongation);
Breastfeeding (limited drug information available – use only if adequate alternatives are not available).

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Abdominal pain, arthralgia, cramping, diarrhea, dizziness, dyspepsia, flatulence, headache, malaise,
nausea, pruritic, rash, vaginitis, vomiting.

Less Common: Abnormal heart rhythm, allergic reaction, anorexia, anxiety, chest pain, conjunctivitis, constipation,
drowsiness, edema, enteritis, fatigue, gastritis, hyperkinesia, hypesthesia, hypotension, insomnia, leukopenia, liver
disorders, myasthenia gravis, neutropenia, palpitations, pancreatitis, paresthesia, photosensitivity, rash, sleep
disturbances, somnolence, thrombocytopenia, tinnitus, urticaria, vertigo.

Rare: Acute renal failure, agitation, angioedema, cholestatic jaundice, convulsions, disturbances in taste and vision,
hemolytic anemia, hepatic necrosis and failure, interstitial nephritis, seizures, syncope.

Drug Interactions

NOTE: Macrolides inhibit P-glycoprotein, which may lead to drug interactions.

Monitor closely with:
Reduces absorption of Azithromycin:
Antacids (e.g. aluminum or magnesium hydroxide)

Reduces therapeutic effect of Contraceptives (Oral)

Avoid concomitant use with:
Increases serum concentration of Digoxin

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs:
Amiodarone, Artemether + Lumefantrine
