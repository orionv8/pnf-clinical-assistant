Dosage

ORAL › 1 mg (as Hydrochloride) Tablet (Rx)

Benign prostatic hypertrophy, by mouth, ADULT, 1 mg every bedtime; may gradually increase at 7-day intervals
according to response; (maintenance dose, 5–10 mg once every bedtime).

Administration

ORAL › 1 mg (as Hydrochloride) Tablet (Rx)

Administer with or without food once daily at bedtime. Give first dose and subsequent increase at bedtime to
prevent syncope.

Indications

For hypertension and lower urinary tract symptoms (LUTS) associated with BPH; treatment of signs and symptoms of
BPH and relief of symptoms of urinary obstruction

Precautions

Hypotension;
Volume depletion;
Renal impairment;
Hepatic impairment;
Patients who are receiving an antihypertensive treatment may require dose reduction and supervision;
Monitor for an improved urine flow and for “first dose” orthostatic hypotension, headache, or GI disturbances, e.g.,
nausea, vomiting, at the beginning of therapy and on a regular basis;
Elderly and patients undergoing cataract surgery (risk of intraoperative floppy iris syndrome)

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Dizziness, asthenia
Less Common: Hypotension, rhinitis, light-headedness, somnolence, palpitation, nausea, edema, sinusitis, dyspnea,
fatigue, headache, back pain, flu-like syndrome, tachycardia, amblyopia, blurred vision, impotence, syncope

Mechanism of Action

An alpha1-selective adrenoceptor antagonist used in BPH to relieve symptoms of urinary obstruction, including
acute urinary retention.
