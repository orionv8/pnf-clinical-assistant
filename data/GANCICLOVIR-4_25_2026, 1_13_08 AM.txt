Dosage

IV INFUSION › 500 mg vial (as sodium) (Rx)

CMV Retinitis, by slow IV infusion, ADULT and CHILD, induction therapy: 5mg/kg/dose every 12 hours for 14-21 days
followed by maintenance therapy of 5mg/kg/day as a single daily dose for 7 days/week or 6mg/kg/day for 5
days/week. Prevention of CMV disease in transplant patients, by slow IV infusion, ADULT and CHILD, same initial
and maintenance dose as CMV retinitis except duration of initial course is 7-14 days, duration of maintenance
therapy is dependent on clinical condition and degree of immunosuppression.

Administration

IV INFUSION › 500 mg vial (as sodium) (Rx)

Should NOT be administered by IM, subcutaneously, or rapid IV push; administer by slow IV infusion over at least 1
hour. Too rapid infusion can cause increased toxicity and excessive plasma levels.

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric:
Refer to adult dosing
Renal Impairment:
IV (induction):
CrCl 50-69mL/min: 2.5 mg/kg/dose every 12 hours.
CrCl 25-49mL/min: 2.5 mg/kg/dose every 24 hours.
CrCl 10-24mL/min: 1.25 mg/kg/dose every 24 hours.
CrCl <10mL/min: Administer 1.25mg/kg/dose 3 times/week following hemodialysis.

IV (maintenance):
CrCl 50-69mL/min: 2.5 mg/kg/dose every 24hours.
CrCl 25-49mL/min: 1.25 mg/kg/dose every 24 hours
CrCl 10-24mL/min: 0.625 mg/kg/dose every 24 hours.
CrCl >10mL/minute: 0.625 mg/kg/dose 3 times/week following hemodialysis.

Indications

Treatment of CMV retinitis in immunocompromised individuals, including patients with acquired immunodeficiency
syndrome; prophylaxis of CMV infection in transplant patients.

Precautions

WARNING: Animal studies have demonstrated carcinogenic and teratogenic effects, and inhibition of
spermatogenesis.
Granulocytopenia (neutropenia);
Anemia and thrombocytopenia may occur;
Use with caution in patients with renal impairment (dosage adjustment or interruption of therapy may be necessary).

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Fever, diarrhea, anorexia, vomiting, thrombocytopenia, leukopenia, anemia, neutropenia, retinal
detachment, sepsis, diaphoresis.

Less Common: Chills, neuropathy, pruritus.

Rare: Anaphylaxis, alopecia, arrhythmia, bronchospasm, cardiac arrest, cataracts, cholestasis, coma, dyspnea,
edema, encephalopathy, exfoliative dermatitis, extrapyramidal symptoms, hepatitis, hepatic failure, pancytopenia,
pulmonary fibrosis, seizure, urticaria, SJS, torsades de pointes, renal failure, visual loss.
