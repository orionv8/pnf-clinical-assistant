Dosage

ORAL › 100 mg tablet (OTC)

NOTE: Dose is individualized and determined by the physician. For hydralazine toxicity related to pyridoxine
deficiency, by mouth, ADULT and ADOLESCENT, 100–300 mg daily; CHILD, 10 to 50 mg daily. Hydralazine toxicity
prophylaxis, by mouth, ADULT, 100 mg once daily; ADOLESCENT, 25–100 mg daily; CHILD, 1–2 mg/kg daily. For
isoniazid toxicity related to pyridoxine deficiency, excluding seizures, by mouth, ADULT and ADOLESCENT, 25–100
mg daily; CHILD, 1–2 mg/kg daily. Isoniazid toxicity prophylaxis, by mouth, ADULT, 10 to 75 mg daily; or 25 mg daily;
ADOLESCENT, 25–100 mg daily; or 25 mg daily; CHILD, 1–2 mg/kg daily. [NOTE: Guidelines recommend prophylaxis
only in patients at risk for peripheral neuropathy, including those with nutritional deficiency, diabetes,
symptomatic HIV infection, renal failure, or in patients who are breastfed.] For the treatment of drug-induced
seizures secondary to acute isoniazid overdose, by mouth, ADOLESCENT, CHILD, and INFANT, Acute ingestion of
known amount, pyridoxine dose equal to amount of isoniazid ingested (maximum, 70 mg/kg per dose, up to 5 g);
Acute ingestion of unknown amount, 70 mg/kg per dose (maximum, 5 g per dose); administer until seizure stops or
maximum initial dose has been administered; if the entire initial dose is not administered, give remaining initial
dose over the next 4–6 hours; may repeat dose every 5–30 minutes as needed if seizures do not resolve or
consciousness is not regained; Asymptomatic patients present within 2 hours of acute ingestion, treat
prophylactically with the initial ingestion dose (e.g., 70 mg/kg per dose or 5 g) and monitor for 6 hours. Seizures due
to acute mushroom poisoning, by IV injection, ADULT, 25 mg/kg over 15–30 minutes, may be repeated as necessary
to a total dose of 15–20 g daily; ADOLESCENT, CHILD, and INFANT, 25 mg/kg over 15– 30 minutes (maximum initial
dose, 5 g), repeat dose as necessary up to 300 mg/kg daily (maximum daily dose, 15–20 g).

Administration

ORAL › 100 mg tablet (OTC)

Administer whole. Do NOT crush, break, or chew. See Pyridoxine (Vitamin B6) under Vitamins in Chapter 1:
Alimentary Tract and Metabolism for other information.

Indications

Management of isoniazid, hydralazine,
hydrogen sulfide, gynomethrin mushroom, and
theophylline poisoning; to prevent development of
peripheral neuritis in patients receiving isoniazid or
hydralazine

Contraindications

Breastfeeding, pregnancy, premature
neonates, renal impairment

Mechanism of Action

A naturally occurring, water-soluble vitamin. It is converted in erythrocytes to its active moiety, pyridoxal phosphate,
which acts as coenzymes in metabolic processes, including metabolism of fat, protein, and carbohydrate.
