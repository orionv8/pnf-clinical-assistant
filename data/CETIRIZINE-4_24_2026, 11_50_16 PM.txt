Dose Adjustment

Geriatric (≥77 years):
Use lower dose (5 mg daily).

Renal and Hepatic Impairment:
For mild-to-moderate impairment, dose reduction is warranted (5 mg daily).

Indications

Perennial and seasonal allergic rhinitis; other allergic symptoms such as hay fever, conjunctivitis, and chronic
idiopathic urticaria

Contraindications

Known hypersensitivity reactions to cetirizine, levocetirizine, or hydroxyzine or any component of the formulation;
severe renal impairment

Precautions

WARNING: May cause CNS depression, which may impair physical or mental abilities.

CNS stimulation may occur with antihistamines, especially in children (caution is advised in patients suffering from
epilepsy); Children and elderly (risk of sedation and anticholinergic effects are increased; not recommended
for children <12 months); Renal impairment; hepatic impairment; excess alcohol intake; avoid use of other sedative
drugs; Lactation (excreted into breast milk).

SKILLED TASKS. May impair ability to perform certain tasks, e.g., operating machinery or driving.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
Dizziness, drowsiness, dryness of mouth, fatigue, headache, insomnia, malaise, nausea, pharyngitis, somnolence

Less Common:
Abdominal pain, anorexia, arthralgia, chest pain, diarrhea, dyspepsia, dyspnea, epistaxis, fever, flushing, increased
appetite, myalgia, tachycardia, thirst, vomiting, weight gain

Rare:
Dystonia, hemolytic anemia, hepatitis, hypersensitivity (including anaphylaxis and bronchospasm), hypotension, rash,
thrombocytopenia

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with: Enhances therapeutic effect of Cetirizine: Anticholinergics e.g., Ipratropium, Tiotropium
(anticholinergic effect), CNS Depressants e.g., Alcohol, Sedatives (sedative effect)

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs:
Analgesics, Opioids (constipation; urinary retention), CNS Depressants e.g., Alcohol, Sedatives, Warfarin
(increased INR; epistaxis)

Reduces therapeutic effect of Cetirizine:
Acetylcholinesterase Inhibitors (Central)

Avoid concomitant use with:
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Cetirizine:
Doxylamine (CNS depression; psychomotor retardation)

Mechanism of Action

A piperazine-derived, long-acting, second-generation H1 receptor antagonist, which is often better tolerated than
sedating antihistamines due to less sedating and less anticholinergic effects.
