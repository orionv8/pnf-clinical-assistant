BORAGINACEAE)]

Drug Classification

ATC CODE

ANATOMICAL

Herbal Preparations

THERAPEUTIC

Lipid Modifying Agents

Technical Specifications PNF Primary Care Reserved AMs

ORAL › 250 mg tablet (OTC)

Dosage

ORAL › 250 mg tablet (OTC)

Acute diarrhea, colic from acute gastroenteritis, biliary colic, by mouth, ADULT, 15 mg/kg/dose, may repeat after 6
hrs up to 3times a day; CHILD 7–12 years, 15 mg/kg/dose, may repeat after 6 hrs up to 3 times per day.

Administration

ORAL › 250 mg tablet (OTC)

May be taken with or without food.

Dose Adjustment

Clinical trials were performed in patients with normal renal and liver function tests; no reports of adverse effects in
patients with renal and liver dysfunction.

Indications

For the management of acute diarrhea, colic from acute gastroenteritis and biliary colic; anti-motility; antispasmodic.

Contraindications

Severe allergy to tsaang gubat or any of its components.

Precautions

When symptoms persist, or irritation occurs, stop and consult your doctor.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Nausea

Drug Interactions

None known

Mechanism of Action

A natural medicinal preparation from the leaves of Carmona retusa (Vahl) Masam (Fam. Boraginaceae) used for the
treatment of biliary colic.

Pregnancy & Remarks

PREGNANCY CATEGORY

In vitro and animal studies have shown Tsaang Gubat to be non-mutagenic and non clastogenic.
