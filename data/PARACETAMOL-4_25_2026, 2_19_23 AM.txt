Dosage

ORAL › 300 mg tablet (OTC)

Mild-to-moderate pain, pyrexia, by mouth, ADULT, 0.5–1 g every 4–6 hours (maximum, 4 g daily); CHILD 6–12 years,
250–500 mg; CHILD 1–5 years, 120–250 mg; CHILD 3 months to 1 year, 60–125 mg or 10–15 mg/kg per dose; may
repeat doses in children every 4–6 hours if necessary (maximum, 4 doses in 24 hours); by rectum, ADULT,
ADOLESCENT, and CHILD =12 years, 650 mg every 4–6 hours (maximum daily dose, 3,900 mg); CHILD >6 up to 12
years, 325 mg every 4–6 hours (maximum daily dose, 1,625 mg); CHILD >3 to 6 years, 120 mg every 4–6 hours
(maximum daily dose, 600 mg); CHILD and INFANT 12–36 months, 80 mg every 4–6 hours (maximum daily dose, 400
mg); INFANT 6–11 months, 80 mg every 6 hours (maximum daily dose, 320 mg daily); by IV injection, ADULT =50 kg,
650 mg every 4 hours or 1,000 mg every 6 hours (maximum single dose, 1,000 mg per dose; maximum daily dose, 4 g
daily); ADULT <50 kg, 15 mg/kg every 6 hours or 12.5 mg/kg every 4 hours (maximum single dose, 15 mg/kg per dose
or 750 mg/dose; maximum daily dose, 75 mg/kg or 3.75 g); CHILD 2–12 years, 15 mg/kg every 6 hours or 12.5 mg/kg
every 4 hours (maximum single dose, 15 mg/kg per dose or 750 mg; maximum daily dose, 75 mg/kg or 3.75 g). Post-
immunization pyrexia, by mouth, INFANT 2–3 months, 10–15 mg/kg per dose followed by a second dose, if
necessary, 4–6 hours later [NOTE: Warn parents to seek medical advice if pyrexia persists after the second dose].
Treatment of acute migraine attack, by mouth, ADULT, 0.5–1 g at first sign of attack, repeated every 4 to 6 hours if
necessary, (maximum, 4 g daily); CHILD 6–12 years, 250–500 mg at the first sign of attack, repeated every 4–6 hours
if necessary (maximum, 4 doses in 24 hours). Pain or fever, by mouth, pediatric dosing: Weight (In kg or lbs), Age
[Dosage in mg] 2.7–5.3 kg or 6–11 lbs, 0–3 months [40 mg] 5.4–8.1 kg or 12–17 lbs, 4–11 months [80 mg] 8.2–10.8 kg or
16–23 lbs, 1–2 years [120 mg] 10.9–16.3 kg or 24–35 lbs, 2–3 years [160 mg] 16.4–21.7 kg or 36–47 lbs, 4–5 years [240
mg] 21.8–27.2 kg or 48–59 lbs, 6–8 years [320 mg] 27.3–32.6 kg or 60–71 lbs, 9–10 years [400 mg] 32.7–43.2 kg or 72–
95 lbs, 11 years [480 mg] NOTE: Infants <3 months should not be given paracetamol unless advised by a doctor. A
dose of 10 mg/kg (5 mg/kg if jaundiced) is suitable.

Administration

ORAL › 300 mg tablet (OTC)

For oral administration, may be taken with or without food. Taking with food may decrease GI upset. When
administering suspensions, shake well before use. For rectal administration, remove the wrapper and insert the
suppository well up into the rectum. For IV administration, administer by IV infusion only. Administer undiluted
over 15 minutes. Use within 6 hours of opening vial or transferring to another container. For doses <1,000 mg (<50
kg), withdraw the appropriate dose from the vial and place in a separate empty, sterile container prior to
administration. Small volume pediatric doses (up to 600 mg [60 mL]) may be placed in a syringe and infused over 15
minutes via syringe pump. For 1,000 mg doses (=50 kg), insert a vented IV set through vial stopper.

Dose Adjustment

Renal Impairment:
Longer dosing intervals and reduce total doses. Avoid prolonged or repeated use.

Hepatic Impairment:
Use with caution and avoid prolonged or repeated use in any type of liver disease. Contraindicated in severe active
liver disease

Indications

Management of mild-moderate pain, fever.

Contraindications

Severe hepatic impairment or severe active liver disease.

Precautions

WARNING: Massive overdose may cause hepatic necrosis and, less frequently, renal damage. Less overdoses can
frequently cause reversible jaundice.

Has been associated with cases of acute liver failure, at times resulting in liver transplant and death. Most are
associated with the doses that exceed the maximum daily limits, and often involve more than 1 paracetamol-
containing product.

Sodium restriction (soluble paracetamol products may contain large amounts of sodium);

Phenylketonuria (soluble products of paracetamol may contain aspartame);

Alcohol dependence; overdosage;

G6PD deficiency;

Hepatic impairment and renal impairment; (dose-related toxicity;

Avoid large doses, e.g., >4 g daily);

Pregnancy (not known to be harmful);

Lactation (small amount is present in milk; short courses are safe in usual dosage; monitor infant).

Adverse Drug Reactions

[Oral, Rectal]
Common and Less Common: Skin rash, nephrotoxicity (chronic overdose), anemia, hyperuricemia, leukopenia,
neutropenia, renal disease, pancytopenia

Rare: Hypersensitivity reaction

[Inj.]
Common: Hypertension, hypotension, peripheral edema, tachycardia, headache, insomnia, agitation, anxiety, fatigue,
trismus, pruritus, skin rash, hypervolemia, hypoalbuminemia, hypokalemia, hypophosphatemia, nausea, vomiting,
hypomagnesemia, constipation, abdominal pain, diarrhea, oliguria, anemia, infusion site reaction, limb pain, muscle
spasm, periorbital edema, atelectasis, abnormal breath sounds, dyspnea, hypoxia, pleural effusion, pulmonary
edema, stridor, wheezing, fever

Less Common: Anaphylaxis, hepatic injury (dose-related), hypersensitivity reaction, severe dermatological reaction
(acute generalized exanthematous pustulosis, Stevens-Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis)

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Vitamin K Antagonists, e.g., Warfarin (anticoagulant effect)
Increases metabolism of Paracetamol: Barbiturates e.g. Phenobarbital; Carbamazepine
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Paracetamol: Barbiturates; Carbamazepine; Ethyl alcohol (liver damage);
Isoniazid
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Dapsone, Topical; Fosphenytoin-Phenytoin;
Imatinib; Sorafenib (hepatotoxic effect); Nitric Oxide, Prilocaine, Sodium nitrite (significant methemoglobinemia)
Reduces therapeutic effect of Paracetamol: Barbiturates, Carbamazepine

Avoid concomitant use with:
Decreases absorption of Paracetamol: Cholestyramine Resin [administer 1 hour apart to minimize this effect]
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Dasatinib (hepatotoxic effect)
Increases serum concentration of Paracetamol: Dasatinib; Probenecid (increases the potential for formation of the
toxic NAPQI metabolite); Sorafenib

TEST INTERACTION. May cause false-positive urinary 5hydroxyindoleacetic acid.

Mechanism of Action

Inhibits COX-1 and COX-2 and has no significant antiinflammatory activity and is particularly useful for treating mild-
to-moderate pain, for reducing fever, and in patients where salicylates and other NSAIDs are contraindicated.
