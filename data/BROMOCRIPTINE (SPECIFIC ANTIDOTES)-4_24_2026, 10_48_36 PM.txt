Dosage

ORAL › 2.5 mg (as mesilate) tablet (Rx)

Neuroleptic malignant syndrome, by mouth, ADULT, 2.5 mg every 8–12 hours, increased if needed (maximum 45 mg
daily).

Administration

ORAL › 2.5 mg (as mesilate) tablet (Rx)

Administer with food to decrease GI distress. Do NOT discontinue abruptly to prevent acute exacerbation.
Discontinue gradually over a period of 1 week. See Bromocriptine under Anti-Parkinson Drugs – Dopaminergic
Agents in Chapter 10: Nervous System for other information.

Dose Adjustment

Hepatic Impairment: May require dose adjustment. Undergoes extensive hepatic metabolism. Use with caution.

Indications

Treatment of neuroleptic malignant syndrome

Contraindications

Syncopal migraine; uncontrolled hypertension; postpartum women with a history of coronary artery disease or other
severe cardiovascular conditions

Mechanism of Action

A semisynthetic ergot alkaloid derivative and a dopamine receptor agonist, which activates postsynaptic dopamine
receptors in the tuberoinfundibular and nigrostriatal pathways, inhibiting prolactin secretion and enhancing
coordinated motor control.
