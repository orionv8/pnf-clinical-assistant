Technical Specifications PNF Primary Care Reserved AMs

ORAL › 2.5 mg tablet (Rx)

Dosage

ORAL › 2.5 mg tablet (Rx)

Migraine, by mouth, ADULT >18 years, 1.25–2.5 mg (maximum single dose, 5 mg), may repeat in 2 hours if the
migraine headache has not resolved or returns after transient improvement (maximum daily dose 10 mg).

Administration

ORAL › 2.5 mg tablet (Rx)

Administer as soon as migraine headache starts. May be broken in half to achieve a smaller initial dose. NOTE: Use
for =10 days per month may lead to worsening of headaches, e.g., medication overuse headache. Withdrawal
treatment may be necessary in the setting of overuse. If a patient does not respond to the first dose, the
diagnosis of migraine should be reconsidered.

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric:
Initiate therapy at the low end of the dosing range.

Renal Impairment:
Zolmitriptan clearance is reduced in patients with severe renal impairment (CrCl 5–25 mL/min.)

Hepatic Impairment:
For moderate to severe impairment, initial dose is 1.25 mg.
Maximum daily dose in severe impairment is 5 mg.

Indications

Acute treatment of migraine with or without aura.

Contraindications

Ischemic coronary artery disease; coronary artery vasospasm, including Prinzmetal variant angina, or other
significant underlying cardiovascular disease; Wolff-Parkinson-White syndrome or arrhythmias associated with
other cardiac accessory conduction pathway disorders; peripheral vascular disease; ischemic bowel disease;
uncontrolled hypertension; recent use (within 24 hours) of other 5-HT1 agonist or an ergotamine-containing
medication; history of stroke, transient ischemic attack, or history of hemiplegic or basilar migraine; co-
administration or use within 2 weeks of MAO-A inhibitor therapy

Precautions

Cardiac events (coronary artery vasospasm, transient ischemia, MI, ventricular tachycardia or fibrillation, cardiac
arrest, and death have been reported within a few hours’ administration); elevated blood pressure; coronary artery
disease (administer first dose in the healthcare provider's office).

Cerebrovascular events (cerebral or subarachnoid hemorrhage and stroke have been reported, some fatal); visual
effects; vasospasm-related events (have been reported); hepatic impairment; serotonin syndrome.

Elderly, Pregnancy; lactation.

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Zolmitriptan: 5HT3 Antagonists e.g., Ondansetron, Antipsychotic Agents
(serotonergic effect)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Zolmitriptan (serotonergic syndrome) 5HT3 Antagonists e.g.,
Ondansetron, Antipsychotic Agents
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Antipsychotic Agents (enhances dopamine
blockade, possibly increasing the risk for neuroleptic malignant syndrome) Metoclopramide (serotonin syndrome;
neuroleptic malignant syndrome)

Avoid concomitant use with:
Decreases metabolism of Zolmitriptan: MAO Inhibitors e.g Phenelzine
Enhances therapeutic effect of Zolmitriptan: Ergot Derivatives (vasoconstricting effect)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Zolmitriptan: Dapoxetine, Other Serotonin Modulators [except Tedizolid]
(serotonin syndrome)

Mechanism of Action

Zolmitriptan is a selective 5-HT1B/1D/1F receptor agonist that causes vasoconstriction of cranial arteries and
sensory nerves of the trigeminal system. This causes vasoconstriction and reduces inflammation associated with
relief of migraine.
