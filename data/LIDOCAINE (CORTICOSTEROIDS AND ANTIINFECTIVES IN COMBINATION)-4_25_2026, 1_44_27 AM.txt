Administration

TOPIC › 10% spray (as hydrochloride), 50 mL (OTC)

For external use only. Apply a moderately thick layer to affected area (~1/8 inch thick), and allow time for numbness
to develop, approximately 20–60 minutes. Reapply if needed. It is unnecessary to dry the site prior to application.
Do NOT use in large quantities, particularly over raw surfaces or blistered areas. Avoid contact with eyes, in case
of accidental contact with eyes, rinse immediately with copious amounts of eyewash and seek treatment by an eye
care specialist. Keep out of reach of children. If swallowed, get medical help or contact a Poison Control Center
right away. See Lidocaine under Anesthetics – Anesthetics, Local (Amides) in Chapter 10: Nervous System for
other information.

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric:
Administer reduced doses commensurate with their age
and physical status.

Indications

Short duration local anesthesia (10 to 15 minutes) of the oral mucous membrane.

Contraindications

Seizures; liver disease; pregnancy; lactation.

Mechanism of Action

A moderately long-acting, local anesthetic, which blocks initiation and transmission of nerve impulses at the site of
application by stabilizing neuronal membrane.
