Contraindications

Patients receiving corticosteroids, immunosuppressive drugs or undergoing radiotherapy, febrile states, pregnancy,
acute infectious diseases, leukemia, severe anemia, severe blood diseases, severe renal impairment,
decompensated heart diseases, patients receiving gammaglobulin or blood transfusion, or known allergies to
vaccine components, and anaphylactic reactions to neomycin.

Precautions

May cause mild pain and tenderness at the injection site.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Fever, arthralgia, arthritis, lymphadenopathy, myalgia, paresthesia.

Less Common: Rash.

Rare: Encephalitis, thrombocytopenia, anaphylactic reactions, urticarial, pruritus, and allergic rash.

Drug Interactions

Immunoglobulins- risk of inactivation

Mechanism of Action

The vaccine is prepared from the live, attenuated strains of Edmonston-Zagreb measles virus and Wistar RA 27/3
rubella virus. Both measles and rubella viruses are propagated on human diploid cells.
