Administration

I-VASC › 636 mg/mL equivalent to 300 mg/mL iodine, 50 mL vial (Rx)

For IV use only. Use sterile technique for all handling and administration. Warm and administer at body or room
temperature. Visually inspect for particulate matter or discoloration before administration. Inspect glass and
plastic containers prior to use for breakage or other damage and do not use damaged containers. Do NOT admix
with other drugs, solutions, or total parenteral nutrition mixtures. Avoid extravasation when injecting, especially
in patients with severe arterial or venous disease. Discard unused portion after use. NOTE: Patients should be
well hydrated prior to and following administration.

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric:
Start at the low end of dosing range, reflecting the greater frequency of decreased hepatic, renal or cardiac function,
and of concomitant disease or other drug therapy.

Pheochromocytoma:
Administer absolute minimum amount required.

Indications

Radiocontrast media for cerebral and peripheral arteriography, coronary arteriography, and left ventriculography; for
angiocardiography; for CT imaging of the head and body, venography, and IV excretory urography; for IV digital
subtraction angiography (IV-DSA)

Contraindications

Symptomatic hyperthyroidism

Precautions

WARNING:
NOT for intrathecal use. Inadvertent intrathecal administration may cause death, convulsions, cerebral hemorrhage,
coma, paralysis, arachnoiditis, acute renal failure, cardiac arrest, seizures, rhabdomyolysis, hyperthermia, and brain
edema.

WARNING:
Procedures involving radiopaque diagnostic agents should only be done by qualified personnel with in- depth

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs:
Cholecystographic Agents (renal toxicity), Metformin (lactic acidosis)

Avoid concomitant use with:
Reduces therapeutic effect of Radioactive Iodine (interferes with thyroid uptake)

TEST INTERACTION.
May interfere with iodine uptake studies, which depend on iodine estimations for up to 16 days following
administration.

Mechanism of Action

A non ionic radiographic contrast agent that opacifies vessels in the path of the flow of ioversol resulting in
adiographic visualization of the internal structures.
