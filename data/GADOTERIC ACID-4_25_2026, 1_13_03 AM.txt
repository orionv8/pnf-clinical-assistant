Administration

IV › 0.5mmol/mL, 10 mL vials (Rx)

Infuse IV bolus at a rate of 1-2 mL per second. To ensure complete injection, follow with saline flush.

Dose Adjustment

Renal impairment:
Dose adjustment is not recommended. However, risk for NSF development increases as renal function decreases.
Gadoteric acid is removed by hemodialysis.

Indications

Contrast medium for magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) in central nervous system (CNS) and whole body indications
including angiography.

Contraindications

Hypersensitivity to gadoteric acid or any component of the formulation.

Precautions

May cause extravasation, gadolinium-retention, hypersensitivity reactions, and nephrogenic systemic fibrosis;
Use in caution in patients with renal impairment.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Less Common:
Headache.

Rare:
Nausea, injection site reactions, burning sensation, feeling cold, rash, somnolence, fatigue, dizziness, vomiting,
pruritus, asthenia, paraesthesia, dysgeusia, pain in extremity, anxiety, hypertension, palpitations, and oropharyngeal
discomfort.

Drug Interactions

None known.

Mechanism of Action

Gadolinium-based contrast agent for use in MRI of the brain, spine, and associated tissues to detect and visualize
areas with disruption of the blood brain barrier and/or vascularity.
