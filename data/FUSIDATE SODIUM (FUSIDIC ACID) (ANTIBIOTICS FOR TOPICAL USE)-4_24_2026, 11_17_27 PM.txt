Dosage

TOPIC › [Access] 2% cream, 5 g tube (Rx)

Skin infection, by topical application, ADULT, apply to the affected area 2–3 times daily for 7 days; may be used with
or without covering dressing.

Administration

TOPIC › [Access] 2% cream, 5 g tube (Rx)

For external use only. Observe caution when applying in the eye region; may cause eye irritation

Indications

Treatment of skin infections caused by staphylococci, streptococci, Corynebacterium minutissimum; impetigo;
infected wounds; folliculitis; boils; sycosis barbae; carbuncles; hidradenitis; paronychia; erythrasma

Precautions

Hepatic disease (monitor liver function); Neonates; Lactation.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Rashes, irritation
Less Common: Hypersensitivity reactions

Drug Interactions

Avoid concomitant use with:
Ciprofloxacin (antagonistic activity)

Mechanism of Action

An antibiotic derived from Fusidium coccineum, which is active against several gram-positive organisms and can
penetrate intact skin. It acts by disrupting translocation of peptide subunits and elongating the peptide chain of
susceptible bacteria, inhibiting protein synthesis.
