Dosage

IM / SC › 25 mcg/0.5 mL Solution for injection, 0.5 mL pre‐ filled syringe or single dose vial (Rx)

Primary immunization, by IM injection, CHILD ≥12 months to <6 years (previously unvaccinated), two 0.5 mL doses,
administered at least 2 months apart; CHILD 7–11 months (previously unvaccinated), three 0.5 mL doses,
administer first 2 doses at least 1 month apart, administer the third dose after 1 year of age, separated from the
second dose by at least 2 months; INFANT 6 weeks to 6 months, -Three-dose primary series, three 0.5 mL doses,
followed by a booster dose (for a total of 4 doses); may administer first dose as early as 6 weeks of age, but is
typically given at 2 months of age; administer second and third doses at 4 and 6 months of age; administer booster
dose at 12 to 15 months of age; allow a minimum interval of 1 month between each of the first 3 doses, and 6
months between third dose and booster dose; -Two-dose primary series, two 0.5 mL doses, followed by a booster
dose (for a total of 3 doses); may administer first dose as early as 6 weeks of age, but is typically given at 2 months
of age; administer second dose at 4 months of age; administer booster dose at 11–12 months of age; allow a
minimum interval of 2 months between the first 2 doses, and 6 months between second dose and booster dose.

Administration

IM / SC › 25 mcg/0.5 mL Solution for injection, 0.5 mL pre‐ filled syringe or single dose vial (Rx)

Administer by IM injection only, preferably into the anterolateral aspect of the thigh in infants and into the deltoid
in children. Do NOT inject by IV or ID. Avoid SC route. Shake well prior to use. See Pneumococcal Conjugated
Vaccine under Vaccines – Bacterial Vaccines in Chapter 7: Anti-infectives for Systemic Use for other information.

Indications

Immunization of infants and children against
Streptococcus pneumoniae infection and invasive diseases caused by serotypes included in the vaccine

Contraindications

Hypersensitivity to any component of the vaccine

Mechanism of Action

An inactivated bacterial vaccine that promotes active immunization against invasive disease caused by S.
pneumoniae.
