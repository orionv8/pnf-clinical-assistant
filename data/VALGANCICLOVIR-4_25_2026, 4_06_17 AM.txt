Dosage

ORAL › 450 mg Tablet (Rx)

Cytomegalovirus Retinitis in Patients with AIDS, by mouth, ADULT, induction dose 900 mg every 12 hours for 21
days. Maintenance dose 900 mg every day following induction treatment, or in patients with inactive
Cytomegalovirus retinitis. Cytomegalovirus prevention in solid organ transplant: Kidney and kidney-pancreas
transplantation, by mouth, ADULT, 900 mg once a day; begin within 10 days of transplant until 200 days post-
transplant; INFANT, <4 months old: Safety and efficacy not established; CHILD, 4 months-16 years old, daily dose
(mg) = 7 x BSA x CrCl; not to exceed 900mg/day; begin within 10 days of transplant and continue until 200 days
post-transplant. Heart transplantation, by mouth, ADULT, 900 mg once a day. Begin within 10 days of transplant
until 100 days post transplant. CHILD, 1 month-16 years old, daily dose (mg) = 7 x BSA x CrCl; not to exceed 900
mg/day; begin within 10 days of transplant and continue until 100 days post-transplant.

Administration

ORAL › 450 mg Tablet (Rx)

Administer orally with food. Do NOT break or crush the tablets. Maintain adequate hydration.

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric:
Start at lower end of dosing range.
Renal Impairment:
CrCl 40-59mL/min: 450 mg every 12 hours (induction),
THEN 450mg/day.
CrCl 25-39mL/min: 450 mg daily (induction), THEN 450 mg
every 2 days.
CrCl 10-24mL/min: 450 mg every 2 days (induction), THEN
450 mg 2 times per week.
<10mL/minute (on hemodialysis): Not recommended.

Indications

Treatment of cytomegalovirus (CMV) retinitis in patients with acquired immunodeficiency syndrome (AIDS).

Precautions

WARNING: May cause dose- or therapy-limiting
granulocytopenia, anemia, and/or
thrombocytopenia. May be teratogenic,
carcinogenic and cause aspermatogenesis.
Monitor patients for hematologic toxicity; female infertility,
aspermatogenesis;
Use with caution in patients with preexisting cytopenias;
concomitant use nephrotoxic drugs;
May be teratogenic or embryotoxic;
Avoid pregnancy; male patients advised to use barrier method during and for 90 days after treatment;
Maintain adequate hydration.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Fever, diarrhea, anorexia, vomiting,
thrombocytopenia, leukopenia, anemia, neutropenia,
retinal detachment, sepsis, diaphoresis.
Less Common: Chills, neuropathy, pruritus.
Rare: Anaphylaxis, alopecia, arrhythmia, bronchospasm,
cardiac arrest, cataracts, cholestasis, coma, dyspnea,
edema, encephalopathy, exfoliative dermatitis,
extrapyramidal symptoms, hepatitis, hepatic failure,
pancytopenia, pulmonary fibrosis, seizure, urticaria, SJS,
torsades de pointes, renal failure, visual loss.
