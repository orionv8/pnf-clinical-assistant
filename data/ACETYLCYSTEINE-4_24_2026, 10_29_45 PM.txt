Dosage

ORAL › 600 mg effervescent tablet (Rx)

Poisoning, by mouth, ADULT, 72-hour regimen consisting of 18 doses (total dose, 1330 mg/kg), initially administer
140 mg/kg loading dose; then administer 70 mg/kg every 4 hours as maintenance dose; by IV infusion, ADULT, 21-
hour regimen consisting of 3 doses (total dose, 300 mg/kg), initially administer 150 mg/kg in 200 mL D5W over 60
minutes as loading dose (maximum, 15 g); followed by a second dose of 50 mg/kg in 500 mL D5W over 4 hours; then
administer a third dose of 100 mg/kg in 1000 mL D5W over 16 hours (maximum, 10 g).

Administration

ORAL › 600 mg effervescent tablet (Rx)

For oral administration, administer as 5% solution within 1 hour of preparation. May be diluted with cola, orange
juice, or other soft drink. If patient vomits within 1 hour of administration, re-administer dose. For IV
administration, use a slow infusion rate to avoid hypersensitivity reactions. Dilute with D5W or physiologic
solution. Undiluted acetylcysteine solution (IV) is hyperosmolar. Adjust as instructed prior to administration. In
patients who continue to experience symptoms of hepatotoxicity or elevated liver function tests after a 72-hour
oral or 21-hour IV regimen, extending the treatment course may be appropriate, specifically in patients with a
suspected massive overdose, concomitant ingestion of other substances, or patients with preexisting liver
disease. NOTE: Consultation with a poison control center or clinical toxicologist is highly recommended to
determine optimal patient care.

Precautions

Anaphylactoid reactions; Acute acetaminophen poisoning (treatment should begin within 8 hours of ingestion or as
soon as possible after ingestion); Repeated supratherapeutic ingestion (RSTI) of acetaminophen; Lactation (use with
caution).

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
Anaphylactoid reaction, flushing, tachycardia, edema, tachycardia, urticaria, rash, pruritus, nausea, vomiting,
pharyngitis, rhinorrhea, rhonchi, throat tightness

Less Common:
Anaphylaxis, angioedema, bronchospasm, chest tightness, cough, dizziness, dyspnea, headache, hypotension,
respiratory distress, stridor, wheezing

Drug Interactions

No known significant interactions

Mechanism of Action

A cysteine derivative with a free thiol (-SH) nucleophilic group able to interact directly with electrophilic group of
oxidant radicals. It acts as a hepatoprotective agent that restores hepatic glutathione. It serves as a glutathione
substitute and enhances the nontoxic sulfate conjugation of acetaminophen.
