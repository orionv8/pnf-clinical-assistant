Indications

Skin and soft tissue infections caused by community-associated methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA);
alternative for anaerobic infections. Alternative to macrolides for severe penicillin allergy; recurrent pharyngitis

Contraindications

History of ulcerative or pseudomembranous colitis.

Precautions

WARNING:
Clostridium difficile-associated diarrhea (CDAD) has been reported and may range in severity from mild diarrhea to
fatal colitis. This may occur during treatment or for 2 months after discontinuation of treatment. (more likely to be
refractory to antimicrobial therapy and may require colectomy).
Reserve clindamycin for serious infection where less toxic antibiotics are inappropriate.
Avoid use for non-bacterial infection including most upper respiratory tract infections.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
Abdominal pain, diarrhea, hypotension, jaundice, nausea, pruritus, rash, urticaria, vomiting.
Frequency not defined: Agranulocytosis, C. difficileassociated diarrhea, eosinophilia (transient), esophagitis, fungal
overgrowth, granulocytopenia, hypersensitivity, neutropenia, polyarthritis, pseudomembranous colitis, SJS,
thrombocytopenia, renal dysfunction.

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Reduces therapeutic effect of Conjugated estrogens or estradiol or ethinylestradiol
Enhances therapeutic effect of the following drugs: Digoxin, Warfarin (decreased vitamin-K producing intestinal
flora may increase INR after a few days)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Neuromuscular blockers (e.g., pancuronium) (possible serious or
lifethreatening interaction; risk of respiratory depression) [use alternatives if possible]
Avoid concomitant use with:
Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs:
Typhoid vaccine (Live) (antagonistic effects) (high likelihood of serious or life-threatening interaction), BCG vaccine
(Live) (antagonistic effects) (high likelihood of serious or life-threatening interaction), Erythromycin (antagonistic
effects)

Mechanism of Action

Lincosamide is an antibiotic that binds to the 50S ribosomal subunit at a site closely related to that at which
macrolides act resulting in bacteriostatic inhibition of microbial protein synthesis.
