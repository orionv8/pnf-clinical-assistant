Dosage

ORAL › 5 mg tablet (Rx)

Asthma or bronchospasm, by mouth, ADULT and ADOLESCENT >15 years, 5 mg per dose every 6 hours 3 times daily;
if side effects occur, reduce dose to 2.5 mg every 6 hours (maximum, 15 mg in 24 hours); ADOLESCENT 12–15 years,
2.5 mg every 6 hours 3 times daily (maximum, 7.5 mg in 24 hours); CHILD <12 years, initially 0.05 mg/kg per dose 3
times daily, increased gradually as required (maximum, 0.15 mg/kg per dose 3–4 times daily or a total of 5 mg per 24
hours.

Administration

ORAL › 5 mg tablet (Rx)

Give drug around-the-clock to promote decrease in variation in peak and trough serum levels. See Terbutaline
under Other Gynecologicals – Sympathomimetics, Labor Repressants in Chapter 5: Genito Urinary System and Sex
Hormones for other information.

Indications

Bronchodilator in reversible airway obstruction and bronchial asthma

Contraindications

Acute or chronic use in the prevention, treatment, or management of preterm labor

Mechanism of Action

A direct acting sympathomimetic drug with beta-adrenergic activity, selectively on beta2 receptors.
