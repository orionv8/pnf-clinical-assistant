Dosage

ORAL › 200 mg tablet (Rx)

NOTE: MR tablets are designed to be taken twice daily due to the controlled release of the active substance.
Epilepsy, by mouth, ADULT, initially 100–200 mg once or twice daily, slowly raise until an optimal response is
obtained (generally at 400 mg twice or thrice daily); ADOLESCENT >12 years, 5–10 mg/kg daily divided every 12
hours then increase gradually to maintenance of 10– 30 mg/kg daily divided every 8 or 6 hours (maximum, 1 g daily
for 12–15 years old and 1.2 g daily if >16 years old); CHILD 6–12 years, initially 10 mg/kg daily in 2–4 divided doses,
increase by 5 mg/kg weekly until therapeutic levels; CHILD <5 years, initially 5 mg/kg daily in 2–4 divided doses,
increase every 5–7 days by 5 mg/kg based on response.

Administration

ORAL › 200 mg tablet (Rx)

May be taken during, after, or between meals. For the syrup formulation, shake well before use. Administer tablets
with little liquid. Swallow MR tablets unchewed with a little liquid. For existing anticonvulsant therapy, add
carbamazepine gradually while existing anticonvulsant is maintained or gradually decreased, except with
phenytoin, which may need to be increased or decreased depending on phenytoin serum levels. See
Carbamazepine under Analgesics – Other Analgesics and Antipyretics: Drugs for Neuropathic Pain in Nervous
System for other information.

Indications

Management of epilepsy; complex or simple partial seizures with or without secondary generalization; generalized
tonic-clonic seizures; mixed forms of seizures.

NOTE: NOT effective in absence seizures (petit mal) and myoclonic seizures.

Contraindications

Atrioventricular block; bone marrow depression; acute intermittent porphyria; combination with MAO Inhibitors e.g.,
Phenezline, Selegeline.

Mechanism of Action

Carbamazepine is an iminostilbene that limits the firing of action potentials evoked by a sustained depolarization in
cortical neurons. It acts by limiting influx of sodium ions or by slowing the rate of recovery of Na+ channels.
