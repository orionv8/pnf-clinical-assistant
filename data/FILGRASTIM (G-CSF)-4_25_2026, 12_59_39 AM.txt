Dosage

IV / SC › 150 micrograms/0.6 mL vial (Rx)

Myelosuppressive chemotherapy recipient with non-myeloid malignancies, by SC injection or IV infusion, ADULT/
CHILD/ ADOLESCENT, 5 micrograms/kg per day, may increase by 5 micrograms/kg for each chemotherapy cycle
according to the duration and severity of the neutropenia; continue for up to 14 days until the absolute neutrophil
count (ANC) reaches 10,000/mm3; discontinue if the ANC surpasses 10,000/mm3 after the expected
chemotherapy-induced neutrophil nadir. AML following induction or consolidation chemotherapy, by SC injection
or IV infusion, ADULT/ CHILD/ ADOLESCENT, 5 micrograms/kg per day, may increase by 5 micrograms/kg (for each
chemotherapy cycle) according to the duration and severity of the neutropenia; continue for up to 14 days until the
absolute neutrophil count (ANC) reaches 10,000/mm3; discontinue if the ANC surpasses 10,000/mm3 after the
expected chemotherapy-induced neutrophil nadir. Bone marrow transplantation, by IV infusion, ADULT, 10
micrograms/kg per day, =24 hours after chemotherapy and =24 hours after bone marrow infusion; adjust the dose
according to the duration and severity of neutropenia; If ANC >1,000/mm3 for 3 consecutive days, reduce dose to 5
micrograms/kg per day; If ANC remains >1,000/mm3 for 3 more consecutive days, discontinue; If ANC decreases to
<1,000/mm3, resume at 5 micrograms/kg per day; If ANC decreases to <1,000/mm3 during the 5 micrograms/kg per
day dose, increase dose to 10 micrograms/kg per day and follow the above steps. Hematopoietic radiation injury
syndrome, by SC injection, ADULT, 10 micrograms/kg once daily; begin as soon as possible after suspected or
confirmed radiation doses >2 gray (Gy) and continue until ANC remains >1,000/mm3 for 3 consecutive CBCs or ANC
exceeds 10,000/mm3 after the radiation-induced nadir. [NOTE: Initiate within 24 hours of exposure of a dose =2 Gy
and/or significant decrease in absolute lymphocyte count, or for anticipated neutropenia <500/mm3 for =7 days.]
Peripheral blood progenitor cell collection and therapy, by SC injection, ADULT, 10 micrograms/kg daily, usually for
6–7 days with apheresis occurring on days 5, 6, and 7; begin at least 4 days before the first apheresis and continue
until the last apheresis; discontinue for WBC >100,000/mm3. Severe chronic neutropenia, congenital, by SC
injection, ADULT/ADOLESCENT/ CHILD/INFANT 7 months & older, initially 6 micrograms/kg per day in 2 divided
doses; adjust dose based on ANC and clinical response; mean dose is 6 micrograms/kg daily; Severe chronic
neutropenia, idiopathic, by SC injection, ADULT/ADOLESCENT/ CHILD/INFANT 7 months & older, initially 5
micrograms/kg once daily; adjust dose based on ANC and clinical response; mean dose is 1.2 micrograms/kg daily;
Severe chronic neutropenia, cyclic, by SC injection, ADULT/ADOLESCENT/ CHILD/INFANT 7 months & older, initially
5 micrograms/kg once daily; adjust the dose based on ANC and clinical response; mean dose is 2.1 micrograms/kg
daily. Primary prophylaxis for children with high likelihood of febrile neutropenia (e.g., children receiving dose-
intense chemotherapy for Ewing’s sarcoma/Primitive neuroectodermal tumors, rhabdomyosarcoma), 5-10 mcg/kg
48-72 hours after completion of chemotherapy.

Administration

IV / SC › 150 micrograms/0.6 mL vial (Rx)

For IV administration, administer as a short infusion over 15–30 minutes for chemotherapy-induced neutropenia,
by continuous infusion for chemotherapy-induced neutropenia, or as an infusion of no longer than 24 hours for
bone marrow transplantation. For SC administration, administer into the outer upper arm, abdomen (except within
2 inches of navel), front middle thigh, or the upper outer buttocks area. Rotate the injection site. Do NOT inject
into areas that are tender, red, bruised, hardened, or scarred, or sites with stretch marks. Do NOT administer
earlier than 24 hours after or in the 24 hours prior to cytotoxic chemotherapy. Should NOT be routinely used in the
treatment of established neutropenic fever. Vials may be diluted in D5W to >15 mcg/mL for infusion. DO NOT dilute
with saline at any time, because the product may precipitate. For 5-15 mcg/mL dilution: Add human albumin to a
final concentration of 2 mg/mL to protect from adsorption to plastic materials

Dose Adjustment

Renal Impairment:
If patient develops glomerulonephritis, consider dose reduction or treatment interruption.

Indications

To decrease the incidence of infection (neutropenic fever) in patients with non-myeloid malignancies receiving
myelosuppressive chemotherapy associated with a significant incidence of severe neutropenia with fever; to
reduce the time to neutrophil recovery and the duration of fever following induction or consolidation chemotherapy
in adults with acute myeloid leukemia (AML); to reduce the duration of neutropenia and neutropenia-related events
in patients with nonmyeloid malignancies receiving myeloablative chemotherapy followed by marrow
transplantation; to increase survival in patients acutely exposed to myelosuppressive doses of radiation; for
mobilization of peripheral blood progenitor cells (PBPCs) for collection by leukapheresis in autologous or allogeneic
hematopoietic cell transplantation; long-term administration to reduce the incidence and duration of neutropenic
complications in symptomatic patients with congenital, cyclic, or idiopathic neutropenia.

Contraindications

History of serious allergic reactions to human granulocyte colony-stimulating factors, such as filgrastim or
pegfilgrastim, or any component of the formulation

Precautions

Allergic reactions (serious allergic reactions, including anaphylaxis may occur; may be managed

symptomatically with antihistamines, steroids, bronchodilators, or epinephrine; reactions may recur within days
after initial allergy management has been stopped; permanently discontinue in serious allergic reactions).

Alveolar hemorrhage (pulmonary infiltrates, hemoptysis may occur; resolved upon discontinuation)

Respiratory distress syndrome

Capillary leak syndrome (hypoalbuminemia, hypotension, edema, or hemoconcentration may occur; may vary in
frequency and severity; may be life-threatening if treatment is delayed)

Cutaneous vasculitis (generally occurs in patients with severe chronic neutropenia on chronic therapy; withhold
treatment if cutaneous vasculitis occurs)

Hematologic effects (excessive leukocytosis or thrombocytopenia may occur; discontinue when absolute neutrophil
count (ANC) exceeds 10,000/mm3 after the ANC nadir has occurred; monitor CBC twice weekly during therapy;
monitor platelet counts)

Severe chronic neutropenia (myelodysplastic syndrome (MDS) and acute myeloid leukemia (AML) have been reported)

Sickle cell disorders (may precipitate severe sickle cell crises, some fatal; discontinue in patients undergoing sickle
cell crisis)

Nephrotoxicity (glomerulonephritis, resulting in azotemia, hematuria, or proteinuria may occur; if glomerulonephritis
is suspected, evaluate for cause).

Splenic rupture (if upper abdominal pain, left upper quadrant pain, or shoulder tip pain occurs, withhold treatment
and evaluate for enlarged spleen or splenic rupture).

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Alopecia, anemia, antibody development, arthralgia, back pain, cardiac arrhythmia, chest pain, dizziness,
dyspnea, epistaxis, erythema, cough, fatigue, fever, headache, hypertension, hypoesthesia, increased lactate
dehydrogenase and uric acid (mild-to-moderate; reversible), leukocytosis, limb pain, maculopapular rash,
musculoskeletal pain, MI, muscle spam, nausea, ostealgia (dose and cycle related), pain, petechia, splenomegaly, skin
rash, thrombocytopenia, transfusion reaction, upper respiratory tract infection, urinary tract infection, vomiting

Less Common: Anaphylaxis, capillary leak syndrome, cerebral hemorrhage, constipation, decreased bone mineral
density, decreased hemoglobin, diarrhea, ethema nodosum, exacerbation of psoriasis, facial edema, hematuria,
hemoptysis, hepatomegaly, hypersensitivity reaction, renal insufficiency, hypersensitivity angiitis, injection site
reaction, hypotension, malaise, mouth pain, oropharyngeal pain, osteoporosis, peripheral edema, proteinuria,
pulmonary hemorrhage, pulmonary infiltrates, severe sickle cell crisis, respiratory distress syndrome, splenic
rupture, Sweet’s syndrome, tachycardia, weakness, urticaria, wheezing

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Bleomycin (pulmonary toxicity), Cyclophosphamide

Avoid concomitant use with:
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Topotecan
Drugs that may potentiate the release of neutrophils‚ such as lithium‚ should be used with caution

DRUG-DIAGNOSTIC TEST INTERACTION. May interfere with bone imaging studies, increased hematopoietic activity of
the bone marrow may appear as transient positive bone imaging changes.

Mechanism of Action

A granulocyte colony stimulating factor (G-CSF) produced by recombinant DNA technology. It stimulates the
production, maturation, and activation of neutrophils.
