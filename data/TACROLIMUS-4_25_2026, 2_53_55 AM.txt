Dosage

ORAL › 1 mg capsule (Rx)

Liver transplant, by mouth, ADULT, initially 0.1–0.15 mg/kg daily in 2 divided doses, given every 12 hours; titrate to
target trough concentrations; CHILD, initially 0.15–0.20 mg/kg daily in 2 divided doses, given every 12 hours; titrate
to target trough concentrations [NOTE: Initiate therapy in pediatric patients at the HIGH end of the recommended
adult dosing ranges]. Heart transplant, by mouth, ADULT, in combination with azathioprine or mycophenolate
mofetil, initially 0.075 mg/kg daily in 2 divided doses, given every 12 hours; titrate to target trough concentrations.
Kidney transplant, by mouth, ADULT, in combination with azathioprine, initially 0.2 mg/kg daily; in combination with
mycophenolate mofetil, initially 0.1 mg/kg daily; titrate to target trough concentrations; administer in 2 divided
doses, given every 12 hours [NOTE: Administer initial postoperative dose no sooner than 6 hours after liver or
heart transplant and within 24 hours of kidney transplant or until renal function has recovered; titrate to target
trough concentrations.]. Rheumatoid arthritis, by mouth, ADULT, 3 mg once daily. Blood and marrow
transplantation, by mouth, ADULT, 0.15 mg/kg daily in 2 divided doses beginning 2 days prior to transplant, dose
adjusted to maintain trough level between 10-20 ng/mL, up to 3 months, thereafter, adjust dose to keep trough
level around 10 ng/ml.

Administration

ORAL › 1 mg capsule (Rx)

becomes necessary, administer consistently with regard to timing and composition of meals. Food decreases rate
and extent of absorption. High-fat meals have the most pronounced effect. NOTE: If dosed once daily, administer
in the morning. If twice daily, administer 12 hours apart. If the morning and evening doses differ, administer the
larger dose in the morning. If dosed 3 times daily, administer 8 hours apart. For combination therapy with
everolimus in liver transplant, administer both doses at the same time. Immediate release and extended release
capsules are NOT interchangeable or substitutable. Serious adverse events, including organ rejection, may occur.
Limit exposure to sunlight and ultraviolet light by wearing protective clothing and using effective sunscreen to
minimize the risk for malignancy.

Dose Adjustment

Renal Impairment:
Use lowest recommended dose. Additional dose reductions may be necessary.
In patients who have undergone kidney transplant, and with postoperative oliguria, initiate therapy no sooner than 6
hours and within 24 hours post-transplant, or until renal function displays evidence of recovery.

Hepatic Impairment:
Use lowest recommended dose. Additional dose reductions may be necessary.

Indications

Prevention of organ rejection in heart, kidney, or liver transplant; treatment of refractory rejection in kidney or liver
transplant; treatment of active rheumatoid arthritis in adult patients nonresponsive to DMARD therapy or when
DMARD therapy is inappropriate; for the prevention of GVHD in Allogeneic-HSCT, in combination with MTX.

Contraindications

No information found

Precautions

WARNING: Should be administered under the supervision of a physician experienced in immunosuppressive therapy
and organ transplantation in a facility appropriate for monitoring and managing therapy. May contain lactose. NOT
recommended for patients who may be lactose intolerant. May be associated with the development of lymphoma and
other malignancies. Increased risk of infection, such as bacterial, viral, fungal, and protozoal infections, including
opportunistic infections.

Cardiovascular effects (e.g. myocardial hypertrophy, prolongation of the QT/QTc and torsade de pointes);
hypertension

Diabetes mellitus, post-transplant (new-onset diabetes and insulin-dependent post-transplant diabetes mellitus
may occur); hyperkalemia (mild-to-severe); gastrointestinal perforation

Infections (may result in serious and fatal outcomes; latent viral infections may be activated); malignancy (e.g.
lymphoma, skin malignancies, post-transplant lymphoproliferative disorder; pure red cell aplasia / PRCA

Nephrotoxicity

Neurotoxicity (including tremor, headache, coma, delirium, seizures, posterior reversible encephalopathy syndrome).

Enhances therapeutic effect of the following drugs: Moderate Risk QTc-Prolonging Agents, Indeterminate Risk and
Risk Modifying

Mechanism of Action

Suppresses cellular immunity, thus inhibiting T-lymphocyte activation, by binding to an intracellular protein, FKBP-12
and complexes with calcineurin dependent proteins to inhibit calcineurin phosphatase activity.
