Administration

2.5 mg tablet (as base, sodium salt or disodium salt) (Rx)

For oral administration, administer without regard to food. This is the preferred route for low doses. For IV
administration, administer as slow IV push at a rate of 10 mg/minute, as bolus injection, or as 24-hour continuous
infusion. The route and rate of administration depends on indication and/or protocol. For intrathecal
administration, do NOT prepare during the preparation of any other agents. After preparation, store in an isolated
location or container clearly marked with a label "intrathecal" use only. Administer only with other medications
intended for administration into the central nervous system. NOTE: Optimal intrathecal chemotherapy dosing
should be based on age rather than on body surface area (BSA) as CSF volume correlates with age and not to BSA.
Doses =250 mg/m2 (=12 g/m2 in children) are associated with moderate emetic potential. Antiemetics may be
recommended to prevent nausea and vomiting. Doses >500 mg/m2 require leucovorin calcium rescue. Doses
between 100–500 mg/m2 may require leucovorin calcium rescue. Monitoring High Dose Methotrexate Treatment
Plasma MTX levels should also be monitored closely to detect any delay in MTX clearance. Depending on the
treatment protocol, plasma MTX assays may be appropriate at 24, 48, and 72 hours after the start of MTX infusion.
Other protocols may require serum MTX measurements at 36 hours (i.e. 12 hours after the end of a 24-hour
infusion) or at 42 hours from the start. Importantly, leucovorin or folinic acid doses are adjusted based on plasma
MTX drug levels, and other supportivecare measures such as hydration and alkalinization can be fine-tuned to
ensure adequate urine output.

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric:
Reduce recommended doses when initiating therapy due to possible decreased metabolism, reduced renal function,
and presence of interacting diseases and drugs, as well as decreased
CSF volume and turnover. Use caution and monitor closely due to increased risk of toxicity.

Renal Impairment:
For patients with CrCl 46–60 mL/minute, administer 65% of the dose.
For patients with CrCl 31–45 mL/minute and patients on intermittent hemodialysis or continuous renal replacement
therapy (CRRT), administer 50% of the dose.
For patients with CrCl <30 mL/minute, avoid use.

Renal Impairment in Children:
For patients with CrCl 10–50 mL/minute/1.73 m2 and patients on continuous renal replacement therapy (CRRT),
administer 50% of the dose.
For patients with CrCl <10 mL/minute/1.73 m2 andpatients on intermittent hemodialysis or continuous ambulatory
peritoneal dialysis (CAPD), administer 30% of the dose.

Hepatic Impairment:
Use with caution in patients with impaired hepatic function or preexisting hepatic damage.
For patients with bilirubin 3.1–5 mg/dL or transaminases >3 times ULN, administer 75% of the dose.
For patients with bilirubin >5 mg/dL, avoid use.

Indications

Therapy of acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL); prophylaxis and treatment of ALL meningeal leukemia; treatment of
trophoblastic neoplasms (gestational choriocarcinoma, chorioadenoma destruens and hydatidiform mole); breast
cancer; head and neck cancer (epidermoid); cutaneous T-cell lymphoma (advanced mycosis fungoides); lung cancer
(squamous cell and small cell); advanced non-Hodgkin’s lymphomas (NHL); osteosarcoma

Contraindications

Breastfeeding

Precautions

WARNING: Methotrexate should be used only by health care providers whose knowledge and experience include the
use of antimetabolite therapy and only in life-threatening neoplastic diseases due to the possibility of serious toxic,
potentially fatal reactions. Serious and fatal toxicities have occurred at all dose levels.

Errors have occurred (some resulting in death) when methotrexate was administered as a “daily” dose instead of a
“weekly” dose intended for some indications. Do NOT use methotrexate formulations and/or diluents containing
preservatives for intrathecal or high-dose methotrexate therapy. Methotrexate-induced lung disease, including
acute or chronic interstitial pneumonitis, is a potentially dangerous lesion, which may occur acutely at any time
during therapy and may require interruption of treatment and careful investigation. Potentially fatal opportunistic
infections, specially Pneumocystis carinii and Pneumocystis jirovecii pneumonia, may occur. Concurrent
administration with NSAIDs may cause severe bone marrow suppression, aplastic anemia, and GI toxicity; do not
administer NSAIDS prior to or during high-dose methotrexate therapy.

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Methotrexate: Trastuzumab (neutropenic effect)
Enhances therapeutic effect of Tofacitinib (immunosuppressive effect)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Methotrexate: Systemic (hepatotoxicity), Denosumab (serious
infections), Leflunomide (pancytopenia and/or hepatotoxicity), Sulfasalazine (hepatotoxicity)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Tegafur
Reduces absorption of Methotrexate: Bile Acid Sequestrants
Reduces diagnostic effect of Coccidioides immitis Skin Test

Avoid concomitant use with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Methotrexate: Roflumilast (immunosuppressive effect)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Methotrexate: Acitretin (hepatotoxic effect), Cyclosporine, Systemic
(nausea, vomiting, oral ulcers, hepatotoxicity and/or nephrotoxicity), Dipyrone (agranulocytosis, pancytopenia),
Foscarnet (nephrotoxic effect), Pimecrolimus, Sulfonamide derivatives, Tacrolimus (Topical), Trimethoprim
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Clozapine (agranulocytosis), Cyclosporine, Systemic
(nephrotoxicity), Dipyrone (agranulocytosis, pancytopenia), Natalizumab (concurrent infection), Vaccines (Live)
Increases serum concentration of Methotrexate: Cyclosporine (Systemic), Dexketoprofen [avoid concomitant
administration with 15 mg methotrexate weekly or more], Loop Diuretics e.g. Furosemide, NSAIDS, Probenecid,
Salicylates e.g. Aspirin
Increases serum concentration of the following drugs: Cyclosporine (Systemic), Loop Diuretics e.g., Furosemide
Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs: BCG (Intravesical), Loop Diuretics e.g. Furosemide, Vaccines
(Inactivated) [complete all age-appropriate vaccinations at least 2 weeks prior to starting Methotrexate; if
vaccinated during Methotrexate therapy, revaccinate at least 3 months after discontinuation], Vaccines (Live) [do not
use concomitantly with high-dose Methotrexate (=0.4 mg/kg per week)]
Reduces therapeutic effect of Methotrexate: Echinacea
Reduces absorption of Methotrexate: Milk-rich Foods

Mechanism of Action

An antimetabolite antineoplastic that acts as an antagonist of folic acid. Methotrexate competitively inhibits the
enzyme dihydrofolatereductase and prevents the formation of tetrahydrofolate, subsequently inhibiting the
formation of DNA and RNA. It is most active against cells in the S phase of the cell cycle.
