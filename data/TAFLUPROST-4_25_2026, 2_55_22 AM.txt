Dosage

OPHTHALM › 15 mcg/mL, 0.3 mL bottle (single dose) (Rx)

Elevated intraocular pressure, ADULT, instill affected eye(s) once daily in the evening.

Administration

OPHTHALM › 15 mcg/mL, 0.3 mL bottle (single dose) (Rx)

Instill into conjunctival sac of affected eye(s). Do not exceed once daily dosing regimen. If other ophthalmics are
prescribed, each should be administered 5 minutes apart. Use immediately after opening preservative-free
product.

Dose Adjustment

Renal and Hepatic impairment:
No dosage adjustments provided in the manufacturer’s labeling.

Indications

Reduction of intraocular pressure in patients with open-angle glaucoma or ocular hypertension.

Contraindications

None.

Precautions

May cause changes to pigmented tissues and macular edema;
Use in caution in patients with intraocular inflammation.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
Conjunctival hyperemia.

Less Common:
Headache, urinary tract infection, common
cold, eye irritation, stinging of eyes, conjunctivitis,
cataract, eye pain, xerophthalmia, blurred vision,
hyperpigmentation of eyelashes, increased eyelash
length, and cough.

Rare:
Dyspnea, exacerbation of asthma, iris hyperpigmentation, iritis, and uveitis.

Drug Interactions

None known.

Mechanism of Action

Tafluprost acid is a fluorinated prostaglandin F2-alpha analog believed to reduce intraocular pressure by increasing
outflow of aqueous humor via the uveoscleral pathway; exact mechanism by which it reduces intraocular pressure is
unknown.
