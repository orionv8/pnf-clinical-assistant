Dosage

ORAL › 400 mg sulfamethoxazole + 80 mg trimethoprim capsule (Rx)

Uncomplicated typhoid fever, by mouth, ADULT, 800/180 mg every 12 hours for 14 days; CHILD, 8 mg/kg of
Trimethoprim in 2 divided doses every 12 hours for 14 days. Internal hordeolum, MRSA, by mouth, ADULT, 800/160
mg every 12 hours. Dacrocystitis, by mouth, ADULT, 2 DS tablets twice a day. Pertussis, by mouth, CHILD ≥ 2 months
old, 8/40 mg/kg in 2 divided doses for 14 days; ADULT, 160/800 mg twice daily for 14 days. Pathogen-specific
treatment, Achromobacter, by mouth, CHILD, 8 mg/kg in divided doses every 12 hours with ceftazidime;
Burkholderia cepacia, by mouth, CHILD, 8 mg/kg in divided doses every 12 hours; Burkholderia pseudomallei, by
mouth, CHILD, 8 mg/kg in divided doses every 12 hours for 12-24 weeks. Enterobacter, by mouth, CHILD, 8 mg/kg in
divided doses every 12 hours.

Administration

ORAL › 400 mg sulfamethoxazole + 80 mg trimethoprim capsule (Rx)

Should be taken WITH food; this is best taken after meals with an adequate amount of fluid

Dose Adjustment

Renal Impairment:
For mild to moderate renal impairment, dose reduction is warranted; contraindicated for severe impairment.

Hepatic Impairment:
Contraindicated in significant hepatic impairment.

Indications

Treatment of uncomplicated typhoid fever, internal hordeolum, dacrocystitis, pertussis, and pathogen-specific
infections such pneumocystis jirovecii

Contraindications

Infants <2 months of age; severe renal or hepatic failure; megaloblastic anemia (due to folic acid deficiency); blood
dyscrasias; acute porphyria; late pregnancy (due to risk of neonatal kernicterus, hemolytic anemia and jaundice).

Precautions

HIV infection;
Photosensitivity, slow acetylator phenotype;
Treatment with oral typhoid vaccine;
Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE); Renal impairment (avoid if severe and if plasma sulfamethoxazole
concentration cannot be monitored),
Low urine pH increases risk of crystalluria (maintain adequate fluid intake to prevent crystalluria);
Hepatic impairment (avoid if severe);
Blood disorders (avoid unless under specialist supervision; monitor blood count and discontinue immediately if
blood disorder develops);
Rash (discontinue treatment immediately);
Asthma;
G6PD deficiency;
Elderly (increased risk of blood dyscrasias and skin disorders);
Pregnancy (first trimester: teratogenic effects which include anencephaly, hypoplastic left heart syndrome, choanal
atresia, transverse limb defect, diaphragmatic hernia; third trimester: neonatal hemolysis and methemoglobinemia);
Breastfeeding (monitor infant for jaundice: there is small risk of kernicterus in jaundiced infants);
G6PD-deficient infants (risk for hemolysis due to sulfamethoxazole).;
Caution in ill or premature infants and avoid in infants <6 weeks.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Anorexia, diarrhea, fever, hyperkalemia, hyponatremia, itch, nausea, rash, sore mouth, thrombocytopenia,
vomiting.
Less Common: Drowsiness, headache, neutropenia, photosensitivity.
Rare: Arthralgia, aseptic meningitis, Clostridium difficile-associated disease, convulsions, depression, erythema,
hallucinations, hepatic necrosis, hepatitis, hypoglycemia, jaundice, lowered mental acuity, megaloblastic anemia,
myalgia, myocarditis, pancreatitis, peripheral neuropathy, pulmonary infiltrates, SJS, SLE, toxic epidermal necrolysis,
urinary obstruction with anuria or oliguria, uveitis, vasculitis, vertigo.

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs:
Drugs causing potassium retention e.g. ACE inhibitors, Other nephrotoxic drugs e.g., gentamicin and streptomycin

Mechanism of Action

Sulfamethoxazole is a bacteriostatic sulfonamide antibiotic that inhibits a critical step in bacterial folate synthesis.
Trimethoprim exerts antimicrobial activity by blocking the reduction of dihydrofolate to tetrahydrofolate, the active
form of folic acid, by susceptible organisms.
