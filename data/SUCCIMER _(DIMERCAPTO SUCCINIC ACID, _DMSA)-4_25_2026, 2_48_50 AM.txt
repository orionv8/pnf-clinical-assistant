Indications

For the treatment of lead, mercury, arsenic, and other heavy metal poisoning

NOTE: NOT to be used for prophylaxis of lead-poisoning in a
lead-containing environment.

Precautions

WARNING: Mild-to-moderate neutropenia has been
observed in some patients. Withhold or
discontinue therapy if absolute neutrophil count
(ANC) is below 1,200 per microliter.
Do NOT use for longer than 3 weeks. Safety of
uninterrupted dosing longer than 3 weeks has not
been established.

Rebound blood lead levels;

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, appetite loss,
hemorrhoidal symptoms, loose stools, metallic taste in
mouth, back pain, abdominal cramps, stomach pains,
head pain, rib pain, chills, flank pain, fever, flu-like
symptoms, heavy head/tired, head cold, headache,
moniliasis, elevated SGPT and SGOT (transient),
elevated alkaline phosphatase, elevated serum
cholesterol, drowsiness, dizziness, sleepiness,
sensorimotor neuropathy, paresthesia, popular rash,
herpetic rash, rash, mucocutaneous eruptions, pruritus,
cloudy film in eye, ears plugged, otitis media, watery
eyes, sore throat, rhinorrhea, nasal congestion, cough,
decreased urination, voiding difficulty, increased
proteinuria, kneecap pain, leg pain

Less Common:
Arrhythmia, mild-to-moderate neutropenia,
intermittent eosinophilia

Drug Interactions

No known significant interactions

TEST INTERACTIONS. False-positive results for ketones in
urine using nitroprusside reagents; false decrease in
measurements of serum uric acid and CPK.

Mechanism of Action

An orally active, heavy metal chelating agent that forms
water-soluble chelates, and consequently, increases the
urinary excretion of heavy metals.
