Dosage

ORAL › 500 mg tablet (OTC)

Anti-urolithiasis, by mouth, ADULT, for the 1st 2 weeks, give 40 mg/kg/day in 3 divided doses, then decrease to
20/mg/kg/day in 2-3 divided doses; CHILD 7–12 years, 1st 2 weeks give 40 mg/kg/day in 3 divided doses, then
decrease to 20/mg/kg/day in 2-3 divided doses.

Administration

ORAL › 500 mg tablet (OTC)

May be taken with or without food.

Dose Adjustment

Clinical trials were performed in patients with normal renal and liver function tests; no reports of adverse effects in
patients with renal and liver dysfunction.

Indications

To increase urinary output in conditions characterized by fluid retention or edema; for treatment and prevention of
kidney stones.

Contraindications

Patients with severe allergy to sambong or any of its components.

Precautions

Patients with excretory urogram showing signs of renal obstruction. Pregnancy and lactation.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Epigastric pain, constipation.

Drug Interactions

None known

Mechanism of Action

A natural medicinal preparation from the leaves of Blumea balsamifera Linn. DC (Fam. Asteraceae), which is utilized in
the treatment of urological disorders.

Pregnancy & Remarks

PREGNANCY CATEGORY

In vitro and animal studies have shown Sambong to be non-mutagenic and non clastogenic.
