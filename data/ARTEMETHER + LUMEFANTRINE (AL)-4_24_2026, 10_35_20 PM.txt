Dose Adjustment

Renal and Impairment:
For mild-to-moderate impairment, use same dosage as in patients with normal function. For severe impairment,
refer patient to a specialist

Indications

For acute, uncomplicated malaria caused by P. falciparum, P. malariae, or P. knowlesi alone or with Plasmodium spp. in
areas with significant drug resistance; 2nd line drug for uncomplicated P. vivax or P. ovale infections.

Contraindications

Family history of sudden death or congenital prolongation of QT interval; history of arrhythmias, clinically-relevant
bradycardia, or CHF accompanied by reduced left ventricular ejection fraction; pregnancy (first trimester);
breastfeeding (during and for 1 week after stopping treatment; present in milk in animal studies)

Precautions

Concomitant administration of drugs that prolong the QT
interval; Acute porphyria and severe and/or complicated
malaria (avoid use); Electrolyte disturbances; monitor
patients unable to take; severe renal and hepatic
impairment (monitor ECG and plasma potassium
concentration)
Pregnancy (avoid use in first trimester); lactation.

SKILLED TASKS. May cause dizziness. Avoid performing
tasks, which require mental alertness, e.g., operating
machinery or driving

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Abdominal pain, anorexia, arthralgia, asthenia, cough, diarrhea, dizziness, fatigue, headache, itch, myalgia,
nausea, palpitations, prolonged QT interval, pruritus, pyrexia, rash, sleep disorder, vomiting, weakness

Less Common: Ataxia, clonus, hypesthesia, paresthesia

Rare: Hepatitis, hypersensitivity reactions

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects Drugs which prolong QT Interval (arrhythmia), Other Antimalarials
(arrhythmia) Reduces therapeutic effect of Hormonal Contraceptives

Avoid concomitant use with:
Reduces therapeutic effect of Artemether + Lumefantrine: CYP3A4 Inducers (potential loss of antimalarial efficacy)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of CYP2D6 Substrates (QT prolongation)

Mechanism of Action

A fixed-dose, antimalarial combination of an oil-soluble, artemisinin analog and a fluorene-derived aryl alcohol that is
used for uncomplicated P. falciparum malaria.
