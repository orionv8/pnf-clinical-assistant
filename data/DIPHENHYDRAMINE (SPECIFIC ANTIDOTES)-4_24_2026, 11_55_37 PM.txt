Dosage

IM / IV › 50 mg/mL (as hydrochloride), 1 mL ampule (Rx)

NOTE: Dose should be individualized according to the needs and response of the patient. Drug-induced
extrapyramidal effects, by IV infusion, ADULT, 10–50 mg at a rate not exceeding 25 mg/min (maximum daily dose,
400 mg); CHILD, 5 mg/kg every 24 hours or 150 mg/m2 every 24 hours in 4 divided doses at a rate not exceeding 25
mg/min (maximum daily dose, 300 mg); by deep IM injection, ADULT, 100 mg (maximum daily dose, 400 mg); CHILD, 5
mg/kg every 24 hours or 150 mg/m2 every 24 hours in 4 divided doses at a rate not exceeding 25 mg/min
(maximum daily dose, 300 mg).

Administration

IM / IV › 50 mg/mL (as hydrochloride), 1 mL ampule (Rx)

For IV administration, do NOT exceed a rate of 25 mg/minute. IM administration must be deep. Local necrosis may
result with SC or ID use. Inspect visually for particulate matter and discoloration prior to administration. See
Diphenhydramine under Anti-Parkinson Drugs in Chapter 10: Nervous System for other information.

Contraindications

Acute asthma; neonates or premature infants; breastfeeding; use of the parenteral form as a local anesthetic

Mechanism of Action

A monoethanolamine-derived, first-generation H1-receptor
antagonist that exhibits antimuscarinic and pronounced
sedative property, with low incidence of GI side effects.
