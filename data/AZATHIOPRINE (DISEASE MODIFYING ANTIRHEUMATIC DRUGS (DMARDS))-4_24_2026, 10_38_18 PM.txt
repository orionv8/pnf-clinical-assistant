(DMARDS))

Technical Specifications PNF Primary Care Reserved AMs

ORAL › 50 mg tablet (Rx)

Dosage

ORAL › 50 mg tablet (Rx)

Rheumatoid arthritis, by mouth, ADULT and CHILD, initially 1 mg/kg daily (50-100 mg) given once daily or divided
twice daily for 6-8 weeks; may increase by 0.5 mg/kg every 4 weeks until response or up to 2.5 mg/kg daily; an
adequate trial should be a minimum of 12 weeks; maintenance dose, reduce dose by 0.5 mg/kg (~25 mg daily) every
4 weeks until lowest effective dose is reached; may be discontinued abruptly (monitor for delayed toxicities).

Administration

ORAL › 50 mg tablet (Rx)

Administer tablets after meals or in divided doses to decrease adverse GI events. Protect from light and moisture
and store between 15–25°C.

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric:
Start with lowest recommended adult doses. Adjust dose to creatinine clearance.

Renal Impairment:
For patients with CrCl 10-50 mL/minute, administer 75% of normal dose.
For patients with CrCl <10 mL/minute, administer 50% of normal dose.
For patients receiving hemodialysis, administer 50% of normal dose and supplement with 0.25 mg/kg.

Oliguric patients, particularly those with tubular necrosis in the immediate posttransplant period (cadaveric
transplant) may have delayed clearance and typically receive lower doses.

Hepatic Impairment:
Reduce dose.

Indications

Management of active rheumatoid arthritis

Contraindications

Pregnancy; patients with rheumatoid arthritis and a history of treatment with alkylating agents (e.g.,
cyclophosphamide, chlorambucil, melphalan) may have a prohibitive risk of malignancy with azathioprine treatment

Precautions

WARNING: Hazardous agent; use appropriate precautions for handling and disposal.

WARNING: Chronic use for immunosuppression increases risk of malignancy, including posttransplant lymphoma and
hepato-splenic Tcell lymphoma in patients with inflammatory bowel disease.

Gastrointestinal toxicity (e.g. severe nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, rash, fever, malaise, myalgia, hypotension, and liver
enzyme abnormalities generally reversible upon discontinuation).

Hematologic toxicity (dose-related; leukopenia, thrombocytopenia, and anemias may occur; may be severe and/or
delayed; patients with intermediate thiopurine methyltransferase (TPMT) activity may be at increased risk for
hematologic toxicity at conventional azathioprine doses; patients with low or absent TPMT activity are at risk for
severe, life-threatening myelotoxicity; myelosuppression may be more severe with renal transplants undergoing
rejection; may require treatment interruption or dose reduction).

Hepatotoxicity (transaminase, bilirubin, and alkaline phosphatase elevations may occur; usually occurs within 6
months of transplant and is normally reversible with discontinuation; rarely, hepatic sinusoidal obstruction
syndrome [SOS], formerly called veno-occlusive disease [VOD] has been reported; discontinue if hepatic SOS is
suspected).

Infections (chronic immunosuppression increases the risk of serious, sometimes fatal, infections; progressive
multifocal leukoencephalopathy [PML] caused by reactivation of the JC virus, has been reported).

Hepatic and renal impairment (dosage reductions may be necessary);

TPMT deficiency (increased sensitivity to myelosuppressive effects; consider TPMT testing in patients with
abnormally low CBC unresponsive to dose reduction);

Elderly;

Lactation (breastfeeding is not recommended by the manufacturer due to potential for serious adverse reactions in
the nursing infant).

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Malaise, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, leukopenia, neoplasia, thrombocytopenia, hepatotoxicity, increased
susceptibility to infection, myalgia, fever

Less Common: Abdominal pain, acute myelocytic leukemia, alopecia, anemia, arthralgia, bone marrow depression
(dose-related), hemorrhage, hepatic veno-occlusive disease, hepatosplenic T-cell lymphomas (HSTCL),
hepatotoxicity (idiosyncratic), hypersensitivity, hypotension, interstitial pneumonitis (reversible), JC virus infection,
macrocytic anemia, malignant lymphoma, malignant neoplasm of skin, negative nitrogen balance, pancreatitis,
pancytopenia, progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy (potentially fatal), skin rash, steatorrhea, Sweet's
syndrome (acute febrile neutrophilic dermatosis), post-transplant lymphoma

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Azathioprine: ACE Inhibitors, Trimethoprim (myelosuppressive effect), Trastuzumab
(neutropenic effect), (myelosuppressive effect)
Enhances therapeutic effect of Sipuleucel-T
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Azathioprine: ACE Inhibitors, Co-trimoxazole (hematologic
abnormalities), Denosumab (serious infections)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Cyclophosphamide (hepatotoxic effect)
Reduces diagnostic effect of Coccidioides immitis Skin Test
Reduces therapeutic effect of Vitamin K Antagonists, e.g., Warfarin (anticoagulant effect)

Avoid concomitant use with:
Decreases serum concentration of Azathioprine: Ribavirin
Enhances therapeutic effect of Azathioprine: Roflumilast (immunosuppressive effect)
Enhances therapeutic effect of the following drugs: Mercaptopurine (myelosuppressive effect), Tofacitinib
(immunosuppressive effect)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Azathioprine: Pimecrolimus
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Leflunomide (hematologic toxicity, such as
pancytopenia, agranulocytosis, and/or thrombocytopenia), Natalizumab (concurrent infection), Tacrolimus Topical,
Vaccines (Live)
Increases serum concentration of Azathioprine: Allopurinol [if concomitant use cannot be avoided, reduce
azathioprine dose to 1/3 to ¼ of usual dose], Febuxostat
Reduces therapeutic effect of Azathioprine: Ribavirin
Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs: BCG, Vaccines, Inactivated [complete all age-appropriate
vaccinations at least 2 weeks prior to starting azathioprine; if vaccinated during azathioprine therapy, revaccinate at
least 3 months after discontinuation], Vaccines, Live [avoid azathioprine doses greater than 3 mg/kg daily]

Mechanism of Action

An imidazolyl derivative of mercaptopurine, which incorporates its metabolites into replicating DNA, stopping
replication. It also blocks the pathway for purine synthesis.
