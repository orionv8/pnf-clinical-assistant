Technical Specifications PNF Primary Care Reserved AMs

Dosage

Administration

Indications

Post exposure prophylaxis in the following :
immuno compromised patients without evidence of immunity;
newborn infants whose mothers have signs and symptoms of varicella around the time of delivery (i.e., 5 days before
to 2 days after).;
hospitalized premature infants born at ≥28 weeks of gestation whose mothers do not have evidenceof immunity to
varicella;
hospitalized premature infants born at <28 weeks of gestation or who weigh 1,000 g at birth, regardless of their
mothers' evidence of immunity to varicella.
In pregnant women, for the prevention or reduction in severity of maternal infection within 4 days of exposure to the
varicella zoster virus.

Contraindications

Severe reaction associated with past human immune globulin administration; IgA deficiency

Precautions

Hypersensitivity and anaphylactic reactions can occur;
immediate treatment (including epinephrine 1:1000) should be available;
Use with caution in patients with isolated IgA deficiency or a history of systemic hypersensitivity to human
immunoglobulins;
Use with caution in patients with thrombocytopenia or coagulation disorders, IM injections may be contraindicated;
Product of human plasma, may potentially contain infectious agents which could transmit disease.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
Headache, injection site pain.

Less Common:
Dizziness, fever, pain, chills, fatigue, flushing, insomnia, rash, dermatitis, erythematous rash, nausea, dysgeusia,
injection site bruising, itching, tenderness, neck pain, myalgia.

Drug Interactions

Avoid concomitant use with:
Reduces therapeutic effects of the following drugs:
Live vaccines (except: influenza virus vaccine (live/attenuated);
rotavirus vaccine;
yellow fever vaccine;
zoster vaccine

Mechanism of Action

An immune globulin specific for varicella zoster virus.
