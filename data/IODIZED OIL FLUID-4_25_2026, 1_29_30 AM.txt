Dosage

ORAL › 500 mg (equivalent to 200 mg elemental iodine) soft gel capsule (Rx)

NOTE: Select lowest dose and frequency that will ensure protection throughout pregnancy, and during lactation
for at least first year postpartum. Prophylaxis of iodine-deficiency-related fetal disorders, by mouth, ADULT
(pregnant women), 300–480 mg every 12 months; or 100–300 mg every 6 months; ADULT (non pregnant fertile
women), 400–960 mg every 12 months; or 200–480 mg every 6 months.

Administration

ORAL › 500 mg (equivalent to 200 mg elemental iodine) soft gel capsule (Rx)

May be administered at any stage of the pregnancy.

Precautions

Use only in pregnant women and women of childbearing age
where the prevalence of iodine deficiency disorder is
classified as moderate or severe, cretinism and neonatal
hypothyroidism are present, or universal salt iodization
programs will not reach women at reproductive age
within 1–2 years.
Identify optimal biological and process indicators to prevent
fetal brain damage as follows:

Biological indicators [NOTE: At least one should be neonatal thyroid stimulating hormone (TSH) or maternal urinary
iodine]:
Infants
• Birth weight
• Perinatal mortality rate
• Neonatal serum TSH
Mothers
• Urinary iodine concentration
• Breastmilk iodine concentration
