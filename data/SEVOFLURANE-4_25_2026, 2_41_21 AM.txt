Administration

RESPIR › Inhalation: 250 mL bottle (Rx)

Administer via sevoflurane-specific calibrated vaporizers. Use with caution in low-flow or closed-circuit systems
since sevoflurane isunstable and may liberate potentially toxic breakdown products.

Renal and Hepatic Impairment:
Use with caution in severe impairment. Safety has not been established.

Indications

Induction and maintenance of general anesthesia.

Contraindications

Known or suspected susceptibility to malignant hyperthermia; history of moderate to severe hepatic impairment
associated with halogenated
anesthetics.

Precautions

Increased intracranial pressure; Neuromuscular disease e.g. Duchenne muscular dystrophy; mitochondrial disorder;
Hypovolemia; Hypotension; Hemodynamically compromised patients; Patients at risk of QT prolongation; Hepatic and
renal impairment

Children; Pregnancy and lactation

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Hypotension, tachycardia, bradycardia, hypertension, agitation, drowsiness, shivering, dizziness,
headache, hypothermia, myoclonus, delirium (emergence), sialorrhea, cough, airway obstruction, laryngospasm,
breath-holding, apnea, fever

Less Common: Acidosis, albuminuria, amblyopia, anaphylactoid reaction, anaphylaxis, atrial arrhythmia, bigeminy,
bronchospasm, atrial fibrillation, cardiac arrhythmia, chest discomfort, complete AV block, confusion, conjunctivitis,
crying, dysgeusia, ST-segment depression on ECG, dyspnea, extrasystoles (supraventricular), facial edema,
glycosuria, hemorrhage, hepatic failure, hepatic insufficiency, hepatic necrosis, hepatitis, hepatotoxicity
(idiosyncratic), hiccups, hypertonia, hyperbilirubinemia, hyperglycemia, hypoxia, hypersensitivity reaction,
hyperkalemia (pediatric; postoperative), hyperventilation, hypophosphatemia, hypoventilation, insomnia, jaundice,
leukocytosis (transient), malignant hyperthermia, myoglobinuria, nervousness, oliguria, pain, pharyngitis, pruritus,
second degree atrioventricular block, skin rash, stridor, syncope, thrombocytopenia, tooth enamel damage
(fluorosis), urinary retention, urticaria, ventricular premature contractions, weakness, wheezing, xerostomia

Drug Interactions

Avoid concomitant use with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of the following drugs: Blood pressure-lowering agents and Hypotensive agents
(hypotensive effect); Dopamine; Epinephrine nasal, oral inhalation and systemic; Norepinephrine (arrhythmogenic
effect); Metyrosine, Pramipexole, Ropinirole, Rotigotine (sedative effect); Neuromuscular-blocking Agents;
Nondepolarizing (neuromuscular-blocking effect); QTcprolonging Agents (QTc-prolonging effect)

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Sevoflurane: Bambuterol; Epinephrine nasal, oral inhalation and
systemic (arrhythmogenic effect); Methylphenidate (hypertensive effect); Rufinamide (sleepiness and dizziness)

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Fenoterol; Formoterol; Isoproterenol; Metaraminol
(arrhythmogenic effect); Other CNS depressants; SSRIs (psychomotor impairment)

Mechanism of Action

Sevoflurane is a fluorinated isopropyl esther. It may act by interfering with the release and re-uptake of
neurotransmitters at post-synaptic terminals and/or alter ionic conduction following receptor activation. It also
activates GABA receptors that produces general anesthetic effect, a decrease in myocardial contractility and mean
arterial pressure and increases respiratory rate.
