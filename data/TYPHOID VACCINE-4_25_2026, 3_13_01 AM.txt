TYPHOID VACCINE

Drug Classification

ATC CODE

J07AP01 (oral)/J07AP02 (parenteral)

ANATOMICAL

Anti-Infectives for systemic use

THERAPEUTIC

Vaccines

PHARMACOLOGICAL

Bacterial Vaccines

CHEMICAL CLASS

Typhoid Vaccines

Technical Specifications PNF Primary Care Reserved AMs

IM › 25 mcg in 0.5mL Pre-filled syringe Vi-capsular polysaccharide S. typhi (IM) (Rx)

ORAL › Enteric-coated tablet (encapsulated) Live attenuated S. typhi (not less than 109) viable strain (Rx)

Dosage

Administration

Dose Adjustment

Primary immunization, by mouth, ADULT and CHILD ≥6 years, 1 capsule on alternate days for 4 doses, completed at
least 1 week prior to expected exposure; by IM injection, ADULT and CHILD ≥2 years, 0.5 mL at least 2 weeks prior to
expected exposure. Reimmunization, by mouth, ADULT and CHILD ≥6 years, repeat full course of primary
immunization every 5 years; by IM injection, ADULT and CHILD ≥2 years, 0.5 mL every 2 years.

Indications

Active immunization against typhoid fever caused by Salmonella typhi

Contraindications

Hypersensitivity to any component of the vaccine; acute febrile illness; congenital or acquired immunodeficient
state

Precautions

Anaphylactoid or hypersensitivity reactions (immediate treatment, including epinephrine 1: 1,000 should be available
during vaccine use; syncope; acute illness; bleeding disorders, including thrombocytopenia; typhoid fever.
Pregnancy (animal reproduction studies have not been conducted); Lactation (not known if excreted into breastmilk).

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
[Inj.]: Malaise, headache, generalized ache, pruritus, nausea, vomiting, injection site reactions (tenderness, pain,
induration, erythema, swelling), muscle tenderness, myalgia, fever
[Oral]: Headache, skin rash, abdominal pain, nausea, diarrhea, vomiting

Less Common:
[Inj.]: Abdominal pain, anaphylaxis, angioedema, arthralgia, asthma, diarrhea, dizziness, flulike symptoms, Guillain-
Barré syndrome, hypotension, inflammation at injection site (angioedema, urticaria), intestinal perforation (jejunum),
hypersensitivity reaction, loss of consciousness, lymphadenopathy, malaise, neck pain, serum sickness, skin rash,
syncope (with and without convulsions), tremor, urticaria, vasodilation, weakness
[Oral]: Anaphylaxis, demyelinating disease, myalgia, pain, rheumatoid arthritis, sepsis, urticaria, weakness

Mechanism of Action

A bacterial vaccine containing virulent strains of Salmonella typhi that confer immunity by the provocation of a local
immune response in the intestinal tract induced by oral ingestion of a live strain with subsequent aborted infection.
