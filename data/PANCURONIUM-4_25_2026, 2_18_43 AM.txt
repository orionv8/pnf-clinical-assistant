PANCURONIUM

Drug Classification

ATC CODE

M03AC01

ANATOMICAL

Musculo-Skeletal System

THERAPEUTIC

Technical Specifications PNF Primary Care Reserved AMs

IM › IV › 2 mg/mL, 2 mL ampule (as bromide) (Rx)

Dosage

IM / IV › 2 mg/mL, 2 mL ampule (as bromide) (Rx)

NOTE: Dose to effect. Doses will vary due to interpatient variability. Intubation, by IV injection, ADULT and CHILD,
initially 0.06– 0.1 mg/kg or 0.05 mg/kg after initial dose (maintenance dose, 0.01 mg/kg administered 60 to 100
minutes after initial dose, then 0.01 mg/kg every 25 – 60 minutes); Pretreatment or priming, 10% of intubating dose
given 3–5 minutes before intubating dose. ICU paralysis (e.g., facilitate mechanical ventilation) in select adequately
sedated patients, by IV bolus, ADULT and CHILD, 0.06–0.1 mg/kg followed by either: By continuous infusion, 1–2
micrograms/kg per minute (0.06–0.12 mg/kg per hour), or; By continuous infusion, 0.8–1.7 micrograms/kg per
minute (0.048–0.102 mg/kg per hour); or By intermittent bolus, 0.1–0.2 mg/kg every 1–3 hours.

Administration

IM / IV › 2 mg/mL, 2 mL ampule (as bromide) (Rx)

May be administered undiluted by rapid IV injection.

Dose Adjustment

Renal Impairment:
For patients with CrCl of 10–50 mL/minute or patients on CRRT, administer 50% of normal dose. Avoid use in patients
with CrCl <10 mL/minute, or patients on hemodialysis or peritoneal dialysis.

Hepatic Impairment:
Large doses may be required and problems may arise in antagonism.

Obesity:
Use ideal body weight for obese patients.

Indications

Facilitation of endotracheal intubation and relaxation of skeletal muscles during surgery; facilitation of mechanical
ventilation in ICU patients

Contraindications

Hypersensitivity to pancuronium, bromide, or any component of the formulation

Precautions

WARNING: Should be administered by adequately trained individuals familiar with its use.

Cross-sensitivity (use extreme caution in patients with previous anaphylactic reactions);

Burn injury (resistance may occur if burns =20% of total body surface area);

Conditions which may antagonize neuromuscular blockade (e.g., respiratory alkalosis, hypercalcemia, demyelinating
lesions, peripheral neuropathies, denervation, and muscle trauma may occur);

Conditions which may potentiate neuromuscular blockade (e.g., electrolyte abnormalities, neuromuscular diseases,
metabolic acidosis, metabolic alkalosis, respiratory acidosis, Lambert-Eaton syndrome and myasthenia gravis may
occur).

Hepatic and renal impairment (use with caution);

Elderly;

Immobilized patients (resistance may occur).

Some dosage forms may contain benzyl alcohol (amounts =99 mg/kg per day of benzyl alcohol have been associated
with a potentially fatal toxicity called “gasping syndrome” in neonates; see manufacturer’s labeling for contents prior
to administration in neonates);

Pregnancy (may be used short-term in cesarean section; reduced doses recommended in patients also receiving
magnesium sulfate due to enhanced effects).

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common and Less Common: Elevated blood pressure and cardiac output, tachycardia, edema, skin flushing,
circulatory collapse, rash, itching, erythema, burning sensation along the vein, excessive salivation, profound muscle
weakness, wheezing, bronchospasm, hypersensitivity reaction, acute quadriplegic myopathy syndrome (prolonged
use), anaphylactoid reactions, anaphylaxis, myositis ossificans (prolonged use)

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Pancuronium: Aminoglycosides (respiratory depressant effect), Calcium Channel
Blockers e.g., Amlodipine, Capreomycin (neuromuscular-blocking effect), Clindamycin (Topical), Cyclosporine
(Systemic), Inhalational Anesthetics, Lincosamide, Lithium, Loop Diuretics e.g., Furosemide, Magnesium Salts,
Minocycline, Procainamide, Quinidine, Spironolactone, Tetracycline Derivatives, Vancomycin
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Pancuronium: Ketorolac (apnea)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Cardiac Glycosides e.g., Digoxin (arrhythmogenic
effect)
Reduces therapeutic effect of Pancuronium: Acetylcholinesterase Inhibitors e.g., Neostigmine, Fosphenytoin-
Phenytoin, Loop Diuretics

Avoid concomitant use with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Pancuronium: Colistimethate, Polymyxin B, Quinine
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Pancuronium: Theophylline Derivatives e.g., Aminophylline
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Corticosteroids (Systemic) (increased muscle
weakness; polyneuropathies; myopathies)
Reduces therapeutic effect of Pancuronium: Theophylline Derivatives e.g., Aminophylline

Mechanism of Action

A non-depolarizing, neuromuscular-blocking agent, that induces muscle relaxation by restricting neural
transmission at the myoneural junction after binding to cholinergic receptor sites.
