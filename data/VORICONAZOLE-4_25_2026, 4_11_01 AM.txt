Dosage

ORAL › [Reserve] 200 mg film-coated tablet (Rx)

Anti-aspergillus prophylaxis, by mouth, by IV infusion, ADULT, 200 mg twice daily. Endophthalmitis, Candida sp., by
IV infusion, ADULT, 400 mg twice a day for 2 doses loading dose then 300 mg, by mouth or IV infusion, twice a day;
CHILD, 12 mg/kg twice a day for 2 doses loading dose then 4 mg/kg, by mouth or IV infusion, twice a day.

Administration

ORAL › [Reserve] 200 mg film-coated tablet (Rx)

Should be taken on an empty stomach: Take at least 1 hour before or after meals.

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric:
Refer to adult dosing

Indications

Treatment of candida endophthalmitis, reserve drug for the treatment of severe fungal infections; prophylaxis for
aspergillus in patients undergoing chemotherapy for AML/MDS with neutropenia and allogeneic HSCT recipients

Contraindications

Hypersensitivity. Concomitant use of CYP3A4 substrates, terfenadine, astemizole, cisapride, pimozide or quinidine;
sirolimus, rifabutin, rifampicin, carbamazepine, long-acting barbiturates, efavirenz, ritonavir, ergot alkaloids

Precautions

Hypersensitivity to other azoles. Infusion-related reactions; patients with potentially proarrhythmic conditions e.g.,
congenital or acquired QT prolongation, cardiomyopathy, sinus bradycardia, existing symptomatic arrhythmias,
concomitant use w/ drugs known to prolong QT interval;

Monitor & correct electrolyte imbalances (hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia and hypocalcemia) prior to and during
therapy.

Monitor for hepatic toxicity and lab evaluation of hepatic function (AST/ALT) at least weekly for the 1st month of
therapy;

Acute renal failure & visual adverse events in severely ill patients with underlying conditions and/or concomitant
medications;

Adults and children with risk factors for acute pancreatitis. Rare exfoliative cutaneous reaction, photosensitivity;
squamous cell carcinoma of the skin & melanoma, periostitis on long-term therapy;

Pregnancy and lactation

Drug Interactions

Decreases excretion of the following drugs:
Ergot alkaloids, Disopyramide
Simvastatin, Pimozide, Ranozaline, Sirolimus

Decreases excretion of Voriconazole:
Efavirenz, Rifabutin, Rifampin, Rionavir, Carbamazepine, Phenobarbital

Mechanism of Action

Azoles are synthetic compounds that can be classified as either imidazoles or triazoles according to the number of
nitrogen atoms. Azoles exhibit activity by reducing the ergosterol synthesis through inhibition of fungal cytochrome
P450 enzymes.
