Contraindications

Hypersensitivity to sofosbuvir, velpatasvir, or any component of the formulation.

Precautions

May cause hepatitis B reactivation.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Headache, and fatigue.

Less Common: Irritability, insomnia, depression, skin rash, nausea, weakness

Rare: Reactivation of hepatitis B virus and angioedema.

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Increases level/effect of the following drugs:
Alprazolam, Atorvastatin, Clonidine, Colchicine, Diazepam, Digoxin, Doxorubicin, Enalapril, Ergotamine, Everolimus,
Fentanyl, Imatinib, Irinotecan, Methotrexate, Midazolam, Mitoxantrone, Nitrofurantoin, Rosuvastatin, Simvastatin,
Tacrolimus, Valsartan, Warfarin.

Decreases levels/effects of Sofosbuvri/Velpatasvir:
Aspirin, Antacids

Avoid concomitant use with:
Increases toxicity of Amiodarone

Decreases leves/effects of Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir:
Carbamazepine, Colchicine, Dexamethasone, Doxorubicin, Efavirenz, Lansoprazole, Nevirapine, Omeprazole,
Oxcarbazepine, Phenobarbital, Phenytoin, Rifabutin, Rifampin, Vinblastine.

Mechanism of Action

Velpatasvir inhibits the HCV NS5A protein necessary for viral replication while sofosbuvir inhibits NS5B RNA
dependent RNA polymerase and acts as a chain terminator.
