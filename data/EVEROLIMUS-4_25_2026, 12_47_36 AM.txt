Dosage

ORAL › 250 micrograms tablet (Rx)

Breast cancer, advanced, hormone receptor-positive, HER2negative, by mouth, in combination with exemestane,
10 mg once daily, continue until no longer clinically beneficial or until unacceptable toxicity. Advanced pancreatic
neuroendocrine tumors (PNET), renal angiomyolipoma, or renal cell cancer (RCC), by mouth, 10 mg once daily,
continue until no longer clinically beneficial or until unacceptable toxicity. Liver transplantation, rejection
prophylaxis, by mouth, in combination with reduced doses of tacrolimus and corticosteroids, initially 1 mg twice
daily beginning at least 30 days post-transplant; adjust maintenance dose if needed at a 4 to 5-day interval based
on serum concentrations, tolerability, and response; goal serum concentration is between 3 and 8 nanograms/mL;
if trough is <3 nanograms/mL, double total daily dose; if trough >8 nanograms/mL on 2 consecutive measures,
decrease dose by 0.25 mg twice daily. Renal transplantation, rejection prophylaxis, by mouth, in combination with
induction of basiliximab, and concurrently with corticosteroids and reduced doses of cyclosporine, initially 0.75
mg twice daily; adjust maintenance dose if needed at a 4 to 5-day interval based on serum concentrations,
tolerability, and response; goal serum concentration is between 3 and 8 nanograms/mL; if trough is <3
nanograms/mL, double total daily dose; if trough >8 nanograms/mL on 2 consecutive measures, decrease dose by
0.25 mg twice daily. Subependymal giant cell astrocytoma (SEGA), by mouth, ADULT and CHILD =1 year, initially 4.5
mg/m2 once daily; round to nearest tablet size; if trough <5 nanograms/mL, increase dose by 2.5 mg daily; if
trough >15 nanograms/mL, reduce dose by 2.5 mg daily; if dose reduction necessary inpatients receiving the
lowest strength available, administer every other day;

Administration

ORAL › 250 micrograms tablet (Rx)

May be taken with or without food. Take consistently with regard to food to reduce variability. Swallow whole with a
glass of water. Do NOT break, chew, or crush. Do NOT administer crushed or broken tablets. Avoid contact with or
exposure to crushed or broken tablets. NOTE: Limit exposure to sunlight and ultraviolet light by wearing
protective clothing and using effective sunscreen to minimize the risk for skin cancer. For breast cancer,
pancreatic neuroendocrine tumors, renal cell cancer, renal angiolipoma, or SEGA, administer at the same time
each day. For liver transplantation, administer consistently ~12 hours apart, at the same time as tacrolimus. For
renal transplantation, administer consistently ~12 hours apart, at the same time as cyclosporine.

Dose Adjustment

Concomitant CYP3A4 Inducer or Inhibitor and/or Pglycoprotein Inhibitor use:
For patients receiving concomitant strong CYP3A4 and/or pglycoprotein inducers (e.g., carbamazepine,
phenobarbital, phenytoin, rifabutin, rifampin, rifapentine), double everolimus dose using increments of =5 mg with
careful monitoring up to a maximum dailydose of 20 mg. If inducer is discontinued, allow 3–5 days prior to reducing
the everolimus dose to recommended starting dose.
For patients receiving concomitant moderate CYP3A4 and/or p-glycoprotein inhibitors (e.g., amprenavir, aprepitant,
diltiazem, erythromycin, fluconazole, fosamprenavir, verapamil), reduce everolimus dose to 2.5 mg once daily;
increase from 2.5–5 mg once daily based on patient tolerance; further reductions may be necessary for adverse
reactions; if dose reduction is required for patients receiving the lowest available strength, consider alternate-day
dosing. If inhibitor is discontinued, allow 2–3 days prior to reducing the everolimus dose to recommended starting
dose.

Renal transplantation:
Adjust dose based on everolimus serum concentrations.

Hepatic Impairment:
Mild impairment (Child-Pugh class A): Breast cancer, PNET, RCC, renal angiomyolipoma, 7.5 mg once daily; may further
reduce to 5 mg once daily if not tolerated.

Liver or renal transplantation, reduce initial dose by ~33%; individualize subsequent dosing based on therapeutic
drug monitoring; target trough concentration is 3–8 nanograms/mL.

SEGA, 75% of usual dose based on calculated BSA, rounded to the nearest strength; target trough concentration is
5– 15 nanograms/mL; may increase dose within the target range to achieve higher concentrations as tolerated

Moderate impairment (Child-Pugh class B):

Breast cancer, PNET, RCC, renal angiomyolipoma, 5 mg once daily; may further reduce to 2.5 mg once daily if not
tolerated.

Liver or renal transplantation, reduce initial dose by ~50%; individualize subsequent dosing based on therapeutic
drug monitoring; target trough concentration is 3–8 nanograms/mL.

SEGA, 50% of usual dose based on calculated BSA, rounded to the nearest strength; target trough concentration is
5– 15 nanograms/mL; may increase dose within the target range if tolerated.

Severe impairment (Child-Pugh class C):
Breast cancer, PNET, RCC, renal angiomyolipoma, maximum dose is 2.5 mg once daily.

Liver or renal transplantation, reduce initial dose by ~50%; individualize subsequent dosing based on therapeutic
drug monitoring; target trough concentration is 3–8 nanograms/mL.

Indications

Prophylaxis of organ rejection in liver transplantation; prophylaxis of organ rejection in renal transplant patients at
low to moderate immunologic risk

Contraindications

Hypersensitivity to everolimus

Precautions

WARNING: Should only be used by physicians experienced in immunosuppressive therapy and management of
transplant patients. Adequate laboratory and supportive medical resources must be readily available.

NOT recommended for use in heart transplant; has immunosuppressant properties which may result in infection. Use
may result in the development of malignancy, including lymphoma and skin cancer. An increased risk of renal arterial
and venous thrombosis has been reported with use in renal transplantation, which may result in graft loss.

Angioedema

Edema (generalized edema, including peripheral edema and lymphedema, and local fluid accumulation, e.g.,
pericardial effusion, pleural effusion, or ascites)

Bone marrow suppression; Malignancy

Fertility effects (e.g. menstrual irregularities, secondary amenorrhea, and increases in luteinizing hormone and
follicle-stimulating hormone in females may occur; azoospermia and oligospermia in males; may cause infertility).

Hepatic artery thrombosis; Hepatic impairment

Hereditary galactose intolerance (may result in diarrhea and malabsorption);

Metabolic effects (e.g. hyperglycemia, hyperlipidemia, and hypertriglyceridemia; increased risk for new-onset
diabetes)

Infections (sepsis, respiratory failure, hepatic failure, or fatality may occur; polyomavirus infection in transplant
patients may be serious and/or fatal; reactivation of hepatitis B)

Mucositis or stomatitis

Nephrotoxicity (e.g. elevations in serum creatinine, renal failure, and proteinuria; an increased incidence of rash,
infection and dose interruptions have been reported in patients with CrCl =60 mL/minute)

Pulmonary toxicity (e.g. noninfectious pneumonitis, interstitial lung disease (ILD), and/or noninfectious fibrosis,
some fatal)

Wound healing complication (may delay wound healing and increase the occurrence of wound-related complications)

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Everolimus: Trastuzumab (neutropenic effect)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Everolimus: Denosumab (serious infections)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: ACE Inhibitors, e.g., Captopril, Enalapril
(angioedema)
Reduces diagnostic effect of Coccidioides immitis Skin Test
Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs: Antidiabetic Agents e.g. Gliclazide

Avoid concomitant use with:
Decreases serum concentration of Everolimus: Dabrafenib, Efavirenz
Enhances therapeutic effect of Everolimus: Roflumilast (immunosuppressive effect), Tofacitinib
(immunosuppressive effect)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Everolimus: Cyclosporine, Systemic (nephrotoxicity), Dipyrone
(agranulocytosis, pancytopenia); Pimecrolimus; Tacrolimus (Topical)
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Clozapine (agranulocytosis), Leflunomide
(hematologic toxicity), Natalizumab, (concurrent infection), Vaccines (Live) (infection)
Increases serum concentration of Everolimus: Conivaptan, Cyclosporine (Systemic), Fusidic Acid (Systemic),
Idelalisib, Mifepristone, P-glycoprotein / ABCB1 Inhibitors
Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs: BCG (Intravesical), Vaccines

FOOD INTERACTION. Grapefruit Juice may increase the serum concentration of Everolimus.

Mechanism of Action

Macrolide immunosuppressant and a mechanistic target of rapamycin (mTOR) inhibitor which has antiproliferative
and antiangiogenic properties.
