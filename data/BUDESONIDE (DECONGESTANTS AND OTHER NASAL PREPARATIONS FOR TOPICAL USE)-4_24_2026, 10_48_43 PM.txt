Dosage

RESPIR › DPI: 100 micrograms/dose x 200 doses with appropriate accompanying dispenser (Rx)

NOTE: Dose should be individualized and tapered to the smallest dose maintaining symptom control. Rhinitis, into
the nostril, ADULT and CHILD ≥6 years, initially 256 micrograms daily once daily in the morning or in 2 divided doses
(morning and evening). Nasal polyps, into the nostril, ADULT, 64–100 micrograms into each nostril twice daily for up
to 3 months.

Administration

RESPIR › DPI: 100 micrograms/dose x 200 doses with appropriate accompanying dispenser (Rx)

Shake well before use. If using for the first time, remove the protective cap from the tip and prime metered pump
by depressing the pump firmly several times. To spray, hold bottle with thumb at base and nozzle between first and
second fingers. Without tilting your head, insert the nozzle into nostril. Fully depress rim with a firm, even stroke
and sniff deeply. Wipe nozzle clean after use.

Dose Adjustment

Geriatric:
Administer the smallest possible effective dose for the shortest duration.

Pediatric:
Titrate to lowest effective dose to minimize systemic effects.

Indications

For seasonal allergic and non-allergic perennial rhinitis; treatment of nasal polyps; prevention of nasal polyps after
polypectomy

Contraindications

Primary treatment of status asthmaticus, acute episodes of asthma

NOTE: NOT for the relief of acute bronchospasm

Precautions

WARNING: Discontinuation of Therapy. Withdraw systemic therapy with gradual tapering of dose. There have been
reports of systemic corticosteroid withdrawal symptoms (e.g., joint or muscle pain, lassitude, depression) when
withdrawing oral inhalation therapy.

Adrenal suppression; Bronchospasm (if this occurs, stop steroid and treat with a fast-acting bronchodilator); Asthma
(not to be used in status asthmaticus or for the
relief of acute bronchospasm); Immunosuppression (use is restricted in active TB); Local oral infections; Kaposi's
sarcoma (if noted, discontinue therapy); myopathy
(monitor creatine kinase; recovery may be delayed); myasthenia gravis; Psychiatric disturbances; seizure disorders;
Cardiovascular disease (myocardial infarction); Diabetes; gastrointestinal disease; Hepatic impairment (long-term
use has been associated with fluid retention); renal impairment (fluid retention may occur); Thyroid disease (changes
in thyroid status may necessitate dose adjustments); Ocular disease; Osteoporosis; Elderly; children (routinely
monitor growth); lactation (excreted in breast milk).

wheezing (patients with severe milk allergy)

Mechanism of Action

A corticosteroid with potent glucocorticoid activity and weak mineralocorticoid activity. It controls the rate of
protein synthesis, depresses the migration of polymorphonuclear leukocytes, fibroblasts, and reverses capillary
permeability and lysosomal stabilization at the cellular level to prevent or control inflammation.
