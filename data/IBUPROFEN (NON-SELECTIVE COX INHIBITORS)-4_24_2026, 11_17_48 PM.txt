Dosage

ORAL › 200 mg tablet (OTC)

Inflammatory musculoskeletal disorders, by mouth, ADULT, 1.2–1.8 g daily in 3–4 divided doses, increased if
necessary (maximum dose, 2.4 g daily; 3.2 g daily in inflammatory disease); maintenance dose of 0.6–1.2 g daily may
be sufficient.

Administration

ORAL › 200 mg tablet (OTC)

Should be taken with food.

Dose Adjustment

Renal and Hepatic Impairment:
Keep dose as low as possible.

Indications

Acute pain and inflammation in rheumatic diseases and other musculoskeletal disorders

Contraindications

Active or history of peptic ulceration

Precautions

WARNING: Associated with an increased risk of adverse cardiovascular thrombotic events, including fatal MI and
stroke.

May increase risk of GI irritation, inflammation, ulceration, bleeding, and perforation.

Renal impairment;

Hepatic impairment;

Cardiac disease;

Coagulation defects;

Allergic disorders.

Elderly;

Pregnancy (avoid use unless the potential benefit outweighs risk; third trimester);

Lactation.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common: Diarrhea, dizziness, dyspepsia, headache, GI ulceration or bleeding, hemorrhage, hypertension, nausea,
raised liver enzymes, salt and fluid retention, vomiting

Less Common: Bronchospasm, confusion, heart failure, esophageal ulceration, hyperkalemia, rash, renal impairment

Rare: Acute renal failure, aseptic meningitis, blood dyscrasias, blurred vision, colitis, cystitis, fluid retention,
hepatitis, stroke, anaphylactic and anaphylactoid reactions, interstitial nephritis, MI, nephrotic syndrome,
photosensitivity reaction, Stevens-Johnson syndrome, tinnitus, toxic epidermal necrolysis, vertigo

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Ibuprofen: Angiotensin II Receptor Blockers (significantly decreased
renal function), Corticosteroids Oral, e.g., Hydrocortisone (gastric ulceration), Digoxin (reduced renal function), Other
NSAIDs

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: ACE Inhibitors (renal impairment; hyperkalemia),
Atenolol, Ciprofloxacin, Levofloxacin (convulsions), Digoxin (exacerbation of heart failure), Diuretics e.g., Loop
Diuretics, Thiazide Diuretics (nephrotoxicity)

Increases serum concentration of Digoxin

Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs: ACE Inhibitors, Angiotensin II Receptor Blockers
(antihypertensive effect)

Aspirin (antiplatelet activity; cardioprotective effect), Diuretics, e.g., Loop Diuretics, Thiazide Diuretics (diuretic
effect)

Avoid concomitant use with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Vitamin K Antagonists, e.g., Warfarin (anticoagulant effect)

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of Ibuprofen: Alendronate (gastric ulceration), Drugs Affecting Clotting
Process, e.g., Ketorolac (bleeding, including GI bleeding), Vitamin K Antagonists, e.g., Warfarin (serious bleeding)

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs: Drugs which are Renally Excreted, e.g., Calcineurin

Inhibitors, Drugs which Increase Potassium Concentration, e.g., Amiloride, Triamterene (hyperkalemia)

Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs: Antihypertensives, e.g., Beta Blockers, ARBs (hypotensive effect)

Mechanism of Action

A propionic acid-derived, nonselective, nonsteroidal antiinflammatory medicine, which is useful for the management
of pain due to inflammation.

Pregnancy & Remarks

PREGNANCY CATEGORY

C, prior to 30 weeks gestation; D, ≥30 weeks gestation
