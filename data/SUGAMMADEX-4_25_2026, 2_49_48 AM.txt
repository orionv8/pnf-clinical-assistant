Dosage

IV › 100 mg/mL solution for injection, 2 mL vial (Rx)

NOTE: Doses and timing of administration should be based on monitoring for twitch responses and the extent of
spontaneous recovery that has occurred. Neuromuscular blockade reversal, rocuronium and/or vecuronium, by IV
bolus injection, ADULT, 4 mg/kg if spontaneous recovery of the twitch response has reached 1 to 2 post-tetanic
counts (PTC) and there are no twitch responses to train-of-four (TOF) stimulation; 2 mg/kg if spontaneous recovery
has reached the reappearance of the second twitch (T2) in response to TOF stimulation. Neuromuscular blockade
reversal, rocuronium only, by IV bolus injection, ADULT, 16 mg/kg if there is a clinical need to reverse
neuromuscular blockade soon (approximately 3 minutes) after administration of a single dose of 1.2 mg/kg of
rocuronium.

Administration

IV › 100 mg/mL solution for injection, 2 mL vial (Rx)

For IV use only. Administer as a single bolus injection over 10 seconds, into an existing intravenous line of a
running infusion with either of the following: 0.9% sodium chloride 5% dextrose 0.45% sodium chloride and 2.5%
dextrose Isolyte P with 5% dextrose Ringer’s lactate solution Ringer’s solution Ensure the infusion line is
adequately flushed between administration of sugammadex and other drugs. Visually inspect for particulate
matter and discoloration prior to administration. NOTE: Ventilatory support is mandatory until adequate
spontaneous respiration is restored and the ability to maintain patent airway is assured.

Indications

For the reversal of neuromuscular blockade
induced by rocuronium and vecuronium in adults
undergoing surgery

Precautions

WARNING: Should be administered by trained health care providers familiar with the characteristics, actions, use,
and complications of neuromuscular blocking agents and neuromuscular block reversal agents.

NOT for use in the reversal of neuromuscular blockade for agents other than rocuronium or vecuronium.

Anaphylaxis and hypersensitivity (eg. anaphylaxis);
Marked bradycardia;
Severe renal impairment (not recommended);
Elderly;
Pregnancy;
Lactation.

RE-ADMINISTRATION. A minimum waiting time is necessary
before administration of steroidal neuromuscular
blocking agent after sugammadex administration.

Minimum Waiting Time: 5 minutes
Dose to be administered: 1.2 mg/kg rocuronium

Minimum Waiting Time: 4 hours
Dose to be administered: 0.6 mg/kg rocuronium, or 0.1 mg/kg vecuronium

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
Incision site pain, procedural complication, airway
complication of anesthesia, anesthetic complication,
wound hemorrhage, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain,
flatulence, dry mouth, pain at administration site,
pyrexia, chills, headache, dizziness, hypoesthesia,
oropharyngeal pain, cough, pain in extremity,
musculoskeletal pain, myalgia, insomnia, anxiety,
decreased RBC count, abnormal ECG QT-interval,
hypertension, hypotension, pruritus, erythema,
hypocalcemia, tachycardia, bradycardia

Less Common:
Recurrence of neuromuscular blockade,
restlessness, depression

Rare:
Hysterectomy

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of the following drugs,
specifically increased aPTT and prothrombin time:
Dabigatran, Rivaroxaban, Unfractionated Heparin

Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs due to
decreased serum concentration:
Hormonal Contraceptives

Avoid concomitant use with:
Reduces therapeutic effect of Sugammadex:
Fusidic Acid (delayed reversal time), Toremifene (delayed
recovery; high binding affinity for sugammadex)

Mechanism of Action

A modified gamma cyclodextrin that reverses neuromuscular blockade induced by rocuronium and vecuronium by
complex formation, reducing the amount of neuromuscular blocking agent available to bind to nicotinic receptors in
the neuromuscular junction.
