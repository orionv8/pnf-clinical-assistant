Administration

IV › 300 mg/mL equivalent to 623 mg iodine, 50 mL vial (Rx)

Warm solution to body temperature shortly before administration to improve tolerability and ease of injection.
Visually inspect for particulate matter and discoloration prior to administration. Do NOT admix with other drugs,
solutions, or total parenteral nutrition mixtures. For intra-arterial administration, administer at a flow rate
approximately equal to the flow rate in the vessel being injected NOTE: Patients should be well hydrated prior to
and following administration.

Dose Adjustment

Renal Impairment and Pheochromocytoma:
Administer absolute minimum amount required.

Indications

Radiocontrast media; for cerebral and peripheral arteriography; for coronary arteriography and left ventriculography,
visceral angiography, and aortography; for IV excretory urography; for contrast CT imaging of head and body

Precautions

WARNING:
NOT for intrathecal use. Inadvertent intrathecal administration may cause death, convulsions, cerebral hemorrhage,
coma, paralysis, arachnoiditis, acute renal failure, cardiac arrest, seizures, rhabdomyolysis, hyperthermia, and brain
edema.

WARNING:
Procedures involving radiopaque diagnostic agents should only be done by qualified personnel with in- depth
knowledge of the procedure to be done. Emergency treatment for severe reactions and appropriate facilities needs
to be readily available should there be any complication in the procedure. Severe delayed reactions may occur after
administration; well- trained personnel and emergency facilities should be readily available within 30- 60 minutes
after administration.

Life-threatening or fatal, anaphylactoid reactions, may occur during or after administration.

Development of severe cutaneous adverse reactions (SCAR) may develop after 1 hour to several weeks following IV
administration, including Stevens-Johnson syndrome and toxic epidermal necrolysis (SJS/TEN), acute generalized
exanthematous pustulosis (AGEP) and drug reaction with eosinophilia and systemic symptoms (DRESS).

Cardiovascular reactions;
Heart failure;
Thromboembolic complications (eg. local and distal organ damage, ischemia, thromboembolism, and organ failure,
including stroke, brachial plexus palsy, chest pain, myocardial infarction, sinus arrest, hepato-renal function
abnormalities);
Pheochromocytoma;
Thyroid storm;
Renal impairment;
Nervous system effects (may disrupt blood-brain barrier;
may accumulate in the brain);
Lactation.

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following drugs:
Biguanides (lactic acidosis)
Cholecystographic Agents (renal toxicity)
Beta- blockers

TEST INTERACTION.
May interfere with iodine uptake studies, which depend on iodine estimations for up to 16 days following
administration
May obscure lesions seen on non-contrast CT scans.

Mechanism of Action

A non-ionic, water soluble x-ray contrast agent that permits radiographic visualization of the internal structures of
the human body by opacifying vessels in the path of flow of the contrast medium via intravascular administration.
