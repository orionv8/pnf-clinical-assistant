Oral candidiasis, Candida albicans, by mouth, ADULT, oral suspension 100,000 U/mL, 4mL 4 times daily for 7-14 days.

Administration

ORAL › 100,000 units/mL suspension, 30 mL (Rx)

Shake well before use. Swish about the mouth and retain in the mouth for as long as possible (several minutes)
before swallowing. For neonates and infants, paint nystatin suspension into recesses of the mouth.

Contraindications

Hypersensitivity to nystatin or any component of the formulation.

Precautions

Not for use in systemic mycoses. Discontinue therapy if irritation or sensitization observed during use.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Localized sensitization; hypersensitivity reactions.

Drug Interactions

Enhances erratic release of Nystatin:
Progesterone Intravaginal Gel

Mechanism of Action

Azoles are synthetic compounds that can be classified as either imidazoles or triazoles according to the number of
nitrogen atoms. Azoles exhibit activity by reducing the ergosterol synthesis through inhibition of fungal cytochrome
P450 enzymes.
