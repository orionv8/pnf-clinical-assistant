Dosage

ORAL › USP grade (Rx)

Elevated intraocular pressure, by mouth, ADULT, 1–2 g/kg taken as a single dose; additional doses of 500 mg/kg
every 6 hours may be taken if needed; CHILD, 1–1.5 g/kg taken as a single dose, may be repeated in 4–8 hours if
needed.

Administration

ORAL › USP grade (Rx)

For use as an oral demulcent. Do NOT swallow excessive amounts. Mix it with a small amount of unsweetened
lemon, lime, or orange juice, pour over cracked ice, and sip through a straw to mask its unpleasant taste. Lie down
when taking this medicine and for a short time after taking it to prevent or relieve headaches. NOTE: When used
preoperatively, administer 60–90 minutes prior to surgery. Precautions should be taken to avoid acute urinary
retention. See Glycerol (Glycerin) under Other Nervous System Drugs in Chapter 10: Nervous System for other
information.

Indications

Management of glaucoma; reduction of
intraocular pressure prior to eye surgery

Mechanism of Action

An osmotic dehydrating agent with hygroscopic and
lubricating properties. Glycerol increases plasma
osmolality, resulting in the movement of water from
extravascular spaces into the plasma through osmosis.
