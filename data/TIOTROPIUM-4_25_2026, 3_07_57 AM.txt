Dosage

RESPIR › Inhalation: DPI: 18 micrograms/dose (as bromide) with appropriate accompanying dispenser (Rx)

COPD, by aerosol inhalation, ADULT, 18 micrograms daily via inhaler device at the same time each day. NOTE: Must
be used in combination with an inhaled corticosteroid and a long-acting beta2-agonist.

Administration

RESPIR › Inhalation: DPI: 18 micrograms/dose (as bromide) with appropriate accompanying dispenser (Rx)

Use only with appropriate accompanying dispenser. Prior to first use, insert cartridge into the inhaler and prime
the unit by actuating the inhaler toward the ground until an aerosol cloud is visible. Repeat 3 more times. The unit
is then primed and ready for use. If not used for more than 3 days, actuate the inhaler once to prepare the inhaler
for use. If not used for more than 3 days, actuate the inhaler once to prepare the inhaler for use.

Dose Adjustment

Renal Impairment:
In patients with CrCl ≤50 mL/minute, use with caution. Monitor patient closely.

Indications

Maintenance treatment of bronchospasm associated with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), including
chronic bronchitis and emphysema; reduction of COPD exacerbations

Contraindications

Hypersensitivity to atropine or its derivatives

Precautions

Bronchospasm; CNS effects; Hypersensitivity reactions; Glaucoma; Prostatic hyperplasia or bladder neck
obstruction; renal impairment (monitor closely for anticholinergic events); Elderly (renal clearance is decreased, and
plasma concentrations are increased due to decreased renal function; monitor urinary function of men with benign
prostatic hyperplasia); Pregnancy and lactation.

SKILLED TASKS. May impair the ability to perform certain
tasks, e.g., operating machinery or driving.

Adverse Drug Reactions

Common:
Chest pain, edema, angina pectoris (including
exacerbation), palpitations, headache, depression,
insomnia, dizziness, paresthesia, voice disorder, skin
rash, pruritus, hypercholesterolemia, hyperglycemia,
xerostomia, abdominal pain, dyspepsia, constipation,
omitting, GERD, oropharyngeal candidiasis, stomatitis,
UTI, hypersensitivity reaction, infection, herpes zoster,
arthralgia, myalgia, arthritis, leg pain, skeletal pain,
cataract, upper respiratory tract infection, pharyngitis,
sinusitis, rhinitis, epistaxis, cough, flu-like symptoms,
laryngitis

Less Common:
Anaphylaxis, angioedema, application site
irritation (including glossitis, oral mucosa ulcer,
pharyngolaryngeal pain), atrial fibrillation, blurred vision,
bronchospasm, dehydration, dermal ulcer, dysphagia,
dysuria, gingivitis, glaucoma, glossitis, hoarseness,
increased intraocular pressure, intestinal obstruction
(including paralytic ileus), joint swelling, paradoxical
bronchospasm, skin infection, mydriasis (contact with eyes), supraventricular tachycardia, tachycardia, throat
irritation, urinary retention, urticaria, xeroderma

Drug Interactions

Monitor closely with:
Enhances therapeutic effect of Tiotropium:
Mianserin (anticholinergic effect)

Enhances anticholinergic effect of the following drugs:
Abobotulinumtoxin-A, Onabotulinumtoxin-A,
Rimabotulinumtoxin-B

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following
drugs:
Analgesics, Opioid (constipation; urinary retention)
Mirabegron
Topiramate
Ramosetron (constipation)

Reduces therapeutic effect of Tiotropium:
Acetylcholinesterase Inhibitors e.g. Neostigmine
Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs:
Gastrointestinal Agents, Prokinetic
Itopride

Avoid concomitant use with:
Enhances anticholinergic effect of Tiotropium:
Aclidinium, Ipratropium, Other Anticholinergic Agents,
Pramlintide, Umeclidinium
Enhances anticholinergic effect of Cimetropium

Increases risk of adverse or toxic effects of the following
drugs:
Eluxadoline (constipation), Glucagon (GI adverse effects), Loxapine, Potassium Chloride (ulcerogenic effect)

Reduces therapeutic effect of the following drugs:
Levosulpiride, Secretin

Mechanism of Action

A competitively acetylcholine inhibitor that reversibly inhibits its action at type 3 muscarinic (M3) receptors in
bronchial smooth muscle causing bronchodilation.
